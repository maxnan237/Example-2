# PGY Investment Memo

**Pagaya Technologies (NASDAQ: PGY) | April 10, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

Pagaya is an AI-powered lending technology platform that evaluates loan applications banks would otherwise decline. Its AI sits inside 30+ lender Partners' workflows via API, approves additional borrowers, and routes the loans into securitization vehicles funded by institutional investors. Pagaya earns fees from both sides (lenders and investors) and retains risk positions in its ABS structures. Founded in Israel in 2016, IPO'd via SPAC in 2022, 100% of revenue from the U.S.

---

## What's Going Right

**First GAAP profitable year.** FY2025 net income of $81M, up from ($401M) in FY2024 — a $483M swing. Adjusted EBITDA hit $371M (+77% YoY). FCF reached $225M from ($42M) two years ago. The business model works at scale.

**Take rate expanding.** FRLPC% (the unit economics metric) grew from 3.2% to 4.9% in two years. Revenue grew 26% on only 9% Network Volume growth — Pagaya is extracting more value per dollar of loans processed.

**100% partner retention.** No Partner has ever left since inception. 30+ U.S. lenders integrated. Customer concentration improved: no single customer >10% in FY2025 (vs. 13% in FY2024).

**Asset-light model.** Loans flow to ABS trusts — Pagaya doesn't carry full credit risk on its balance sheet. Operating leverage is dramatic: opex (ex-production) dropped from 73% to 22% of revenue in three years.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**AI untested through a credit cycle.** The company's own filing: "Our AI technology has not yet been extensively tested during different economic conditions, including down-cycles." FY2024's ($401M) net loss showed what happens when investment losses spike — and that was without a recession.

**$504M in retained credit exposure.** Risk retention positions in ABS trusts carry mark-to-market and credit risk. Rising delinquencies would cause real losses.

**$824M in high-cost debt.** Senior notes at 8.875%. Cash interest of $92M/year exceeds GAAP net income ($81M). Below-investment-grade credit profile limits flexibility.

**Network Volume decelerating.** Growth slowed from +17% (FY2024) to +9% (FY2025), with Q4 at just +3% YoY (including SFR wind-down). The Q1 2026 guidance miss ($315-335M vs. $344M consensus) caused a 24% single-day stock drop.

**"True lender" regulatory risk.** If courts rule Pagaya is the real lender (not its bank Partners), loans may violate state usury laws — a potentially existential outcome.

**118% dilution since IPO.** Share count grew from 38M to 83M, with 13M more potentially dilutive securities outstanding.

## Decision

**Current stance: Speculative interest.**

At ~4x forward earnings, 0.49x EV/Sales, and 8/8 analyst Buy ratings, Pagaya is either deeply undervalued or correctly priced for credit cycle risk. The business model has proven it works at scale (first profitability, $225M FCF), but:

- The AI is **untested through a credit downturn** — FY2024's ($401M) loss was a warning shot
- **Network Volume is decelerating** (+3% in Q4) and Q1 2026 guidance missed consensus
- **$824M in 8.875% debt** creates a high fixed-cost burden
- **$504M in retained credit exposure** is the balance sheet risk that could reignite losses

**This is a credit cycle bet.** If you believe consumer credit stays healthy through 2027, the stock is dramatically mispriced. If you think a recession is coming, the downside is real but more limited (~60%) given the already-compressed valuation.

**Key triggers:**
- Q1 2026 results (May 2026) — at high end of guide ($335M) = bullish
- Consumer delinquency data — stable = bullish; spiking = bearish
- ABS market conditions — if spreads widen, Pagaya's funding cost rises
- New Partner announcements — evidence of continued growth
