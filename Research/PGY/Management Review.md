## 2. Capital Allocation

### Profitability trajectory

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Net Income (Loss) | $81M | ($401M) | ($128M) | ($302M) |
| Operating CF | $239M | $48M | ($22M) | ($40M) |
| FCF | $225M | $30M | ($42M) | ($62M) |

The transition from cash-burning startup to $225M in FCF in two years is impressive. Management has demonstrated ability to scale revenue while dramatically cutting opex (G&A down 34% in FY2025).

### Debt management

Management refinanced expensive term loan debt (SOFR +7.50%) with 2030 Senior Notes (8.875%) and a new revolving facility — still expensive but more structural. The $6.9M opportunistic debt repurchase at 87.4% of par in Dec 2025 shows capital allocation awareness.

### No dividends or buybacks — appropriate at this stage

All cash is reinvested or used for debt service. At $824M in debt and the company's growth stage, this is the correct priority.

## 4. Guidance Credibility

### The Q4 FY2025 beat / Q1 FY2026 miss

Q4 FY2025 results were record-setting: $335M revenue, $98M Adjusted EBITDA, $34M GAAP net income. But management guided Q1 FY2026 at $315-335M — below the $344M consensus.

Management's explanation: deliberate pullback from higher-risk credit segments as a risk management choice, not driven by credit stress.

[Motley Fool Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript]:
> Management indicated it was pulling back from higher-risk credit segments as a deliberate risk management choice.

*(Note: earnings call data from external transcripts — not from uploaded filings)*

**Judgment:** The explanation is plausible — proactive risk management is better than reactive. But the market didn't buy it (stock fell 24%). The credibility test will be whether Q1 2026 actuals come in at the high end of the guided range and whether credit quality validates the conservative stance.

### FY2026 guidance range

$1.4B — $1.575B (8-21% growth). The wide range itself suggests uncertainty. At the high end, it would confirm the growth story. At the low end, it would validate the bears.

## 5. Communication Quality

The FY2025 10-K is notably candid about risks:

> "Our AI technology has not yet been extensively tested during different economic conditions, including down-cycles."

> "Currently, a limited number of Partners account for a substantial portion of our Network Volume."

Management provides useful non-GAAP metrics (FRLPC, FRLPC%, Adjusted EBITDA) alongside GAAP — allowing investors to track unit economics.

**Weakness:** Partner names are never disclosed, making it impossible to independently assess concentration or relationship quality.

---

## 6. Overall Assessment: **Good (improving)**

The management team has executed a remarkable turnaround: from ($302M) GAAP loss and ($62M) FCF in FY2022 to $81M GAAP profit and $225M FCF in FY2025 — while growing revenue 74%. The co-founder team provides alignment and continuity.

**Concerns:** The Q1 2026 guidance miss damaged near-term credibility. The wide FY2026 guidance range (8-21%) signals management uncertainty about demand. And the absence of insider buying at $12 (despite Krubiner buying at higher prices in 2024) is a mild negative signal.
