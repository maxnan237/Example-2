## 3. Industry Structure

The AI lending intermediary market is **early-stage and fragmented**. Legacy credit decisioning (FICO-based) still dominates >90% of lending decisions. The TAM for AI-driven credit enhancement is large, but adoption is gradual because:

1. **Regulatory caution** — banks are conservative about delegating credit decisions to third-party AI
2. **Model explainability** — regulators require lenders to explain adverse actions, which is harder with ML models
3. **Credit cycle uncertainty** — no AI lending model has operated through a full recession
4. **Integration inertia** — bank IT systems are slow to adopt new vendors

---

## 4. Cyclicality

Consumer lending is inherently cyclical. Key risk factors:
- **Unemployment** drives delinquency rates, which determine loss severity on retained positions
- **Interest rates** affect ABS investor appetite and consumer borrowing demand
- **Credit availability** cycles between loose (high volume, higher risk) and tight (lower volume, lower risk)

The industry experienced a mini-cycle in 2022-2023: rising rates tightened ABS markets, reduced volume, and caused mark-to-market losses on retained positions. Pagaya's FY2024 ($401M) net loss was partly a consequence.

## 5. Regulatory Environment

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:

| Regulation | Risk to Pagaya |
|---|---|
| **ECOA / Reg B** | AI models could produce disparate impact on protected classes |
| **CFPB oversight** | Broad authority over consumer financial products; uncertain future |
| **"True lender" doctrine** | If Pagaya deemed the lender (not the bank Partner), loans may violate state usury laws |
| **Investment Company Act** | If deemed an investment company, burdensome compliance requirements |
| **State-level regulation** | Varying state laws on lending, data privacy, interest rate caps |

> "There continues to be uncertainty about the future of the CFPB and as to how its strategies and priorities [...] will impact our business."

---

## Key Takeaway

Pagaya operates in a large, growing market (AI lending) with a differentiated position (asset-light model serving large banks) but faces **structural cyclicality risk** (consumer credit) and **regulatory uncertainty** (AI fairness, "true lender"). The competitive environment is intensifying as Upstart scales and large banks consider building internal AI. The market rewards platforms with the best data and proven performance through cycles — and Pagaya has not yet faced that test.
