# PGY — Q4 FY2025 Earnings Analysis

*Quarter ended December 31, 2025 | Reported February 9, 2026*
*Sources: Pagaya IR press release, Motley Fool earnings call transcript, Seeking Alpha*
*Note: Quarterly data from external press releases — not from uploaded 10-K*

---

## Section 1: Guidance vs. Reality

| Metric | Guided | Actual | Delta | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$320-340M (est.) | **$335M** | In-line to slight beat | **Met** |
| Adjusted EBITDA | ~$85-95M (est.) | **$98M** | Beat | **Beat** |
| Network Volume | ~$2.5-2.7B (est.) | **$2.7B** | In-line | **Met** |

EPS of $0.36 (GAAP diluted) beat consensus of ~$0.69 adjusted — but bases differ across sources.

**The real story was Q1 2026 forward guidance**: $315-335M revenue vs. $344M consensus → stock fell 24%.

Management explanation: deliberate pullback from higher-risk credit segments.

> "Pagaya outlines $1.4B-$1.575B 2026 revenue guidance as it shifts focus to risk management"

**Judgment:** Beat on Q4 actuals, miss on forward guide. The risk management framing is credible but unverifiable until credit data confirms.

## Section 2: KPI Trends (Year-over-Year)

| KPI | Q4 FY2024 | Q4 FY2025 | Change | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total Revenue | ~$279M | $335M | +20% | Healthy growth |
| Network Volume | $2.6B | $2.7B | +3% | Decelerating (but +34% ex-SFR) |
| FRLPC | ~$117M | $131M | +12% | Take rate expanding |
| GAAP Net Income | ($262M) | $34M | +$296M | Massive swing to profitability |
| Adjusted EBITDA | ~$64M | $98M | +53% | Strong operating leverage |

**Narrative check:** Growth headline (+20% revenue) is solid, but Network Volume growth of +3% is concerning. Revenue is growing primarily through take rate expansion (FRLPC%), not volume. This **partially contradicts** management's growth narrative — volume is flat, not growing.

## Section 3: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved
1. **GAAP profitability achieved** — Q4 was the 4th consecutive profitable GAAP quarter
2. **Adjusted EBITDA margin** expanded to ~29% (from ~23% YoY)
3. **No massive investment losses** — unlike the FY2024 quarters

### Materially deteriorated
1. **Network Volume growth collapsed** to +3% YoY (including SFR wind-down)
2. **Forward guidance below consensus** — first significant miss, causing 24% stock drop
3. **SFR wind-down** removing a revenue stream entirely

### What is new
1. **Risk management pivot** — management actively pulling back from higher-risk segments
2. **$350M revolving ABS** with 26North — new funding structure
3. **$720M forward flow** with Sound Point Capital — expanded POS funding
