## 3. FY2026 Guidance

Management guided FY2026 revenue of **$1.4B — $1.575B** (8-21% growth). Q1 2026 was guided at $315-335M, below the $344M consensus — this triggered the 24% single-day drop.

At the midpoint ($1.49B) and assuming continued margin improvement:
- If Adjusted EBITDA margin holds at ~28%: Adjusted EBITDA ~$417M
- If GAAP net income margins expand to ~10%: GAAP NI ~$149M → EPS ~$1.80
- At forward P/E of 4.1x: the market is pricing in near-zero growth beyond FY2026

## 5. Key Valuation Consideration

The critical question is whether the market is correctly pricing credit cycle risk or whether the 73% drawdown has created a genuine mispricing.

**Arguments for mispricing (stock too cheap):**
- Forward P/E of 4x on a company growing 26% with first-ever GAAP profitability
- 8/8 analysts at Buy with median target 3x current price
- FRLPC% expanding (better unit economics)
- FCF inflection ($225M in FY2025 from negative in FY2023)

**Arguments for correct pricing (stock fairly valued or expensive):**
- AI not tested through credit cycle — FY2024 showed ($401M) loss when things went wrong
- Q1 2026 guidance miss suggests growth is decelerating
- $824M in debt at 8.875% — expensive and constraining
- 118% dilution since IPO; 13M more potentially dilutive securities
- Network Volume growth decelerated to +9% (from +17%)
- "True lender" regulatory risk is existential if realized
