# Pagaya Technologies (PGY) — Business Overview

*Analysis date: 2026-04-10 | Primary source: FY2025 10-K (period ending Dec 31, 2025)*

---

## 1. What the Company Does

Pagaya is an AI-powered lending technology platform that sits between banks/fintech lenders ("Partners") and institutional investors. Its AI evaluates loan applications that Partners would otherwise decline, enabling more approvals without the lender taking additional credit risk — the loans flow into securitization vehicles funded by institutional investors.

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We are a product-focused technology company that deploys sophisticated data science and proprietary, AI-powered technology to enable better outcomes for financial institutions, their existing and potential customers, and institutional or sophisticated investors."

> "Since our inception, our network has processed more than $3.6 trillion in loan applications across our consumer credit asset classes."

Founded in Israel in 2016, went public via SPAC in June 2022. Headquarters in New York, NY with primary R&D in Tel Aviv.

### Key Products

| Product | Asset Class | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Decline Monetization** | All | Flagship — rejected applications get AI "second look"; enables Partners to approve customers they'd otherwise decline |
| **Dual Look** | Personal, Auto | Concurrent evaluation alongside Partner's own models in real-time |
| **First Look** | Auto | Applications routed to Pagaya's AI before Partner reviews |
| **FastPass** | Personal, Auto | Removes verification friction for qualified borrowers |
| **Direct Marketing Engine** | Personal | AI-driven customer acquisition through direct mail/email |
| **Affiliate Optimizer** | Personal | Optimizes third-party affiliate channel originations |
| **Darwin** | SFR | Single-family rental property selection and management (being wound down) |

## 2. Revenue Model

Pagaya earns fees from both sides of its network:

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:

**From Partners (AI Integration Fees):** Earned for creating and delivering assets that comprise Network Volume. This is the largest revenue stream ($1.13B in FY2025).

**From Investors (Capital Markets Execution Fees):** Earned from ABS transaction pricing and forward flow execution.

**Contract Fees:** Administration, management, performance, and servicing fees from Financing Vehicles ($130M in FY2025).

**Interest & Investment Income:** From risk retention holdings and cash balances ($48M interest, -$8M investment loss in FY2025).

| Revenue Component | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Network AI fees | $1,131M | $916M | $696M |
| Contract fees | $130M | $88M | $77M |
| **Revenue from fees** | **$1,261M** | **$1,005M** | **$773M** |
| Interest income | $48M | $32M | $39M |
| Investment (loss)/income | ($8M) | ($5M) | $0.5M |
| **Total Revenue & Other Income** | **$1,301M** | **$1,032M** | **$812M** |

**Production Costs** ($749M in FY2025) compensate Partners for originating assets — effectively the "cost of goods."

**Key metric — FRLPC** (Fee Revenue Less Production Costs):

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| FRLPC | $512M | $407M | $264M |
| FRLPC % (of Network Volume) | **4.9%** | **4.2%** | **3.2%** |

The take rate has expanded from 3.2% to 4.9% in two years — a strong signal of improving unit economics.

## 3. Customer Base

### Partners (fee-generating side)

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "A scalable product with significant growth potential and success to date, with expansion to 30+ U.S. lenders"
> "Strong Partner relationships with 100% historical retention since inception"

Partners are banks and fintech lenders across personal loans, auto loans, and point-of-sale. No specific Partners are named in the filing.

**Concentration improving:**

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, Concentration Note]:
> "For the year ended December 31, 2025, no single customer accounted for 10% or more of the Company's total revenue."
> "During the year ended December 31, 2024 [...] one customer individually represented greater than 10% of total revenue and collectively totaled approximately 13%."

**Related-party revenue (Financing Vehicles):**
- FY2025: $653M (51.7% of fee revenue) — down from 80.5% in FY2023
- This represents fees earned from Pagaya-sponsored vehicles, not third-party concentration

### Investors (capital side)

> "Investors in our Financing Vehicles range from large institutional investors, such as pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, and asset management firms, to high-net-worth individuals and family offices."

## 6. Key Dependencies

### Capital markets access

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "Our ability to raise capital from asset investors is vital to our ability to offer products to Partners. If we are unable to raise capital from asset investors at competitive rates, or at all, it would materially reduce our revenue and cash flow."

The business requires continuous ABS issuance and forward flow agreements. Recent deals: $350M revolving ABS with 26North Partners (Jan 2026), $720M forward flow with Sound Point Capital.

### AI technology

> "We are heavily dependent on our AI technology. If we are unable to continue to improve our AI technology or if our AI technology does not operate as we expect [...] we could improperly evaluate products."

> "Our AI technology has not yet been extensively tested during different economic conditions, including down-cycles."

### Partner relationships

> "Currently, a limited number of Partners account for a substantial portion of our Network Volume [...] If these Partners were to cease or limit operations with us, our business [...] could be adversely affected."

### Regulatory environment

Key regulations: ECOA (fair lending), FCRA (credit reporting), CFPB oversight, "true lender" doctrine risk, Investment Company Act considerations.

> "Certain of the Partners' arrangements have been criticized in government and media reports as 'rent-a-charter' or 'rent-a-bank.'"
