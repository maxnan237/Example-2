## 2. Profitability Trajectory — The Inflection

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Operating Income (Loss) | **$264M** | $67M | ($24M) | ($252M) |
| Operating Margin | **20.3%** | 6.5% | (3.0%) | (33.6%) |
| Net Income (Loss) to PGY | **$81M** | ($401M) | ($128M) | ($302M) |
| Adjusted EBITDA | **$371M** | $210M | $82M | ($5M) |
| Adjusted Net Income | **$275M** | $67M | $17M | ($33M) |

FY2025 was the **first GAAP profitable year** — a $483M swing from ($401M) to $81M. The improvement was driven by:
- Revenue growth (+26%)
- Operating leverage: opex ex-production grew just 0.5% ($368M → $288M in tech/sales/G&A)
- G&A declined from $241M to $160M (-34%) through cost discipline

**The gap between operating income ($264M) and net income ($81M) reflects $107M in investment losses and $25M in debt extinguishment charges** — non-operating items that distort the bottom line.

### Investments in loans & securities — the key balance sheet item

$945M in loans and securities (61% of total assets) represents risk retention positions in Pagaya-sponsored ABS transactions. Of this, $504M is held for regulatory risk retention requirements.

This is **not traditional lending exposure** — Pagaya doesn't originate loans — but it does carry mark-to-market and credit risk on these retained positions. The FY2024 net loss of ($401M) was largely driven by ($404M) in investment losses on these securities.

## Accounting Flags

### 1. Investment losses dominate the P&L below operating income

FY2025: ($107M) in investment losses + ($25M) debt extinguishment = ($132M) in below-the-line charges. These turned a $264M operating profit into an $81M net profit. These losses are real (mark-to-market on retained ABS positions) and could recur in credit downturns.

### 2. Related-party revenue is majority of fees

$653M of $1,261M in fee revenue (51.7%) comes from related-party Financing Vehicles — entities Pagaya sponsors and manages. While this is the nature of the business model, it means over half of revenue comes from entities Pagaya itself controls.

### 3. Risk retention creates balance sheet exposure

$504M in risk retention holdings exposes Pagaya to credit losses on the loans its AI approved. If consumer credit deteriorates, these positions would take losses that flow directly to net income — as happened in FY2024 ($404M in losses).

### 4. High-cost debt

8.875% on $493M in senior notes is expensive. Annual interest of ~$44M on this tranche alone. Total cash interest paid was $92M in FY2025 — significant relative to the $81M GAAP net income.
