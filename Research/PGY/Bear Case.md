# Pagaya Technologies (PGY) — Bear Case

*Analysis date: 2026-04-10 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, external data*

---

## 1. Thesis Killer: Credit Cycle Exposure on an Untested AI

[PGY_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "Our AI technology has not yet been extensively tested during different economic conditions, including down-cycles."

Pagaya's entire business depends on its AI correctly pricing credit risk on consumer loans. In a recession:
- Consumer delinquencies rise → losses on $504M in risk retention holdings
- Partners tighten their own credit standards → fewer applications flow to Pagaya
- ABS investors demand higher spreads → funding costs rise, volumes drop
- The AI may systematically misprice risk in conditions it hasn't seen

**FY2024 preview:** Even without a recession, investment losses hit ($404M) in FY2024, driving a ($401M) net loss. A true credit cycle could be far worse.

---

## 2. Structural Weaknesses

### 2a. Capital markets dependency

The business model requires continuous access to ABS markets. If securitization markets seize (as in 2008, partially in 2022), Pagaya cannot fund loans and revenue stops.

> "Our ability to raise capital from asset investors is vital to our ability to offer products to Partners. If we are unable to raise capital from asset investors at competitive rates, or at all, it would materially reduce our revenue and cash flow."

### 2b. Partner concentration risk

> "Currently, a limited number of Partners account for a substantial portion of our Network Volume."

While no single customer exceeded 10% in FY2025, "a limited number" generating "a substantial portion" means losing 2-3 Partners could materially impair revenue.

### 2c. Related-party revenue dominance

$653M (51.7%) of fee revenue comes from Pagaya-sponsored Financing Vehicles — entities the company itself controls. This blurs the line between arm's-length revenue and self-dealing.

### 2d. High-cost debt at 8.875%

$493M in senior notes at 8.875% reflects below-investment-grade credit. $92M in annual cash interest consumes more cash than GAAP net income ($81M). A refinancing at maturity (Aug 2030) could be at even higher rates if credit markets tighten.

### 2e. "True lender" regulatory risk

> "Certain of the Partners' arrangements have been criticized in government and media reports as 'rent-a-charter' or 'rent-a-bank.'"

If courts rule that Pagaya (not its bank Partners) is the "true lender," the loans could be unenforceable or subject to state usury laws — a potentially existential regulatory outcome.

## 3. Assumptions the Bull Case Takes for Granted

1. **Consumer credit stays benign.** The bull case assumes delinquencies remain manageable. But unemployment is cyclical, and Pagaya's AI approves borrowers that traditional models decline — by definition, a riskier pool.

2. **ABS markets stay open.** The bull case assumes continuous investor appetite. But ABS spreads can widen dramatically in risk-off environments.

3. **FRLPC% keeps expanding.** The take rate rose from 3.2% to 4.9%. The bull case extrapolates this. But Partners may resist further fee increases, and competition from Upstart (which returned to profitability at scale) constrains pricing power.

4. **Network Volume grows.** FY2025 NV grew just 9% (and only 3% in Q4 including SFR wind-down). The deceleration from 17% (FY2024) is concerning.

5. **Dilution slows.** Shares outstanding grew 118% since IPO. The bull case assumes this stabilizes, but 13.1M additional dilutive securities remain outstanding.

---

## 4. Permanent Impairment Scenarios

### Credit recession
A rise in unemployment to 6%+ would likely cause: (a) $200-400M+ in investment losses on retained securities, (b) Partner volume pullbacks, (c) ABS market widening or closure. Pagaya could swing back to large net losses and face liquidity pressure from debt service on $824M in borrowings.

### Regulatory action
A CFPB enforcement action on AI lending fairness, or a successful "true lender" challenge, could force Pagaya to restructure its entire business model. This is a low-probability, high-severity risk.

### Partner defection
If 2-3 of the largest Partners leave (e.g., a large bank builds internal AI underwriting), revenue could decline 20-30% with limited ability to replace the volume quickly.

## Summary

The bear case is straightforward: **Pagaya's AI underwrites consumer credit that banks themselves decline, retains $504M in risk on those loans, funds operations with 8.875% debt, and has never operated through a credit downturn.** The FY2024 loss of ($401M) demonstrated what happens when investment losses spike — and that was without a recession. The Q1 2026 guidance miss (causing a 24% single-day stock drop) suggests the market is already skeptical about growth sustainability. At ~$12, the stock may be cheap — or it may be pricing in a credit cycle that hasn't arrived yet.
