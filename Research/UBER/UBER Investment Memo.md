# UBER Investment Memo

**Uber Technologies (NYSE: UBER) | April 11, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

Uber is a global technology platform connecting 202M monthly active consumers with ridesharing, food/grocery delivery, and freight logistics across 70+ countries. Three segments: Mobility (57% of revenue ex-Freight, 26% EBITDA margin), Delivery (31%, 18% margin), and Freight (12%, breakeven). The platform processed $194B in Gross Bookings in FY2025, generating $9.8B in free cash flow. CEO Dara Khosrowshahi has executed one of the great corporate turnarounds since taking over in 2017.

---

## What's Going Right

**Cash flow machine.** $9.8B FCF in FY2025 (+42% YoY) on $52B revenue. Adj. EBITDA of $8.7B (+35%). EBITDA margin on Gross Bookings expanded from 1.5% (FY2022) to 4.5% (FY2025). Uber went from burning cash to generating nearly $10B/year in three years.

**Network effects are compounding.** 202M MAPCs, 11.3B+ annual trips. Cross-platform users (rides + delivery) are 2.2x more engaged (11.4 trips/month vs. 5.2). Each product feeds the other.

**Uber One is building switching costs.** 36M+ members spending 3x more with +15% higher retention. Members drive 32% of Mobility+Delivery Gross Bookings. This is converting a low-switching-cost marketplace into a membership relationship.

**Advertising is a high-margin compounder.** $1.6B run rate growing 60%+. Near-zero incremental cost — pure leverage on existing platform traffic.

**$27B buyback commitment.** 19% of market cap authorized for repurchases. CFO publicly called the stock "undervalued." At ~$72, this is highly accretive.

**AV positioning is capital-smart.** Partnerships with Waymo, Nuro, WeRide, Rivian, Nvidia — aggregating AV fleets rather than building self-driving tech. Capital-light approach that preserves FCF while maintaining platform relevance.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Autonomous vehicles could bypass the platform.** Waymo operates its own consumer app in SF/LA/Phoenix — no Uber needed. If AV operators go direct to consumers, Uber's 27% take rate and demand network lose relevance. This is a 5-10 year risk but real.

**Driver reclassification is existential.** 150,000+ Drivers have filed arbitration demands. If reclassified as employees, benefits/taxes/minimum wage would fundamentally change the cost structure. Uber explicitly flags this as its #1 risk.

**U.S. Delivery is #2.** DoorDash holds ~60-67% of U.S. food delivery vs. Uber's ~23%. In the most important delivery market, Uber is a distant second.

**Low switching costs — self-acknowledged.** Uber's own filing: "low barriers to entry, low switching costs." Riders and Drivers multi-app routinely.

**Freight is a drag.** Revenue declined 26% since FY2022, negative EBITDA. Doesn't leverage the consumer network.

**Insurance reserves doubling.** $9.8B (FY2024) vs. $4.7B (FY2022). Auto liability claims are a growing, hard-to-predict cost.

## Decision

**Current stance: Constructive.**

Uber at ~$72 is a high-quality business trading at a value-stock multiple. The company generates nearly $10B in annual FCF, is growing EBITDA 35%, buying back 19% of market cap, and building emerging high-margin businesses (advertising, membership) — all while trading at 15x earnings, a fraction of what the market pays for Lyft or DoorDash.

The structural risks are real:
- **AV disruption** could disintermediate the platform (5-10 year horizon)
- **Driver reclassification** could transform the cost structure
- **DoorDash** is winning U.S. delivery

But at this price, significant pessimism is already embedded. The base case offers +20% upside; the bull case offers +95%. Time is on the investor's side — every quarter without AV disruption or regulatory change, Uber generates another $2.5B in FCF and buys back more stock.

**Key triggers:**
- Q1 2026 results (May 2026) — reacceleration vs. guidance softness
- Waymo direct expansion pace — disintermediation signal
- Driver classification legislation — any major market shift
- Uber One growth past 40M — switching cost narrative strengthening
