### GAAP net income is unreliable — use Adjusted EBITDA

FY2024 net income of $9.9B includes a $5.8B deferred tax asset release and $1.8B in unrealized equity gains (Aurora +$629M, Grab +$723M, Didi +$357M). FY2022's ($9.1B) loss included ($7.0B) in unrealized equity losses. These are non-operating, non-cash swings.

**Adjusted EBITDA is the real operating profitability metric:**

| | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adjusted EBITDA** | **$6,484M** | **$4,052M** | **$1,713M** |
| Adj. EBITDA margin (on revenue) | 14.7% | 10.9% | 5.4% |
| Adj. EBITDA margin (on Gross Bookings) | 4.0% | 2.9% | 1.5% |
| YoY growth | +60% | +137% | — |

EBITDA margins have tripled in two years (5.4% → 14.7%). On Gross Bookings, the platform earns 4 cents on every dollar transacted — up from 1.5 cents.

## Accounting Flags

### 1. Equity investment volatility dominates GAAP P&L
$1.8B in unrealized gains (FY2024) and ($7.0B) in losses (FY2022) from Didi/Aurora/Grab create massive P&L swings. Always use Adjusted EBITDA, not GAAP net income.

### 2. Deferred tax asset of $6.2B
The FY2024 release of the valuation allowance contributed $5.8B to net income — non-cash, non-recurring. But the DTA itself is a real asset: Uber can shield future earnings from U.S. taxes.

### 3. Insurance reserves growing rapidly
$9.8B in insurance reserves (FY2024, up from $4.7B in FY2022) for auto liability claims. These are estimated liabilities that could require upward revision.

### 4. Driver classification is an off-balance-sheet contingency
150,000+ arbitration demands from Drivers. If reclassified as employees, the cost structure would fundamentally change (benefits, payroll taxes, minimum wage guarantees). Not quantified on the balance sheet.
