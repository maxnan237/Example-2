# Uber Technologies (UBER) — Industry Scan

*Analysis date: 2026-04-11 | Sources: FY2024 10-K, external industry data*

---

## 1. Market Size

**Rideshare market:** ~$144-156B (2025), projected $660-790B by 2034-2035 (~18-20% CAGR).
**Food delivery market:** DoorDash dominates U.S. at ~60-67% share; Uber Eats ~23%; Grubhub ~16%.

*(Note: market size from Mordor Intelligence, Fortune Business Insights, Statista — not from uploaded filings)*

---

## 2. Competitive Landscape

[UBER_10K_FY2024.htm, Competition]:

**Mobility:** Bolt, Didi, Grab, Lyft, Ola. Also: personal vehicles (majority of passenger miles), taxis, public transit.

**Delivery:** DoorDash, Deliveroo, Glovo, Instacart, Gopuff, Rappi, iFood, Delivery Hero, Just Eat Takeaway, Amazon.

**Freight:** C.H. Robinson, Total Quality Logistics, RXO, XPO, Echo Global, DHL, NEXT Trucking.

**AV:** Waymo, Tesla, Zoox (Amazon), Motional, Avride, Nuro.

### Key competitive dynamic

Uber's advantage is **cross-platform scale** — the only company operating ridesharing + delivery + freight + advertising + membership at global scale. No competitor matches this breadth:

| Competitor | Rides | Delivery | Freight | Ads | Membership |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Uber** | Yes (global) | Yes (global) | Yes (US/CAN) | Yes ($1.6B) | Yes (36M+) |
| Lyft | Yes (US only) | No | No | Limited | Limited |
| DoorDash | No | Yes (US-dominant) | No | Yes (growing) | DashPass |
| Grab | Yes (SE Asia) | Yes (SE Asia) | No | Limited | GrabUnlimited |

## 3. Autonomous Vehicle Landscape

The AV transition is the single biggest structural uncertainty for the rideshare industry.

**Uber's position:** Platform aggregator — partnering with Waymo (Austin, Atlanta), Nuro, WeRide (Dubai), plus Rivian ($1.25B for 50K robotaxis), Nvidia (LA/SF in 2027). CEO expects robotaxi service in 10+ countries by late 2026.

**Risk:** Waymo is expanding its own consumer app independently (SF, LA, Phoenix, Dallas, London), suggesting it may not need Uber's demand network long-term.

---

## 4. Regulatory Environment

[UBER_10K_FY2024.htm, Government Regulation]:

**Driver classification** is the dominant regulatory risk. Most U.S. states and many international markets regulate Uber under TNC frameworks that permit IC classification, but this is under constant legal challenge.

**Banned/restricted markets:** Argentina, Germany, Italy, Japan, South Korea, Spain have laws banning or heavily restricting ridesharing.

**Data privacy:** GDPR (EU), CCPA (California) impose compliance costs.

---

## Key Takeaway

Uber operates in large, growing markets (rideshare 18-20% CAGR, delivery ~15% CAGR) with strong network effects but acknowledged low switching costs. The competitive set is fragmented globally but concentrated in the U.S. (Lyft in rides, DoorDash in delivery). The AV transition is the defining long-term strategic question — Uber is positioning as the platform layer, but this bet is unproven.
