## Key Consideration

Uber at $72 is being valued like a **mature, slow-growth company** (15x P/E, 6.8% FCF yield), yet it's growing revenue 18%, EBITDA 35%, and FCF 42%. The market appears to be pricing in some combination of:

1. AV disruption risk (5-10 year horizon)
2. Driver reclassification risk (political/regulatory)
3. The Q1 2026 soft guidance (near-term deceleration fear)

If these risks don't materialize near-term, the valuation has meaningful room to expand.
