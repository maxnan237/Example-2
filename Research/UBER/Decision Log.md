# Uber Technologies (UBER) — Decision Log

*Initiated: 2026-04-11*

---

## Investment Thesis Summary

Uber is a $52B-revenue global platform generating $9.8B in FCF, trading at ~15x earnings — a discount to every peer — while growing EBITDA 35% and FCF 42%. The market is pricing in AV disruption risk and regulatory uncertainty at a level that may significantly undervalue the business if those risks don't materialize near-term.

**Bull thesis:** Best-in-class marketplace with 202M MAPCs, cross-platform engagement (2.2x lift), Uber One (36M members), high-margin advertising ($1.6B, +60%), and $27B in buybacks — all at 15x P/E with 6.8% FCF yield. The AV platform strategy (aggregator, not developer) is capital-efficient.

**Bear thesis:** Self-acknowledged low switching costs, autonomous vehicles could disintermediate the platform, Driver reclassification is existential, U.S. Delivery is distant #2 to DoorDash, and Freight is a value destroyer.

## Decision Framework

**To buy at ~$72:**
- Base case offers +20% upside, bull case +95%
- FCF yield of 6.8% is attractive for a company growing 35%+ EBITDA
- $27B buyback at these prices is highly accretive
- AV platform positioning (Waymo, Nuro, Rivian, Nvidia) is credible
- The discount to peers (15x vs. Lyft 56x, DoorDash 99x) is unjustified by fundamentals

**To avoid:**
- Believe AV disruption arrives within 3-5 years (not 5-10)
- Believe Driver reclassification is imminent in the U.S.
- Believe DoorDash will eventually dominate delivery globally (not just U.S.)

**Current stance: Constructive.** The risk/reward at ~$72 is favorable. Uber is generating nearly $10B in FCF, growing 35%+ EBITDA, buying back 19% of market cap, and trading at a fraction of peer multiples. The structural risks (AV, regulation) are real but distant. This is a business where time is on the investor's side — the longer AV disruption takes, the more FCF Uber generates and returns to shareholders.
