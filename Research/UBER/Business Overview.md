# Uber Technologies (UBER) — Business Overview

*Analysis date: 2026-04-11 | Primary source: FY2024 10-K (period ending Dec 31, 2024)*

---

## 1. What the Company Does

Uber is a global technology platform connecting consumers with ride services, food/grocery delivery, and freight logistics across 70+ countries and 15,000+ cities.

[UBER_10K_FY2024.htm, Item 1]:
> "We are a technology platform that uses a massive network, leading technology, operational excellence, and product expertise to power movement from point A to point B."

Founded 2009. HQ: San Francisco. ~31,100 employees (18,000 outside the U.S.). Millions of independent Drivers and Couriers operate on the platform but are NOT employees.

---

## 2. Business Segments

### Mobility (57% of revenue, ex-Freight)

Ridesharing, carsharing, taxis, rentals, micromobility. The core business.

| | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $25,087M | $19,832M | $14,029M |
| Gross Bookings | $83,024M | $68,897M | $52,665M |
| Take rate | 30.2% | 28.8% | 26.6% |
| Segment Adj. EBITDA | $6,497M | $4,963M | $3,299M |

Take rate expanding from 26.6% to 30.2% — Uber is capturing more per ride.

### Delivery (31% of revenue, ex-Freight)

Uber Eats (restaurants), grocery, alcohol, convenience, retail. Also includes Uber Direct (white-label delivery-as-a-service) and advertising.

| | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $13,750M | $12,204M | $10,901M |
| Gross Bookings | $74,614M | $63,726M | $55,778M |
| Take rate | 18.4% | 19.2% | 19.5% |
| Segment Adj. EBITDA | $2,471M | $1,506M | $551M |

Delivery EBITDA grew 4.5x in two years ($551M → $2,471M). Take rate declining slightly — competitive pressure from DoorDash.

### Freight (12% of revenue)

Digital freight brokerage connecting shippers and carriers. Revenue = Gross Bookings (reported gross).

| | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $5,141M | $5,245M | $6,947M |
| Adj. EBITDA | ($74M) | ($64M) | $0M |

Freight is the weakest segment — revenue declining, still unprofitable. Challenged by soft freight cycle.

## 3. Revenue Model

[UBER_10K_FY2024.htm, Revenue Recognition]:
> "We generate substantially all of our revenue from fees paid by Drivers and Merchants for use of our platform."

Uber is an **agent** in most markets — revenue is net of Driver/Merchant earnings and incentives. In some markets (and all Freight), Uber is the **principal** and reports gross.

**Revenue streams:**
1. **Platform fees** from Drivers and Merchants (core — majority of revenue)
2. **Advertising** — sponsored listings, journey ads (growing 60%+, ~$1.6B run rate by Q4 FY2025)
3. **Uber One subscriptions** — $9.99/month, 30M members as of Dec 2024 (36M+ by mid-2025)
4. **Freight** — transportation management services (reported gross)

## 6. Key Dependencies

### Driver/Courier supply

> "If we are unable to attract or maintain a critical mass of Drivers, consumers, merchants, Shippers, and Carriers [...] our platform will become less appealing."

### Driver classification risk

> "Our business would be adversely affected if Drivers were classified as employees, workers or quasi-employees instead of independent contractors."

150,000+ Drivers have filed arbitration demands in the U.S. Massachusetts was resolved in June 2024 (drivers remain ICs).

### Autonomous vehicles — platform aggregator strategy

Uber partners with Waymo, Aurora, Nuro, WeRide, and others rather than developing its own AV technology. Waymo robotaxis operate on Uber's platform in Austin and Atlanta. Risk: AV partners may bypass Uber's platform as they scale.

### App store dependency

> "We rely on third parties maintaining open marketplaces to distribute our platform."

### Regulatory — banned in some markets

> "Certain jurisdictions, including Argentina, Germany, Italy, Japan, South Korea, and Spain [...] have adopted laws [...] banning certain ridesharing products."
