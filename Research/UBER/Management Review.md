# Uber Technologies (UBER) — Management Review

*Analysis date: 2026-04-11 | Sources: FY2024 10-K, external data*

---

## 1. CEO: Dara Khosrowshahi

Born 1969 in Iran; family fled during the 1979 revolution. Brown University (Economics). Career: Allen & Company → IAC/InterActiveCorp → Expedia CEO (2005-2017) → Uber CEO since August 2017.

Khosrowshahi forfeited ~$184M in unvested Expedia equity to take the Uber role — significant personal conviction. He replaced co-founder Travis Kalanick during a period of corporate governance crisis.

**FY2025 compensation:** ~$35.6M total (~97% equity). Base salary $1M.

*(Note: CEO background from Wikipedia, compensation from external proxy data — not from uploaded filings)*

### Track Record

Under Khosrowshahi (2017-present), Uber has:
- Turned operating income from ($4.1B) loss (FY2019) to $2.8B profit (FY2024)
- Grew FCF from near-zero to $6.9B (FY2024) / $9.8B (FY2025)
- Scaled to 171M MAPCs (FY2024) / 202M (FY2025)
- Launched Uber One (0 → 36M+ members)
- Built $1.6B advertising business from nothing
- Initiated $27B in buyback authorizations
- Positioned Uber as the AV platform aggregator (Waymo, Nuro, WeRide deals)

This is one of the most successful CEO turnarounds in technology history. The transformation from money-losing, scandal-plagued startup to $9.8B FCF cash machine was not preordained.

---

## 2. Capital Allocation

### Buyback commitment

$7B authorized Feb 2024; expanded to $27B by Aug 2025. $1.25B in FY2024, accelerating in FY2025. At current market cap (~$144B), total authorizations represent ~19% of the company — aggressive and shareholder-friendly.

CFO publicly stated the stock was "undervalued" when initiating the ASR.

### No dividends — appropriate

Uber is in growth/reinvestment phase. Buybacks are the right capital return mechanism given the accumulated deficit ($20.7B) and growth opportunities.

### Freight acquisition — questionable

The Transplace/Freight business ($6.9B revenue in FY2022 declining to $5.1B in FY2024, negative EBITDA) is the one capital allocation miss. It doesn't leverage the consumer network and has been a drag on overall margins.

---

## 3. Insider Activity

CEO Khosrowshahi holds ~1.2M shares directly. Recent activity is routine RSU vesting — no large discretionary sales or open-market purchases identified. The $27B buyback program is the strongest signal of management conviction.

---

## 4. Guidance Credibility

FY2024 results generally met or beat expectations throughout the year. The Q4 FY2025 report (Feb 2026) showed strong actuals but "soft" Q1 2026 guidance, causing a stock decline. This is the first meaningful guidance disappointment under Khosrowshahi.

---

## 5. Overall Assessment: **Excellent**

Khosrowshahi has executed one of the great corporate turnarounds — transforming Uber from a cash-burning, governance-challenged company into a profitable platform generating nearly $10B in annual FCF. The AV platform strategy (aggregating partners rather than building in-house) is capital-efficient and strategically sound. The $27B buyback commitment demonstrates confidence. The only blemish is the Freight segment, which appears to be a strategic misfit.
