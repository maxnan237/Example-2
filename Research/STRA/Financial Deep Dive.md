## Accounting Flags

### 1. Large goodwill (73% of assets) creates impairment risk

$1.24B in goodwill — primarily from 2018 Capella merger ($632M USHE) and ANZ acquisitions ($510M). ANZ segment fair value exceeded carrying value by ~17% in FY2024 — modest cushion. Any material ANZ deterioration could trigger write-down.

### 2. Flat dividend may overstate shareholder return focus

Dividend at $2.40 for 5+ years despite FCF growing from $80M to $154M. Payout ratio of just 16% of FCF could easily support a 50-100% dividend increase. The decision to prioritize buybacks over dividend growth is defensible but signals management's view that the stock is undervalued.

### 3. 90/10 compliance at Strayer is tight

89.64% federal revenue at Strayer is 36 bps from the 90% cap. Any increase in Title IV dependence (reduced employer partnerships, enrollment shift) could force corrective actions.

### 4. ANZ revenue declining in USD

ANZ revenue declined 2.2% in FY2025 partly due to AUD/NZD currency weakness. Forex is a continuous headwind. Underlying local-currency performance is better but creates USD reporting volatility.

### 5. Restructuring costs recurring

$22M in restructuring in FY2025 (after $2M in FY2024, $16M in FY2023). Ongoing facility consolidation and AI-driven restructuring. Not a one-time event.

### 6. Borrower defense exposure is material

~8,640 BDTR applications pending (6,770 Capella + 1,870 Strayer). Not quantified on balance sheet but represents potential material liability if DOE grants relief and seeks recoupment.
