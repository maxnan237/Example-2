# Strategic Education Inc (STRA) — Bear Case

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, external data*

---

## 1. Thesis Killer: Secular Decline in Post-Secondary Demand + Regulatory Overhang

The post-secondary for-profit education sector has been in secular decline for 15 years:
- Industry enrollment peaked in 2010 at ~13M students
- Has declined ~25% since despite population growth
- Borrower sentiment increasingly negative (student debt concerns)
- Public perception of for-profit education remains damaged from 2010s scandals
- **18-year-old population peaked in 2025**, 15-year decline projected

[STRA_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "Post-secondary education is highly competitive and online education is subject to rapid technological change. [...] Public colleges may offer programs similar to those of our universities without tuition or at a lower tuition level as a result of government subsidies (including various 'free college' programs)."

STRA's average annual enrollment declined in FY2025 (USHE -1.4%, ANZ -1.8%). If this continues, the business has limited growth runway and the ETS segment alone must compensate.

---

## 2. Structural Weaknesses

### 2a. 90/10 Rule compliance at Strayer is tight

Strayer derived **89.64%** of revenue from federal funds in FY2024 — just 36 bps from the 90% ceiling. Any enrollment mix shift toward more federal-funded students would force corrective action (tuition cuts, enrollment restrictions) or risk Title IV loss.

[STRA_10K_FY2025.htm]:
> "For fiscal year 2024, Strayer University derived approximately 89.64% of its cash-basis revenues from federal education assistance."

### 2b. Borrower Defense exposure is massive and unquantified

~**8,640 borrower defense applications** pending (6,770 Capella + 1,870 Strayer). If the Department of Education grants relief, STRA could face recoupment demands. The filing explicitly warns:

> "The Company is unable to predict whether the Department will grant BDTR relief [...] If the Department were to successfully seek recovery [...] any such recovery could have a material adverse effect on our business."

This is an off-balance-sheet contingent liability that could be hundreds of millions.

### 2c. Goodwill is 73% of assets — impairment risk

$1.24B in goodwill + $249M in intangibles = $1.49B on a $2.04B balance sheet. ANZ segment's fair value exceeded carrying value by only **17%** in the FY2024 impairment test — modest cushion. A recession or regulatory shock could trigger write-downs of $100M+.

### 2d. Dividend has been flat for 5+ years

$2.40/share annual dividend unchanged since 2021. Payout ratio of only 16% of FCF suggests plenty of room — but management's decision NOT to raise it for 5+ years signals caution about sustainability. Either the company lacks confidence in the future or is hoarding cash for regulatory contingencies.

### 2e. ANZ segment stagnant

ANZ revenue declined 2.2% in FY2025 (-1.8% enrollment + currency headwinds). Australia's Ministerial Direction 111 imposed international student caps in Dec 2024, limiting growth. ANZ is 20% of revenue but only contributing marginally to growth.

### 2f. AI disruption to the credential model

AI tutoring (Khan Academy, ChatGPT, Duolingo Max) offers personalized learning at near-zero marginal cost. AI-based skill credentials (Google, LinkedIn, Microsoft) bypass the degree model entirely. If employers increasingly accept alternative credentials, the four-year degree's value proposition erodes — STRA's core business.

---

## 3. Assumptions the Bull Case Takes for Granted

1. **Enrollment stabilizes.** USHE enrollment declined in FY2025. The bull case assumes this reverses via ETS growth. If it continues declining, the fixed-cost structure amplifies margin compression.

2. **Regulatory stability.** Gainful employment rule, 90/10 Rule, BDTR all remain at current strictness. If any tightens (e.g., Strayer forced to reduce Title IV reliance), business model breaks.

3. **AI enables margin expansion, not revenue disruption.** The bull case sees AI as a $70M+ cost-saving tool. The bear case sees AI as destroying the degree premium.

4. **ETS continues 40%+ revenue growth.** If Workforce Edge partner additions slow or Sophia subscriber growth stalls, the growth engine dies.

5. **Employer funding partnerships persist.** If employer tuition benefits face tax reform or labor market weakness reduces retention spend, the B2B channel weakens.

---

## 4. Permanent Impairment Scenarios

### Title IV loss at Strayer
If Strayer breaches 90/10 (even for a single year), it loses Title IV eligibility. ~90% of revenue disappears. Strayer represents ~50% of USHE revenue ($434M). This is an existential event for half the company.

### Massive BDTR settlement
If DOE rules on 8,000+ pending applications with significant recoupment, STRA could face $200-500M+ in retroactive liability. Balance sheet would absorb but growth optionality disappears.

### AI-driven credentialing replacement
If Google/Microsoft/LinkedIn certifications displace degrees for working adults (STRA's core demographic), the enrollment funnel dries up over 5-10 years. Not immediate but existential.

### Demographic cliff amplification
If traditional universities, facing 15-year enrollment decline, aggressively expand into online working-adult programs, STRA loses market share to better-capitalized nonprofits with brand advantages.

## Summary

STRA operates in a structurally declining industry (for-profit post-secondary education) with multiple regulatory overhangs (90/10, BDTR, gainful employment, OBBBA loan limits), faces secular demographic headwinds (15-year decline in traditional students starting 2026), and has an emerging AI disruption threat to the degree-based credential model. While management has executed well — growing ETS at 40% margins, expanding operating margins, buying back shares aggressively — the fundamental question is whether the traditional degree business can sustain itself long enough for ETS to become the dominant revenue stream. At ~12x forward earnings with 3% yield, the stock is cheap — but it's cheap for reasons that may not resolve favorably.
