## 2. Capital Allocation Track Record

### Disciplined acquisition history

- **2018 Capella merger** — transformative; created the STRA platform; largely successful integration
- **2020 ANZ acquisition** (Torrens, Think) — mixed results; ANZ segment now declining; goodwill cushion tight
- **No large acquisitions since 2020** — focus shifted to organic growth and buybacks

### Debt-free balance sheet

- Paid down $61M of term debt in FY2024
- Zero long-term debt as of Dec 2025
- $250M undrawn revolver
- $153M in cash + investments

This is a fortress balance sheet that provides resilience against regulatory shocks.

### Capital returns accelerating

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buybacks | $139M | $12M | $10M |
| Dividends | $58M | $59M | $59M |
| Total | $197M | $71M | $69M |

**FY2025 buybacks were 14x the prior year**, reflecting management conviction that the stock was undervalued. Average repurchase price of $81.30 is below the current ~$82. $213.5M authorization remaining through Dec 2026.

### Dividend: flat, not growing

$2.40/share annual dividend unchanged since 2021. Only 44% of EPS / 16% of FCF payout ratio. Either management is cautious about regulatory contingencies or prioritizing buybacks (which are more tax-efficient than dividends).

The flat dividend for 5+ years despite FCF growing from $80M to $154M is a modest negative signal for income-focused investors.

## 3. Operational Execution

### Margin expansion

Operating margin expanded from 6.6% (FY2022) to 13.7% (FY2025) — 710 bps in 3 years. Drivers:
- Mix shift to high-margin ETS
- AI-driven cost savings ($30M in FY2025)
- Facility consolidation (reduced U.S. footprint by 200K sq ft in 2025)
- Share count reduction

### ETS segment execution

Building Sophia Learning and Workforce Edge from essentially zero in 2018 to a $148M / 40% margin business in 2025 is impressive. The 41% revenue growth in FY2025 demonstrates continued acceleration.

### USHE execution mixed

USHE enrollment declined 1.4% in FY2025 (after growing 6%+ in each of FY2023-FY2024). Management attributes this to a competitive environment and intentional focus on higher-quality enrollment, but the trend is worth monitoring.

### ANZ execution poor

ANZ revenue has declined for two years (USD basis). Currency is a factor but local-currency growth has been weak. The $510M goodwill on this segment has only 17% cushion in impairment testing.

## 5. Guidance and Communication

**Notable:** STRA has **not provided specific FY2026 numerical guidance** in recent disclosures. Management has referenced the 2023 Investor Day "notional model" as a proxy. This lack of explicit forward guidance is unusual and potentially frustrating for investors.

Analyst Truist cited "more difficult path to consensus revenue in 2026" as the rationale for their downgrade — suggesting management's communication about 2026 has been cautious or opaque.

**Q4 FY2025 results** (reported Feb 26, 2026):
- Revenue $323M (in-line with consensus)
- Adj. EPS $1.75 (beat $1.47 consensus by 19%)
- Stock rallied +9.8% on Mar 3, 2026

Quarterly execution has been solid; annual communication has been less generous.

---

## 6. Overall Assessment: **Good**

McDonnell and Silberman have built STRA into a well-managed, debt-free company with expanding margins and a differentiated ETS growth engine. Execution on the operating model has been strong — $1.27B revenue at 13.7% op margin with AI-driven cost advantages is impressive in a challenging sector.

**Strengths:**
- 20-year CEO tenure with track record
- Debt-free balance sheet (rare in education)
- Aggressive buyback program at accretive prices
- Insider buying by Chairman
- Successful ETS scaling (Sophia, Workforce Edge)
- AI cost advantage execution

**Concerns:**
- Dividend flat for 5+ years (conservative signal)
- ANZ segment execution is weak
- Limited forward guidance frustrating analysts
- Thin analyst coverage (3 analysts) reflects market apathy

**Not found in available filings:** Specific CEO succession plan, given McDonnell's long tenure.

Overall, management is operationally strong and capital-discipline-focused, but lacks the external communication polish that drives premium valuations.
