## Section 4: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved vs. last year
- **ETS scaling sharply** — 41% revenue growth, 40% margin
- **Operating margin expanded** 90 bps through AI cost savings
- **USHE persistence improved** — 88.3% vs. 86.9%
- **Employer-affiliated enrollment** — 32.3% vs. 29.6%
- **Buybacks accelerated dramatically** — $139M vs. $12M
- **Debt fully paid off** (no debt vs. $61.3M)

### Materially deteriorated vs. last year
- **USHE enrollment declined** 1.4% (after +6.4% in FY2024)
- **ANZ segment** — revenue and enrollment both declining
- **Dividend remains flat** at $2.40
- **Restructuring costs elevated** ($22M vs. $2M)

### What is new
- **Media Design School** became subsidiary of Strayer (Sept 2025)
- **Signal Labs partnership** with LearnLaunch for ed-tech innovation
- **Workforce Edge expansion** — NUC University (Spanish-language) added

### What did NOT change despite management emphasis
- **FY2026 guidance** — no specific numerical targets (opacity frustrating analysts)
- **Dividend** — flat for 5+ years despite strong FCF growth
- **Segment mix** — USHE still 68%, ETS still only 12%

## Summary

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Execution vs. Expectations | **Strong** — EPS beat by 19%, revenue in-line |
| Management Credibility | **Mixed** — operational execution strong; forward communication opaque |
| Business Momentum | **Decelerating** — 4% revenue growth down from 7.7% prior year; USHE enrollment declining |

The Q4 FY2025 print was a **quality earnings beat** — operational efficiency driving margin expansion — but underlying revenue momentum is decelerating. The ETS segment (+41%) is offsetting USHE weakness (-1.4% enrollment) for now, but the transition dynamic is clear: STRA must continue executing on ETS scaling to offset traditional university enrollment pressure.

**Next earnings: Q1 2026, April 23, 2026 (pre-market).** Key watch items: USHE enrollment trend, ETS growth rate, any commentary on FY2026 outlook or regulatory developments.
