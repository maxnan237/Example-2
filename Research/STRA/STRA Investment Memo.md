# STRA Investment Memo

**Strategic Education Inc (NASDAQ: STRA) | April 12, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

STRA is a $1.27B-revenue post-secondary education company operating three segments: Strayer University + Capella University (US Higher Ed, 68% of revenue), Torrens University + Think Education + Media Design School (Australia/New Zealand, 20%), and Education Technology Services (Sophia Learning + Workforce Edge, 12%). The ETS segment is growing 41% at 40% operating margins — the growth engine. Debt-free balance sheet with $153M cash. HQ: Herndon, VA. 6,134 employees. Serves primarily working adults (avg age 33).

---

## What's Going Right

**ETS segment is exceptional.** Growing 41% YoY at 40% operating margins. Sophia Learning subscribers up 47% YoY. Workforce Edge serves 80 employer partners covering ~3.9M employees. This segment is becoming a real business — $148M revenue today, could exceed $250M by FY2027.

**Debt-free balance sheet.** $153M cash + $0 long-term debt + $250M undrawn revolver. In a sector historically dominated by leveraged operators, STRA's balance sheet is a meaningful quality differentiator.

**Aggressive capital returns.** $139M in buybacks during FY2025 (14x the prior year) at $81.30 average — near current prices. Combined with dividends, $197M returned to shareholders (11% yield on market cap).

**AI cost advantage.** $30M in AI-driven cost savings in FY2025, with management targeting $70M+ by 2027. This is flowing through to margin expansion (13.7% op margin, up 90 bps YoY).

**Insider buying.** Chairman Silberman bought $58K in open market at ~$81 in March 2026 (following $850K purchase in March 2025). Insider conviction at current prices.

**Persistence improving.** USHE student persistence up to 88.3% from 86.9% YoY. Better student outcomes = lower acquisition cost = better unit economics.

**Employer-affiliated enrollment growing.** 32.3% of USHE enrollment vs. 27.2% two years ago. This shifts revenue mix away from federal aid (reducing 90/10 risk) and toward more stable B2B contracts.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Post-secondary enrollment secular decline.** Industry has been shrinking for 15 years. USHE enrollment declined 1.4% in FY2025 after growing 6%+ in FY2024. 18-year-old population peaked in 2025; 15-year demographic cliff projected.

**Strayer 90/10 Rule is tight.** Strayer University derived 89.64% of revenue from federal aid in FY2024 — just 36 bps from the 90% ceiling. Any slip triggers Title IV loss for Strayer.

**Borrower Defense exposure.** ~8,640 applications pending (6,770 Capella + 1,870 Strayer). If DOE grants relief, STRA could face hundreds of millions in recoupment.

**ANZ segment declining.** Revenue -2.2% in FY2025 with only 17% goodwill cushion. Currency headwinds + international student caps in Australia.

**AI disruption to degrees.** Google Certificates, LinkedIn Learning, Microsoft certifications increasingly accepted as degree alternatives. If this trend accelerates, STRA's core business model weakens.

**Dividend flat for 5+ years.** $2.40 unchanged since 2021 despite FCF doubling. Either management is cautious or prioritizing buybacks — but the flat dividend is a negative signal for income investors.

**Thin analyst coverage.** Only 3 analysts. Limited institutional sponsorship. Truist recently downgraded to Hold citing "difficult path to consensus revenue in 2026."

## Decision

**Current stance: Constructive value play.**

At ~$82 with a forward P/E of ~12x, STRA is cheap for valid reasons (secular industry decline, regulatory overhangs) but also cheaper than a sum-of-the-parts analysis justifies. The key insight is:

**The market is pricing STRA as a single declining for-profit university.**

**But inside STRA is a hidden business:**
- **ETS segment** growing 41% at 40% margins
- **Workforce Edge** with 80 employer partners — a B2B enterprise SaaS-like business
- **Sophia Learning** subscription platform with 47% subscriber growth

If ETS were spun out or separately valued, it alone could justify $1B+ of the current $1.83B market cap. The USHE + ANZ segments would essentially be free.

**The catalyst for multiple expansion:** As ETS continues scaling (target: 20%+ of revenue within 2-3 years), analysts and the market should begin recognizing the mix shift. Meanwhile, the debt-free balance sheet, aggressive buybacks, and 2.9% dividend provide cushion during the transition.

**Key triggers to watch:**
- Q1 2026 earnings (April 23, 2026) — USHE enrollment and ETS growth
- Any regulatory clarity on BDTR (currently 8,640 applications pending)
- ETS revenue crossing $200M — would force analyst recognition
- Continued insider buying by Chairman Silberman

**Key risks:**
- USHE enrollment declines accelerating
- Strayer 90/10 breach
- DOE granting BDTR relief with recoupment
- AI credentialing replacing degrees meaningfully
- ANZ goodwill impairment (only 17% cushion)

This is a **patient-capital thesis** — the value is real but takes 2-3 years to surface. Not a trade, not a short-term story. But for a long-duration portfolio willing to ride out regulatory and sector overhangs, the risk/reward at ~$82 is favorable.
