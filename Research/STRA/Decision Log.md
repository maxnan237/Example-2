# Strategic Education Inc (STRA) — Decision Log

*Initiated: 2026-04-12*

---

## Investment Thesis Summary

STRA is a post-secondary education company with a debt-free balance sheet, 40%-margin ETS growth engine (Sophia, Workforce Edge), aggressive capital returns, and insider buying — trading at 12x forward earnings with a 2.9% dividend yield. Challenged by secular enrollment declines and regulatory overhangs.

**Bull thesis:** Debt-free balance sheet with $153M cash, ETS growing 41% at 40% margins, AI delivering $30M+ annual cost savings (expanding to $70M+ by 2027), Chairman buying in open market, $139M in FY2025 buybacks, forward P/E of 12x — a value + quality setup in an unloved sector.

**Bear thesis:** Secular enrollment decline (USHE -1.4% in FY2025), 90/10 Rule risk (Strayer at 89.64%), ~8,640 pending borrower defense applications, ANZ segment declining, AI disruption to degree credentials, demographic cliff starting 2026. The sector has real structural headwinds.

## Decision Framework

**To buy at ~$82:**
- ETS segment alone could be worth $1B+ at SaaS-like multiples (vs. $1.83B total market cap)
- Debt-free balance sheet with $153M cash (EV of only $1.68B)
- 11% total capital return yield (dividends + buybacks)
- Chairman buying in open market — insider conviction
- 12x forward P/E is cheap vs. quality metrics
- Sum-of-the-parts suggests $100-115 fair value

**To avoid:**
- Believe USHE enrollment will continue declining sharply
- Believe regulatory actions (BDTR, 90/10, OBBBA) will materially damage revenue
- Believe AI credentialing will replace degrees within 5 years
- Believe ETS growth will materially slow

**Current stance: Constructive value play.** STRA is a classic "overlooked small cap" — strong fundamentals, debt-free, aggressive capital returns, but in an out-of-favor sector with limited analyst coverage. The ETS segment is a genuine asset that the market is undervaluing. The overall risk/reward is favorable but requires patience — this is a 2-3 year thesis, not a short-term trade.

**Key triggers:**
- Q1 2026 earnings (April 23, 2026) — USHE enrollment trend, ETS growth
- Any regulatory clarity on BDTR or gainful employment
- ETS crossing $200M revenue (would force analyst recognition)
- Continued Chairman open-market buying
- Dividend increase (would signal management confidence)

**Key risks to monitor:**
- Strayer 90/10 breach
- Borrower defense relief granted by DOE
- USHE enrollment declining >5% in any quarter
- Any goodwill impairment charge
- AI-based credentialing gaining meaningful employer acceptance
