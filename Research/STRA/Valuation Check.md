## 7. Key Valuation Considerations

**The market is treating STRA as a single-entity for-profit university.** The ETS segment, which is effectively a B2B enterprise SaaS business with 40% margins and 41% growth, deserves a meaningfully higher multiple. If ETS continues to scale from 12% to 20%+ of revenue, the multiple should expand.

**Regulatory overhang discount is real but quantifiable.** At ~12x forward EPS vs. sector historical ~15x, the stock trades at roughly a 20% regulatory risk discount. This is arguably appropriate given Strayer's 90/10 proximity and BDTR exposure.

**Capital returns are accelerating.** $139M in buybacks in FY2025 at $81/share (near current price) is management voting with conviction. Combined capital return yield = ~11% ($197M / $1.83B). This is a high-quality income/total-return setup.
