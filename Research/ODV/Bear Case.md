# Osisko Development (ODV) — Bear Case

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12*

---

## 1. Thesis Killer: Going Concern Warning Is Real

The most important sentence in the FY2025 financial statements:

[ODV_FY2025_ex992.htm, Note 1]:
> "The working capital position as at December 31, 2025 combined with the available credit facilities and the proceeds received from subsequent equity financing completed after period end **will not be sufficient to meet the Company's obligations, commitments and forecasted expenditures up to March 2027**."

Translation: Even after raising C$640M+ through Dec 2025, an additional ~C$196M Feb 2026 bought deal, and C$25M Appian warrant exercise, ODV still doesn't have enough capital to reach first gold. This means **more equity raises are coming**, and the only question is:
- At what price?
- How much additional dilution?
- Will Appian draws be available when needed?

Every equity raise at US$3-4/share adds ~C$70-100M but dilutes existing holders 5-10%. At this rate, reaching first gold could require another 20-30% in dilution.

## 2. Structural Weaknesses

### 2a. FID is NOT yet declared

As of April 2026, ODV has not declared Final Investment Decision on Cariboo. This matters because:

- **US$350M of the Appian facility is conditional** on FID + milestones
- Without FID, construction ramp-up can't begin at full pace
- Original FS assumed Q3 2025 construction start; we're now 18+ months late
- The H2 2027 first gold timeline looks increasingly aspirational

**If FID slips to H2 2026**, first gold slips to 2028. Every year of delay adds:
- ~US$20-30M in Appian interest accrual
- ~C$30-50M in overhead burn
- Dilution risk as the company bridges the gap

### 2b. Ground conditions are worse than anticipated

[ODV_FY2025_ex993.htm]:
> "Development progress has been below plan while encountering challenging ground conditions in and around the Lowhee fault, requiring enhanced ground support."

This is concerning because:
- Development is 2.1 km completed (early stage)
- Lowhee fault area had some of the highest expected production
- Enhanced ground support = more cost, slower progress
- Ground conditions could be worse at depth (FS assumed 1,000m depth extensions)

### 2c. 5% NSR to OR Royalties is perpetual

At Cariboo base case (US$2,400/oz):
- Annual royalty payment: ~C$29M/year during production
- LOM total: ~C$292M

**The royalty never expires and has no buyback right.** Every future ounce of gold ODV produces generates a 5% haircut paid to an affiliated entity. This is structurally unattractive for long-term holders.

### 2d. Management ownership is negligible

Combined insider ownership is <1% of shares. For a C$1.4B market cap company requiring massive execution, management has limited skin-in-the-game. This creates:
- Less alignment on dilution decisions (management diluted 262% without proportionate personal pain)
- Less motivation to minimize costs (why cut corners for 0.2% of savings?)
- Less urgency on construction timeline

### 2e. Dilution has been extreme and continues

| Period | Shares Outstanding |
|---|---|
| Jan 2024 | 84M |
| Dec 2024 | 137M |
| Dec 2025 | 255M |
| Mar 2026 | 305M |
| **Fully diluted** | **428M** |

**262% dilution in 27 months. Fully diluted is another 41% on top of that.**

If ODV needs another C$300M before first gold (likely) and raises at US$3.50/share, that's another 90M shares = **15-20% more dilution**.

### 2f. Xatśūll First Nation legal risk

One of three affected First Nations has not signed a benefits agreement. Xatśūll has:
- Threatened judicial review of permits
- Cited Tsilhqot'in consent precedent (Supreme Court of Canada)
- Demanded consent, not just consultation

An adverse court ruling could:
- Suspend permits
- Require renegotiation
- Delay construction by 12-24 months
- Force costly additional mitigations

### 2g. Single-asset concentration

Cariboo is the sole material property. No diversification:
- Any problem at Cariboo = company problem
- Mine closure/impairment = existential
- No other cash flow sources (Tintic is tiny, San Antonio divested)

## 3. Assumptions the Bull Case Takes for Granted

### 3a. Gold stays high

Current US$4,750/oz gold is 98% above FS base case. **A 30-40% gold correction would return the company to marginal economics.** Gold at US$3,000/oz still works; gold at US$2,000/oz puts the project under pressure.

Historical precedent: Gold fell from US$1,900/oz (2011) to US$1,050/oz (2015) — a 45% decline over 4 years. It could do so again.

### 3b. Construction stays on budget

C$881M initial capex was estimated in April 2025. Construction in 2026-2027:
- Labor costs inflating
- Steel/cement costs volatile
- Equipment shortages in mining
- Ground conditions worse than planned

**Typical mining project capex overruns: 20-40%.** A 25% overrun adds C$220M in unfunded capex = another equity raise.

### 3c. Remaining Appian tranches become available

US$350M of Appian financing is conditional on milestones. If those milestones aren't met (or are met late), ODV must raise equity to fill the gap — at lower prices if gold falls.

### 3d. First gold occurs in H2 2027

Given:
- FID not declared as of April 2026
- 5-week suspension from fatality
- Ground conditions worse than planned
- ~30 months from construction start to first gold per FS

H2 2027 is improbable. **H1 2028 is more likely; H2 2028 is plausible.**

### 3e. Ramp-up occurs smoothly

Mining operations rarely hit nameplate capacity immediately:
- Typical ramp-up: 12-24 months
- Artemis Gold (BC, similar size) took ~9 months to commercial production
- Ground conditions problems could extend ramp-up

Revenue in production years 1-2 is typically 60-80% of nameplate.

### 3f. NSR doesn't compound with other obligations

The 5% NSR + Appian interest servicing + environmental rehabilitation provisions could stack in ways that leave less FCF to equity holders than FS modeling suggests.

## 4. Permanent Impairment Scenarios

### Scenario A: FID delayed 12+ months, equity raise at lower price
If gold falls to US$3,500/oz and FID slips to Q4 2026, ODV likely raises equity at US$2.50-3.00/share for ~C$200M. Dilution of another 25-30%. Fair value re-rates lower.

### Scenario B: Capex overrun + gold decline
C$1.1B actual capex vs. C$881M budget (+25%), combined with gold falling to US$2,800/oz. Project economics would fall from 22.1% IRR to ~8-10% IRR — marginal for equity returns.

### Scenario C: First Nations legal ruling
Xatśūll wins judicial review. Project suspended for 18-24 months. Interest continues accruing on Appian. Massive re-rating of the equity.

### Scenario D: Construction incident
Another serious incident causes extended shutdown. Regulator mandates safety improvements. Timeline slips 6-12 months. Investor confidence damaged.

### Scenario E: Trapped capital
Mine comes online but at higher costs than planned. FCF at spot prices (US$3,000/oz) is ~C$100M/year vs. C$158M FS base case. Payback extends from 2.8 years to 5+ years. Equity returns muted even if gold stays firm.

## 6. Governance Red Flags

1. **<1% insider ownership** — management compensation not aligned with preserving per-share value
2. **Combined CEO + Chairman** (Sean Roosen) — concentrated authority
3. **Related-party transactions** with OR Royalties (NSR, streams, historical spinout) — ongoing
4. **Warrant grants to lenders** (Appian got 5.6M warrants at C$4.43) — dilutive
5. **Flow-through shares** create exploration spending obligations
6. **Multiple share classes issued** at different prices in different years — inconsistent pricing
7. **Share-based compensation** (C$4.0M in FY2025) — modest but not zero dilutive

## Summary

ODV is a **leveraged bet on three simultaneous outcomes**: (1) gold prices staying elevated, (2) successful construction on budget and schedule, and (3) capital markets access for continued funding.

If all three work, the stock could 2-3x from current levels as Cariboo comes online in 2028. If any single vector breaks — gold correction, construction issues, funding difficulty, or Xatśūll legal action — the downside is severe (-40 to -70%).

The **going concern warning + 262% dilution + perpetual 5% NSR + FID delay + fatality + ground conditions + Xatśūll risk** create a genuinely high-risk profile. This is not a suitable holding for investors who cannot tolerate meaningful capital loss. At best, it warrants a small speculative position sized for the possibility of complete loss.
