# Osisko Development (ODV) — Moat Analysis

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12*

**Framework note:** Development-stage miners have fundamentally different "moat" dynamics than operating businesses. The relevant question is less about competitive advantage and more about **project defensibility** — does the asset itself have characteristics that are hard to replicate?

---

## 1. Switching Costs — **N/A**

Gold is a commodity. Buyers don't choose ODV's gold over another miner's gold — it all sells at spot (less transport/refining costs). No customer switching costs exist.

**Rating: N/A** (commodity business).

---

## 2. Network Effects — **Absent**

Single-asset mining company with commodity output. Zero network effects.

**Rating: Absent.**

---

## 3. Cost Advantages — **Moderate**

Cariboo's projected **AISC of US$1,157/oz** places it in the second quartile of global gold production cost curve. For context:
- Bottom decile: US$700-900/oz (best orebodies, simple geometry)
- **Cariboo: US$1,157/oz** (middle of the cost curve)
- Third quartile: US$1,400/oz
- Fourth quartile: US$1,700+/oz (marginal mines)

At current gold prices (~US$4,750/oz), every mine is profitable. Cost advantage matters more when gold falls below US$1,500/oz — then Cariboo's position is defensive but not dominant.

### Cost drivers

**Positive:**
- Underground mining can be lower cost than open pit if grades are high
- 3.62 g/t reserve grade is above industry average (~1.5-2.0 g/t open pit, ~4-6 g/t underground)
- Lowhee fault area shows higher grades (45+ g/t intercepts)
- Proximity to existing road/rail/power infrastructure

**Negative:**
- BC labor costs elevated
- Cold climate operations
- Ground conditions worse than anticipated (Lowhee fault) — reduces mining rate
- Complex vein geometry requires selective mining

**Rating: Moderate.** Competitive but not industry-leading.

## 4. Intangible Assets — **Strong (for mining)**

### Mineral reserves are the key intangible

Cariboo's **2.07 Moz probable reserves + 3.48 Moz M&I/inferred resources** represent a proven orebody that cannot be created — only discovered. ODV spent years and ~$100M+ in exploration to prove up this resource.

### Permits are effectively irreplaceable

[ODV_FY2025_ex993.htm]:
> "Together with the Mines Act permits, these approvals mark the successful completion of the permitting process [...] solidifying the Cariboo Gold Project's **shovel-ready status**."

Permitting a new BC underground gold mine today would take:
- **4+ years** (ODV's EA took approximately 4 years)
- **C$50-100M+** in engineering, environmental, and consultation costs
- Increasingly onerous First Nations consent requirements

**Cariboo is already fully permitted (as of Q4 2024)** — this is a significant intangible asset that would be extraordinarily difficult for any competitor to replicate in the same jurisdiction.

### Brand / track record

The "Osisko" brand carries credibility in Canadian mining finance. Sean Roosen's Canadian Malartic track record (built, sold to Agnico/Yamana for C$3.9B) provides market confidence — though ODV's execution has been mixed since spinout.

### IP / Technology

No meaningful IP; mining is not a technology business.

**Rating: Strong.** Permits + proven reserves are the real intangible assets.

---

## 5. Efficient Scale — **Absent**

Gold mining is a fragmented global industry. Hundreds of companies produce gold; thousands of exploration-stage projects exist. No natural oligopoly protects ODV.

**Rating: Absent.**

## Moat Trajectory: **Narrowing → Potentially Widening**

### Currently narrowing because:

1. **FID delay** — each quarter without declaration extends timeline and reduces optionality
2. **Dilution** — 262% share growth in 27 months dilutes each shareholder's claim on the orebody
3. **Construction risks** — ground conditions worse than expected; fatality caused site suspension
4. **5% NSR to OR Royalties** — perpetual 5% take off the top, regardless of execution success

### Potentially widens if:

1. **FID declared in 2026** — unlocks remaining US$350M Appian facility
2. **First gold pour achieved** — transitions from developer (higher risk, lower multiple) to producer (lower risk, higher multiple)
3. **Resource expansion** — 160,000m 2026 drilling campaign may meaningfully grow reserves
4. **Upside from current gold prices** — Cariboo economics at US$4,750/oz are dramatically better than FS base case

## Special Consideration: The "Osisko Option Value"

ODV has one characteristic most junior miners lack: a **path to strategic buyout by the Osisko family** or another Canadian gold major.

Sean Roosen has a pattern of building mines then selling them to larger operators:
- **Canadian Malartic** → sold to Agnico Eagle / Yamana (2014) for C$3.9B
- **Osisko Mining (Windfall)** → sold to Gold Fields / South32 JV (Dec 2023) for ~C$2.2B
- **ODV (Cariboo)?** → Potential sale target once de-risked

This creates embedded **acquisition optionality** — a large gold producer (Agnico, Barrick, Newmont, Kinross) could acquire ODV post-FID or post-first-pour at a premium.

**Not a moat in the traditional sense, but a structural feature of Canadian junior mining.**

---

## Moat Summary

ODV is a **commodity producer without meaningful competitive moat** in the traditional sense. Its defensibility rests on:

1. **Permitted orebody** (Cariboo is shovel-ready) — hard to replicate in BC
2. **Proven reserves** (2.07 Moz P&P) — provides production visibility once operating
3. **Acquisition optionality** — Sean Roosen's track record of selling built mines

Against these positives:
- **5% perpetual NSR to OR Royalties** — permanent economic drag
- **Single-asset concentration** — no diversification
- **Commodity price dependence** — no control over revenue price
- **Capital-intensive business** — requires continuous funding access

The moat is best characterized as **jurisdictional/regulatory** (permits are the real barrier to entry) rather than economic. Companies like Cariboo are rare, but ODV itself has no structural advantage against another well-capitalized operator who might buy the asset out of financial distress.
