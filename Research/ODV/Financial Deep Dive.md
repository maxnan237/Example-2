## 6. Streaming & Royalty Obligations

### 1. Cariboo 5% NSR to OR Royalties (PERPETUAL)

The most economically significant ongoing encumbrance. At base case (US$2,400/oz), the 5% NSR represents:
- ~C$292M in LOM royalty payments
- ~C$29M/year during production years
- ~12% of Cariboo operating costs + royalties

**No buyback right disclosed** — this is a permanent 5% haircut on Cariboo revenue.

### 2. Tintic 2.5% → 2.0% metal stream (US$20M advance received)

OR Royalties entitled to 2.5% of refined metal production from Tintic until 27,150 oz gold delivered, then 2.0% thereafter. 25% of spot paid by OR on delivery.

## Accounting Flags

### 1. Warrant liability volatility drives reported losses
$88M non-cash charge in FY2025 from change in warrant liability fair value. This will continue reporting as earnings volatility as gold price and share price move.

### 2. Impairment charges recur
Tintic ($138M in FY2023), QR Mill ($28M in FY2025). Both reflect prior acquisition overpayment. Any future acquisition carries similar risk.

### 3. Capitalization of interest costs
$12.6M in interest capitalized into mining interests during FY2025 — this understates current-period interest expense and embeds more capital in the project balance.

### 4. Environmental rehabilitation provisions
C$99M in mine closure obligations. Mostly non-cash today but represents real future cash outflow.

### 5. Flow-through shares
Oct 2025 raise included C$30M of flow-through shares at 35-45% premium to common shares. These create a tax benefit for purchasers but create obligation on ODV to spend a specified amount on eligible exploration expenses.

### 6. Warrant liability at $225M is ~18% of market cap
As gold/share prices rise, this liability grows, creating a negative feedback to reported earnings — but no cash impact. Investors should look through this item.
