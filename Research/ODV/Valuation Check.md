## 2. P/NAV Analysis

### What does current market cap imply?

**At April 2025 Feasibility Study base case (US$2,400/oz):**
- After-tax NPV (5%): **C$943M**
- ODV fully-diluted market cap: **C$1.4B**
- **Implied P/NAV: 1.48x** (overvalued at base case)

**At April 2025 FS spot case (US$3,300/oz):**
- After-tax NPV (5%): **C$2,066M**
- Implied P/NAV: **0.68x** (fair to cheap)

**At current spot gold (~US$4,750/oz) — extrapolated:**
- Estimated after-tax NPV (5%): **~C$3.5-4.5B** (linear extrapolation)
- Implied P/NAV: **0.30-0.40x** (substantially cheap)

**Interpretation:** ODV trades at P/NAV of ~0.3-0.4x at current gold prices, compared to:
- Producing peers (Artemis Gold): P/NAV 1.5-2.0x
- Development-stage peers with FID: P/NAV 0.6-0.8x
- Development-stage pre-FID peers: P/NAV 0.3-0.5x (where ODV sits)

ODV is priced as a pre-FID developer. Upon FID + first gold, a re-rating to 0.8-1.0x P/NAV is plausible = **2-3x upside**.

## 8. Key Valuation Considerations

### Why the stock trades below NAV

1. **Going concern warning** — literally in the auditor's opinion
2. **FID delay** — construction not formally underway
3. **Dilution overhang** — 115.6M warrants outstanding, more raises coming
4. **Xatśūll legal risk** — live litigation threat
5. **Execution risk** — Sean Roosen's track record has some misses (Osisko Development itself hasn't delivered a built mine yet)
6. **Ground conditions** — Lowhee fault issues reduce confidence
7. **5% NSR perpetual burden** — economic drag

### What could close the gap

1. **FID declaration** — single biggest catalyst
2. **First gold pour** — transitions to producer multiple
3. **Resource expansion** from 2026 drilling campaign
4. **Xatśūll agreement** or favorable court ruling
5. **Strategic buyer interest** — Agnico, Kinross, or similar could bid
6. **Continued gold price strength** — provides margin for execution issues

## 9. Acquisition Value

Gold majors acquiring advanced-stage development projects typically pay:
- **1.0-1.5x P/NAV** (at acquirer-consensus gold prices)
- **25-50% premium to recent trading**

For ODV:
- Acquirer might assume US$3,500/oz long-term gold → NAV ~C$2.5B
- 1.0x P/NAV → offer price ~C$2.5B = ~C$5.80/fully-diluted share
- **30% premium to current = ~C$6/share**

Potential acquirers:
- **Agnico Eagle** — operates in Canada, has capital
- **Barrick** — US-based, active acquirer
- **Kinross** — mid-tier with Canadian operations
- **Newmont** — recently digested Newcrest; could pause
- **Wesdome / SSR** — smaller but could stretch
- **Chinese majors** — have been acquiring Canadian gold assets

**Takeover optionality is real but not immediate** — most buyers prefer de-risked projects, meaning acquisition is more likely post-FID or post-first-gold.

## Summary

ODV trades at **P/NAV of ~0.3-0.4x** based on current gold prices — historically cheap but reflects real execution risks. The valuation framework is extreme:

- **Base case** (modest execution issues, gold at US$3,500/oz): Fair value ~C$3 (-32%)
- **Bull case** (smooth execution, gold holds at US$4,500): Fair value ~C$10.50 (+125%)
- **Bear case** (execution problems, gold falls): Fair value ~C$0.44 (-90%)

This is a **binary, leveraged bet on gold prices + construction execution**. Sizing matters — this is not a core holding but may be appropriate as a small speculative position for investors with gold exposure views and high risk tolerance.
