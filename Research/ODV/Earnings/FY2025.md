# ODV — FY2025 Annual Results Analysis

*Fiscal year ended December 31, 2025 | Reported March 28, 2026*
*Sources: Q4/FY2025 Press Release (GlobeNewswire), Audited Consolidated Financial Statements, MD&A*

**Note:** ODV is a Canadian filer reporting under IFRS in CAD. Pre-production miner — traditional earnings metrics less meaningful than operational and capital milestones.

---

## Section 1: Financial Results (CAD)

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $35.5M | $4.6M | **+677%** |
| Operating Loss | ($81.7M) | ($62.4M) | Widened |
| Net Loss | ($169.0M) | ($86.3M) | Widened |
| EPS (diluted) | ($0.95) | ($0.92) | Flat |
| Operating Cash Flow | ($25.0M) | ($52.3M) | **Improved** |

### What drove the revenue jump

FY2025 revenue of $35.5M came almost entirely from Tintic (Utah) small-scale mining:
- 3,970 oz gold sold from Trixie test mining operations
- Average gold price received ~US$2,600/oz

**This is NOT Cariboo revenue** — Cariboo remains pre-production.

### What drove the net loss

| Loss Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Operating loss | ($81.7M) |
| Change in FV of warrant liability | ($88.4M) |
| Finance costs | ($6.7M) |
| Other income | +$17.2M |
| Net loss (continuing ops) | ($159.5M) |
| Loss from discontinued (San Antonio) | ($9.5M) |
| **Net Loss (Total)** | **($169.0M)** |

**$88M of the $169M loss is non-cash** — related to warrant liability fair value mark-to-market. As gold/share prices rise, warrant liability grows. Ignore this line for analytical purposes.

## Section 3: Critical Events (Post Year-End)

### January 22, 2026 — Contractor fatality
- Worker died in surface activity incident
- Site suspended
- Investigation completed
- **March 2, 2026:** Site activities resumed after regulatory approval

This 5-6 week suspension set back the already-delayed timeline.

### February 2026 — US$143.8M Bought Deal

Raised at US$3.54/share (40.6M new shares). Increased shares outstanding to 295.7M. At this point, fully diluted to 428M.

### February 2026 — JDS Energy & Mining contracted

Project and Construction Management Services contract awarded to JDS. Major operational step — indicates serious intent to move toward construction.

### March 9, 2026 — Appian warrant exercise

5.6M warrants exercised at C$4.43 = C$24.9M proceeds. Lender-held warrants from July 2025 facility.

## Section 6: 2026 Outlook & Management Commentary

### 2026 budgeted expenditures at Cariboo: C$61.8M

Breakdown:
- Underground development: $40.2M
- Regional surface exploration: $6.8M
- Mine design/processing/other: $9.9M
- Infill drilling: $0.6M
- Conversion drilling: $1.8M
- Surface directional drilling: $2.5M

**Does NOT include** major construction capex which requires FID.

### Drilling program

**160,000m 2026 drilling campaign** underway — largest in company history:
- Infill/conversion drilling at known deposits
- Exploration drilling to 1,000m depth extensions
- 3 rigs operating
- ~5,000m completed as of March 2026

Could meaningfully expand reserves if successful.

### First gold timeline

Management has NOT publicly revised the H2 2027 first gold timeline, but given:
- FID not declared (18+ months later than FS assumed)
- 5-6 week fatality-related suspension
- Ground conditions worse than planned
- 30-month construction period per FS

**H2 2027 is aspirational. H1 2028 is more realistic. H2 2028+ is plausible.**

## Section 7: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved vs. FY2024
- **Appian US$450M facility secured** — largest financing in company history
- **Cash position 4x higher** — C$106M → C$422M
- **Operating burn 52% lower** — C$52M → C$25M (Tintic revenue helped)
- **Full permitting achieved** (Dec 2024) — removes regulatory risk
- **Construction manager hired** (JDS in Feb 2026)

### Materially deteriorated
- **Dilution +87% shares YoY** (255M vs. 137M)
- **Warrant liability +232%** ($225M vs. $68M)
- **Contractor fatality** — safety record impaired
- **Ground conditions worse than planned** — construction risk up
- **Going concern warning** — first appearance in audit opinion

### Net verdict

FY2025 was a **mixed year of significant capital raising and setbacks**. The company has more resources than ever but faces execution challenges that continue to delay first gold.
