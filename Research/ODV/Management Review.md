# Osisko Development (ODV) — Management Review

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12*

---

## 1. CEO: Sean Roosen

### Background

- **Age:** ~65 (born early 1960s, exact not disclosed)
- **Education:** Haileybury School of Mines (Ontario)
- **30+ years** in mining industry
- **Role at ODV:** Executive Chairman AND CEO (since November 2020 spinout)

### Track record

**The Success:**
- **Canadian Malartic (Quebec)** — Co-founded Osisko Mining Corporation in 2003. Built Canada's largest open-pit gold mine. Sold to Agnico Eagle/Yamana in 2014 for C$3.9B. **Massive value creation.**
- **OR Royalties** — Founded in 2014 using Canadian Malartic royalty as seed asset. Grew to $4B+ royalty company. Roosen was CEO/Chair until 2020.
- **Osisko Mining (OSK)** — Windfall project in Quebec. Sold to Gold Fields / South32 JV in Dec 2023 for ~C$2.2B. Another successful build-and-sell outcome.

**The Mixed Results:**
- **ODV** — Still pre-production 5+ years after formation. Stock has traded as low as US$1.43 (down 80% from 2021 highs). Significant dilution. Execution slower than planned.
- **Hammond Reef** — Earlier project; not developed.

### Assessment

Roosen has a **strong historical track record** but ODV itself has not yet produced a mine under his leadership. The Canadian Malartic playbook (find undervalued district, build big mine, sell to major) has worked before — but ODV is 5+ years into execution without the big exit. Questions for investors:

1. Is ODV the next Canadian Malartic — or the exception that proves his record isn't transferable to this project?
2. Why combined CEO/Chair role instead of separating them?
3. Why <1% aggregate insider ownership? (Canadian Malartic had stronger insider participation)

### Awards

- 2017 Mines and Money Americas "Best CEO in North America"
- Frequent conference keynote / recognized industry figure

## 4. Capital Allocation

### History of capital deployment

**Since spinout (November 2020):**
- Raised ~C$1.4B+ in cumulative share capital
- Accumulated deficit: C$767M — implies ~55% of invested capital has been destroyed through operating losses and impairments

### Capital allocation track record

**The bad:**
- **Tintic acquisition** (May 2022, US$156.6M) — taken C$138M impairment in FY2023 and is now on care and maintenance. A roughly 90% destruction of invested capital.
- **Multiple equity raises at prices below IPO** — diluted long-term holders substantially
- **High-cost debt** — SOFR + 9.6% on Appian facility reflects distressed pricing

**The good:**
- **San Antonio divestiture** (Jan 2026) — exited Mexican project that wasn't core; freed capital
- **Permitting success** at Cariboo — completed full permitting in 2023-2024
- **Raised capital at increasing prices** in 2025-2026 (US$1.80 → US$2.05 → US$3.54) — suggests improving execution narrative

### Going-forward priorities

Management has signaled:
1. Focus on Cariboo build-out
2. Continue exploration drilling (160,000m in 2026)
3. Potentially another acquisition (but no specific target)

- 2025 FS assumed Q3 2025 construction start — delayed
- **Pattern: Construction start keeps slipping**

**Achieved:**

- Resource estimates (consistent with drilling results)
- Financial raises (completed when needed)

### Assessment

**Guidance credibility is mixed.** Financial/permitting milestones have been met; construction/FID timelines have repeatedly slipped. Investors should discount any forward FID timeline by 6-12 months.

- Limited personal downside if stock falls further

## 8. Related-Party Transactions

### OR Royalties relationship

ODV was spun out from OR Royalties in 2020 via reverse takeover. Ongoing relationships:

1. **5% NSR royalty on Cariboo** — perpetual, significant economic drag
2. **Tintic metal stream** (2.5% → 2.0%)
3. **Board nomination rights** (until OR drops below 9.9%)
4. **Right of first refusal** on royalty/stream deals (until OR drops below 10%)

These arrangements benefit OR Royalties at ODV's expense. Board independence questions arise given the intertwined Osisko family.

### Assessment

Transparently disclosed but inherently conflicted. OR Royalties is effectively a perpetual levy on ODV's future gold production — set up at spinout when ODV was a pure subsidiary. Current ODV shareholders inherited this structure.

---

## 9. Overall Assessment: **Mixed**

### Strengths
- Roosen's historical track record (Canadian Malartic)
- Osisko Mining's Windfall success (recent)
- Strong technical hires in 2025-2026
- Capital market access (can raise when needed)
- Transparent disclosures

### Concerns
- **Combined CEO + Chairman** role
- **<1% aggregate insider ownership**
- **Construction timeline slippage** (pattern, not one-off)
- **Massive dilution history** (262% in 27 months)
- **Going concern warning** in FY2025 audit
- **Related-party NSR** permanently drags economics
- **Tintic capital destruction** shows M&A judgment imperfect

### Verdict

Roosen is **highly capable** in mining finance and M&A but has not yet demonstrated strong mine construction execution under his direct leadership at ODV. Canadian Malartic was a joint effort with partners; Windfall was sold before being built. **ODV is effectively Roosen's first solo build-and-operate project as CEO** — and it's not going particularly smoothly.

The team is experienced but unproven together. Investors should weight the track record appropriately — Canadian Malartic doesn't guarantee Cariboo success.
