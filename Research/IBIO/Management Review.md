## Capital Allocation Track Record

### The Case for the Prosecution

**1. The Bryan, TX CDMO disaster ($20M+ in value destroyed).**

- **November 2021:** Purchased Bryan, TX facility for $28.75M (financed $22.375M with Woodforest) under the prior management team and strategic direction.
- **November 2022:** Announced divestiture of CDMO business.
- **FY2023-2024:** $21M in impairments recognized.
- **May 2024:** Sold to Texas A&M Board of Regents for **$8.5M** — proceeds wired directly to lender. $4.5M deficiency settled with pre-funded warrants to Woodforest. $4.8M loss on sale recognized.

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Note 5]: "During the fiscal year ended June 30, 2024, the Company recorded an additional fixed asset impairment charge of $3.1 million, a loss on the sale of the Facility of approximately $4.8 million."

*Mitigating fact:* the 2021 purchase predated Brenner's CEO tenure (June 2023). He inherited the facility and executed the divestiture — arguably competent wind-down management, but the strategic error belongs to predecessors.

**2. Aggressive and value-destructive dilution.**

Two reverse splits (1:25 in Oct 2022, 1:20 in Nov 2023) required by serial mass equity issuance. The August 2025 Leerink offering at $0.699/unit priced at roughly 30% of where the stock was a year prior, adding 143M+ potential shares of dilution overhang.

The **Warrant Inducement Transaction in April 2025** — exercising 5.6M warrants at $0.86 while issuing 11.2M new warrants at $0.125 in exchange — is a classic desperate-financing move. It raised $6.2M gross but at severe cost to existing holders.

**3. Contingent milestones paid with equity.**

Both AstralBio licenses were paid with stock or stock-derived credits rather than cash. This preserves cash but transfers value to AstralBio at prevailing (depressed) share prices.

### The Case for the Defense

**1. Prior management's strategic errors.**

The plant-based biologics strategy (FastPharming) and Bryan, TX facility purchase predate Brenner's tenure. Blaming current leadership for inherited capital structures is unfair.

**2. Speedy pivot execution.**

The September 2022 RubrYc acquisition (under Brenner as CSO) bought a real AI platform and legitimate oncology candidates for **just $1M in stock + up to $5M milestones**. The acquisition was struck as RubrYc was running out of cash — opportunistic and low-cost.

**3. The AstralBio collaboration** has generated three obesity/cardiometabolic programs (IBIO-600, IBIO-610, amylin agonist) from a minimal cash outlay. If any succeed, the economics are highly attractive ($28M milestones per program is small relative to typical biotech economics).

**4. PD-1 divestiture.** Selling the PD-1 agonist to Otsuka for $1M upfront + $52.5M potential milestones (February 2024) converted a non-core preclinical asset into cash and optionality — sensible portfolio management.

**5. Cash runway extension.** Under Brenner's tenure, the company has extended cash runway from quarterly-dependent to **into Q3 FY2028 (~March 2028)** — nearly 2 years of runway. Funding Phase 1 data readouts for both IBIO-600 and potentially IBIO-610 without another raise is now plausible.

| Prior Guidance | Delivered? |

**Assessment:** Guidance under Brenner has been mixed. The IBIO-101 oncology timeline slipped materially and was then re-scoped (a quiet admission the program is not advancing). The CDMO sale timeline also slipped. However, the critical milestone — IBIO-600 reaching first-in-human — is being delivered on schedule, which is the only guidance that currently matters to investors.

### Focus

The strategy has clearly narrowed to **obesity/cardiometabolic as the primary focus**, with oncology programs in "partnering-available" mode. This is honest and appropriate — a 20-person team cannot drive multiple Phase 1 programs simultaneously.

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Strategy]: "As we continue to leverage our technology stack and develop our existing immune-oncology pre-clinical pipeline, we are also seeking strategic partners with the capabilities to more rapidly advance these programs towards the clinic."

## Overall Assessment

### Strengths
- **Strong CSO/scientific leadership** (Brenner pedigree is legitimate)
- **Credible strategic pivot** to obesity/cardiometabolic with in-licensed differentiated assets
- **Capital-efficient platform** through AstralBio structure
- **First-in-human timeline being delivered**
- **Healthcare-specialist institutional support** (Frazier, EcoR1, Logos)

### Weaknesses
- **Dual CEO/CSO role** in a capital-dependent business
- **Low insider ownership** (~2.5%) and no open-market insider buying
- **History of massive value destruction** (Bryan TX facility; serial dilution; reverse splits)
- **Missed prior guidance** on IBIO-101 timeline
- **Capital allocation still skews toward equity-funded survival**, not clinical optionality monetization

### Rating: **Mixed — competent but under pressure.**

Brenner and Duran inherited a broken company and have executed a difficult pivot with limited capital. The Phase 1 IBIO-600 clearance is the first major deliverable under this team's tenure. Whether they can translate that into sustained clinical validation and a non-dilutive partnership deal remains unproven.

Management should be judged primarily on the following forward tests:

1. **Do they deliver clean IBIO-600 Phase 1 safety / PK data by mid-2027?**
2. **Do they secure a Big Pharma partnership for IBIO-600 or IBIO-610 before needing another dilutive raise?**
3. **Do they avoid a third reverse stock split?**
4. **Do they monetize the oncology pipeline for meaningful non-dilutive cash?**

Any two of these four would signal vastly improved management effectiveness.
