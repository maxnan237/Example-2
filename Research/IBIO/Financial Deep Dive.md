## 10. The Financial Bottom Line

iBio's financials tell a clear story: **a clinical-stage biotech with negligible revenue, persistent losses, a $332M accumulated deficit, and an existential dependence on capital markets that has resulted in 500:1 dilution and two reverse stock splits in four years.**

- **Positive:** Cash runway is now credible (~$77M pro-forma, into ~March 2028). Going concern is formally alleviated. Operating expenses are controlled and R&D spending is focused on the two lead obesity programs.
- **Negative:** The company has burned $332M of cumulative investor capital. Each round of dilution transfers value from existing holders to new buyers at progressively lower prices. Another ~143M warrant-share overhang from the August 2025 offering will flood the float if IBIO-600 Phase 1 is successful and price rises above $0.70 (Series G/H strike). A third reverse split is plausible.

The financial structure is **engineered for capital-markets survival**, not for shareholders. That dynamic may change only if IBIO-600 produces clinical data compelling enough to support a partnership deal — the only plausible non-dilutive funding path.
