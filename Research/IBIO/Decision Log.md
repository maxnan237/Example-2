## Current Stance: **Speculative — Watchlist Only**

iBio is a binary-outcome preclinical biotech trading near net cash. At $2.22, the stock offers ~$0.06/share of implied pipeline value — essentially a free option on IBIO-600 Phase 1 success, offset by substantial dilution overhang and pipeline execution risk.

**I am not a buyer at $2.22** for three reasons:
1. The August 2025 Series G/H warrant overhang (71M+ shares at $0.70 strike) creates a ceiling on meaningful upside until warrants are retired or expire
2. First-in-human dosing has only just cleared; no clinical data yet exists
3. Base-rate Phase 1 failure probability in antibody biologics is 60-75%; at current valuation, the probability-weighted fair value is only marginally above current price

**I would consider initiating a small, speculative position (≤0.5% of portfolio)** at or below net cash (~$2.10/share) as a pure option on IBIO-600 Phase 1 data.

**I would consider adding** if any of the following catalysts materialize:
- Clean IBIO-600 Phase 1 safety data (late 2026 / early 2027)
- Big Pharma partnership deal for IBIO-600 or IBIO-610
- Biohaven BHVN Phase 2 obesity data positive (2H 2026) — validates class

## Decision Framework

### When to add
- Stock trades below **$2.00** (below net cash per share) AND no negative pipeline news
- IBIO-600 Phase 1 produces **clean safety + PK in allometric range** (half-life >40 days in humans)
- **Partnership deal** announced with Big Pharma: $30M+ upfront for IBIO-600 or IBIO-610
- **Biohaven Phase 2 obesity data positive** 2H 2026 — validates myostatin class for obesity

### When to trim or exit
- IBIO-600 Phase 1 **safety signal** (SAEs, immunogenicity, hepatic/cardiac findings)
- IBIO-600 Phase 1 **PK failure** — human half-life <30 days, eliminating dosing differentiation
- **Third reverse stock split** announced or executed
- **Capital raise** at price below $1.50 in an emergency context
- **AstralBio license termination** on any program
- Stock trades above **$4.50** without commensurate clinical de-risking (selling into sell-side promotional rally)

### When to avoid entirely
- Any Nasdaq minimum-bid-price deficiency notice returns
- Cash runway shortens below 12 months in any quarterly update
- **Key management departure** (CEO or CSO)
- Patent portfolio further contracts or key IBIO-600/610 patents face opposition

## Position Sizing

At current price and facts, position size should be **capped at 0.5% of portfolio** given:
- Binary clinical outcome (60-75% base-rate Phase 1 failure)
- Dilution overhang reduces even bull-case upside
- Liquidity risk (34.5M shares outstanding, 27M float; wide bid-ask for large positions)
- Information asymmetry vs. healthcare specialist funds (EcoR1, Frazier, Logos)

Appropriate framing: **this is venture-capital-style optionality in public-equity wrapper**, not a long-term investment thesis.

## Bottom Line

**iBio is a speculative ticker on the watchlist, not a core holding.** The combination of (a) a small-cap, preclinical-stage biotech, (b) near-net-cash valuation, (c) imminent Phase 1 data catalyst, and (d) legitimate mechanism differentiation (2-4x/year dosing) creates a genuine asymmetric option — but only if entered at or below net cash and sized appropriately.

**Current action: Monitor.** Re-evaluate at Q3 FY2026 earnings (May 18, 2026) and upon first-patient-dosing announcement. Convert to small position only if entry price falls to ~$2.00 or if IBIO-600 Phase 1 produces compelling early safety / PK data.
