## What Changed This Quarter

### 1. Capital Structure Tightened Dramatically
- **August 22, 2025:** $50M gross / $46.5M net Leerink offering closed (71.5M pre-funded warrants + 71.5M Series G/H warrants @ $0.699/unit)
- **January 13, 2026:** **$26M gross / $24.4M net** PIPE — 11.06M shares at $2.35, led by **Frazier Life Sciences**, co-placement agents Leerink, Oppenheimer, LifeSci
- **Cash at Dec 31, 2025:** $52.7M (does NOT include January 2026 PIPE)
- **Pro-forma cash ~$77M** with ~2 years runway

The January 2026 PIPE is a material positive. Frazier Life Sciences is a respected healthcare specialist fund; leading a $26M PIPE in a microcap biotech signals genuine pipeline conviction.

### 2. IBIO-600 Crosses Clinical Threshold
- **April 8, 2026 (post-quarter):** Australia TGA clearance for Phase 1 single ascending dose (SAD) trial in overweight/obese adults
- First patient dosing expected Q2 2026 (current quarter)
- Study design: randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled

### 3. R&D Spending Accelerating Sharply
- Q2 FY26 R&D of $4.3M is **126% higher** than the year-ago quarter ($1.9M)
- Reflects GLP toxicology study costs, clinical trial start-up fees, and IBIO-610 IND-enabling work
- Annualized FY26 R&D is trending toward $18-22M vs. FY25 $8.3M — **over 2x**

### 4. G&A Also Rising
- Q2 FY26 G&A of $5.2M — up **93%** YoY
- Drivers not detailed in external sources; likely includes higher personnel comp, capital-raise legal/advisory fees, and increased public-company compliance costs
- **Flag:** G&A running higher than R&D ($5.2M vs. $4.3M) is unusual for a Phase 1 biotech; should normalize as clinical spending grows

### 5. Nasdaq Compliance Regained
- November 2025 (external): Nasdaq notice closed after regaining minimum bid price compliance
- Eliminates near-term forced-reverse-split risk

## Quick Assessment

**Q2 FY2026 was a transitional quarter.** Operationally, IBIO-600 hit its clinical-stage milestone, validating the 18-month strategic pivot. Financially, the January 2026 PIPE materially strengthened the balance sheet and brought in a credible anchor investor (Frazier). But burn rate is doubling year-over-year as clinical costs materialize, and the stock continues to trade near net cash — suggesting the market still assigns minimal value to the pipeline.

**Next catalyst (first patient dosed) is weeks away; the critical catalyst (Phase 1 safety data) is 6-12 months away.** Positioning until then should be patient.
