# iBio (IBIO) — Bear Case

---

## Thesis Killer

**IBIO-600 Phase 1 data fails.** Any of the following would be terminal or near-terminal:
- **Safety signal** (serious immune-mediated adverse events, unexpected cardiovascular or hepatic findings)
- **PK failure** — measured human half-life comes in well below the 57-147 day allometric prediction, collapsing the 2-4x/year dosing thesis and making IBIO-600 undifferentiated from Regeneron's trevogrumab
- **Immunogenicity** — anti-drug antibody development in humans at rates that compromise repeat dosing

**Timeline:** First patient dosed Q2 2026. Initial safety and PK readout expected late 2026 / early 2027. The stock will be event-driven around that window.

**Why it kills the thesis:** iBio is a **2-asset company** (IBIO-600 + IBIO-610). IBIO-610 is 12+ months behind IBIO-600 and faces similar mechanism risk. If IBIO-600 fails, iBio's clinical pipeline collapses to a preclinical-only company with $60-70M cash and a ~$25-30M market cap — followed by years of wind-down or further pivot.

---

## Structural Weaknesses

### 1. In-Licensed Lead Assets (Not Owned)

Both IBIO-600 and IBIO-610 are **in-licensed from AstralBio**. iBio does not own composition-of-matter IP at the molecule level; it pays up to **$28M in milestones per asset** plus single-digit % sublicense fees to AstralBio.

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors Summary]: "Our most advanced product candidates depend on intellectual property licensed from third parties and termination of any of these or future licenses could result in the loss of significant rights, which could adversely affect our business, results of operations and financial condition."

AstralBio has substantial leverage. If iBio fails to meet development milestones, AstralBio can terminate — stripping iBio of its only clinical-stage assets.

### 2. Sub-Scale Competitor in a Mega-Cap Market

Direct peers Regeneron (~$50B+), Scholar Rock ($5.7B), Biohaven ($1.4B) all have **deeper pipelines, bigger teams, more capital, and earlier/more advanced clinical data**. Regeneron specifically is already in Phase 2 for trevogrumab + semaglutide (COURAGE). Even if iBio's long-half-life thesis is correct, first-to-file / first-to-data economic advantages in biotech typically go to better-capitalized peers.

### 3. Serial Capital-Markets Dependency

iBio has raised equity in essentially every fiscal year since 2020. The company:
- Executed **two reverse splits** (1:25 in Oct 2022, 1:20 in Nov 2023)
- Hit **Nasdaq minimum bid price non-compliance** July 2025 (cured November 2025)
- Issued a **"Warrant Inducement Transaction" in April 2025** — a desperate-financing technique
- Issued **71.5M pre-funded warrants + 71.5M Series G/H warrants** in August 2025 at $0.699/unit
- Sold **11.06M shares at $2.35** to Frazier-led PIPE in January 2026

Outstanding warrants (16M at Jun 30, 2025) + post-year-end August 2025 issuance (~143M more potential shares) create **massive dilution overhang**: if the stock rises above the $0.70 strike, Series G/H warrants flood the float with ~71M additional shares.

### 4. Management Concentration + Key Person Risk

Dr. Martin Brenner holds **both CEO and CSO roles**. If Brenner departs or is incapacitated, iBio loses its primary scientific voice and external-facing credibility simultaneously.

### 5. No Manufacturing Infrastructure

After selling Bryan, TX for $8.5M (vs. $28.75M purchase price, ~$20M+ value destroyed), iBio has **no internal manufacturing**. All biologic production is outsourced to Lonza and other CDMOs — a margin-compressive and supply-chain-vulnerable position.

### 6. Patent Portfolio Collapsed

Issued patents dropped from **69 (FY2024) to 16 (FY2025)** as the legacy plant-based portfolio expired. iBio now relies on pending applications (23 US, 47 international) that may or may not issue with defensible claims.

## Permanent Impairment Scenarios

- Stock collapses to ~$0.50-$1.00 (trading at deep discount to cash)
- Clinical pipeline effectively gone; company becomes a cash shell + legacy oncology programs
- Management forced to liquidate or attempt third pivot
- **Permanent loss: 55-80%** from current ~$2.22

### Scenario B: PK Failure — Human Half-Life Much Shorter Than Predicted
- Thesis of "2-4x/year dosing" collapses
- IBIO-600 becomes indistinguishable from Regeneron's trevogrumab (which already has Phase 2 data)
- No partnership deal; iBio becomes clinical also-ran
- Company continues burning cash toward IBIO-610 data (early 2027) but with damaged credibility

### Scenario C: Competitor Dominates (Biohaven Phase 2 + Regeneron Phase 2)

- Class value crystallizes but iBio is too far behind to capture meaningful share

- **Permanent loss: 30-50%**

### Scenario D: Capital Crisis — Third Reverse Split + Mass Dilution
- IBIO-600 Phase 1 delayed; cash runway compresses; stock falls below $1 and stays there
- Forced 1-for-10 reverse stock split late 2027
- Emergency raise at effective $0.30 pre-split price
- Shareholder base turns over entirely; long-time holders permanently impaired
- **Permanent loss: 70-90%**

### Scenario E: Class-Wide Safety Concerns Emerge
- Regeneron COURAGE readout reveals triplet safety signal (already 2 deaths in trial, though in high-CV-risk population)
- FDA raises questions about myostatin/activin class safety
- IBIO-600 Phase 1 further delayed or scope-reduced
- Entire subsector re-rates down 40-60%; iBio as smallest player suffers most
- **Permanent loss: 60-80%**

## Summary: Why iBio Is Genuinely Risky

iBio is a **microcap, preclinical-stage (Phase-1-initiating), in-licensed-asset, 20-person, capital-markets-dependent biotech** that has already destroyed $332M of cumulative capital and executed two reverse stock splits in four years.

Its lead asset is competitive with Regeneron, Scholar Rock, and Biohaven — all of whom have larger teams, more capital, and earlier clinical data. Its differentiation claim (long half-life → 2-4x/year dosing) is based on **non-human-primate pharmacokinetic data** that historically translates poorly to humans.

The stock trades at essentially net cash (~$2.16 NCPS vs. $2.22 price), meaning the market is pricing the pipeline at **roughly zero**. This is either a glaring buying opportunity or a rational reflection of the probability-weighted outcome.

**The bear case is not cynicism — it is the base-rate expectation for preclinical microcap biotechs.** Per published data, approximately **85-90% of Phase 1 candidates fail to reach approval**. Combined with iBio's specific structural weaknesses (in-licensed assets, dilution overhang, sub-scale position, dependency on capital markets), the base rate for permanent impairment or severe underperformance is meaningfully higher than for typical small-cap biotechs.

If you own IBIO, you are making an **optionality bet**, not an investment. Size it accordingly.
