# IBIO Investment Memo

**iBio, Inc. (NASDAQ: IBIO) | April 14, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

iBio is a **20-person preclinical biotech in San Diego** developing AI-designed antibodies for obesity and cardiometabolic disease. Its lead asset **IBIO-600** (anti-myostatin) just received Australia TGA clearance to begin a Phase 1 trial — the first clinical study in the company's history. The thesis: engineered ultra-long-half-life antibodies (2-4x/year dosing) given alongside GLP-1s to preserve muscle mass and prevent weight rebound. Market cap **$76.5M** at net cash; zero product revenue; $332M accumulated deficit across a history of serial dilution and two reverse stock splits.

---

## What's Going Right

**IBIO-600 cleared to Phase 1 in Australia (April 8, 2026).** First patient dosing expected Q2 2026. The company has officially transitioned from preclinical to clinical-stage — the first such milestone in its 15+ year public history. *(External — not in 10-K.)*

**Balance sheet materially strengthened.** August 2025 Leerink offering raised $46.5M net; January 2026 PIPE (led by healthcare-specialist Frazier Life Sciences) added another $24.4M net. Pro-forma cash **~$77M**; runway into Q3 FY2028 (~March 2028). Going concern warning alleviated in FY2025 10-K.

**Credible scientific leadership.** CEO/CSO Dr. Martin Brenner has strong biopharma pedigree (Merck endocrinology, Pfenex CSO — sold to Ligand for $516M; AstraZeneca, Eli Lilly, Recursion). The pivot to obesity/cardiometabolic aligns directly with his Merck diabetes/NASH background.

**Differentiated mechanism thesis.** IBIO-610 (anti-Activin E, INHBE) is first-in-class, distinct from Regeneron's activin A approach. Preclinical DIO mouse data: combo with semaglutide produced 35.3% weight loss (vs. 27.8% semaglutide alone), with lean-mass preservation and **prevention of post-semaglutide rebound weight gain** — a genuine commercial differentiator if it holds in humans.

**Analyst sell-side support.** 4 covering analysts (Chardan, Jones Trading, Oppenheimer, Leerink), average target $4.75 (+114% vs. $2.22). (Caveat: these are capital-markets-linked shops.)

**Healthcare-specialist institutional backing.** Frazier, EcoR1, Logos, Affinity — all respected healthcare funds — hold meaningful positions.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Phase 1 safety or PK failure is a thesis-killer.** Allometric scaling from NHPs to humans is notoriously unreliable. If IBIO-600's actual human half-life comes in at 10-30 days vs. predicted 57-147, the 2-4x/year dosing differentiation collapses and IBIO-600 becomes undifferentiated vs. Regeneron's trevogrumab.

**In-licensed lead assets.** Both IBIO-600 and IBIO-610 are licensed from AstralBio — iBio does not own composition-of-matter IP. Up to $28M in milestones per asset owed to AstralBio; any license termination strips iBio of its clinical pipeline.

**Massive dilution overhang.** August 2025 offering added **~143M potential shares** (71.5M pre-funded warrants + 71.5M Series G/H warrants @ $0.70 strike). If the stock rises above $0.70 — well below current price — warrants flood the float. Fully diluted share count could reach **~195M+** vs. current 34.5M outstanding.

**Sub-scale in a mega-cap competitive space.** Direct peers Regeneron ($50B+), Scholar Rock ($5.7B), Biohaven ($1.4B) all have larger teams, more capital, more advanced clinical data. iBio is ~50-500x smaller than its direct competitors.

**Capital-allocation track record is poor.** Bryan, TX CDMO facility purchased in 2021 for $28.75M, sold in 2024 for $8.5M — ~$20M+ destroyed. Two reverse stock splits (1:25 in 2022, 1:20 in 2023) required by serial mass dilution. History of missed guidance (IBIO-101 IND slipped; CDMO sale timing slipped).

**Accelerating burn rate.** Q2 FY2026 quarterly burn ~$9M vs. $1.5M/month in FY2024 — quintuple. R&D doubled YoY. Runway estimate assumes a pace that is already lagging reality.

## Decision

**Current stance: Speculative — Watchlist Only**

At $2.22 (essentially net cash per share), iBio offers a genuine asymmetric option on IBIO-600 Phase 1 data. The probability-weighted fair value is only marginally above current price, but the distribution is heavily right-skewed: a clean Phase 1 + partnership deal produces ~$5-6/share; a safety signal produces <$1/share.

The investor is being asked to believe four things:
1. IBIO-600 will produce clean Phase 1 safety data (late 2026 / early 2027)
2. Human PK will confirm the 57-147 day half-life prediction from NHP
3. Big Pharma will value the differentiation enough to strike a meaningful partnership deal
4. Capital structure will hold — no third reverse split, no emergency raise at sub-$1 prices

**The thesis becomes compelling only below net cash (~$2.00/share)** — where the option is free and the downside is backstopped by cash. At current $2.22, the $0.20 premium over net cash is arguably a fair price for the option, but offers no margin of safety.

**The thesis becomes uninvestable if** Phase 1 dosing slips past Q3 2026, burn rate exceeds $10-12M/quarter, or Biohaven/Regeneron class data (due 2H 2026) disappoints.

**Size any position as venture-capital-style optionality (≤0.5% of portfolio), not as a long-term investment.**
