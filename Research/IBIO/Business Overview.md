## What iBio Is Today

iBio is a **preclinical-stage biotechnology company** developing antibody therapeutics for **obesity and cardiometabolic disease** using an in-house AI-driven antibody discovery platform.

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Overview]: "iBio, Inc... is a preclinical stage biotechnology company leveraging the power of Artificial Intelligence ('AI') for the development of hard-to-drug precision antibodies in the cardiometabolic and obesity space. Our core mission is to harness the potential of AI and machine learning ('ML') to unveil novel biologics which other scientists have been unable to develop."

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Overview]: "We anticipate the commencement of our first human clinical trials in late fiscal 2026 or early fiscal 2027."

**Positioning:** Not a primary GLP-1 competitor. iBio's thesis is that the current wave of GLP-1 therapies (semaglutide, tirzepatide) drive weight loss via muscle loss as well as fat loss, and leave patients with rebound weight gain after discontinuation. iBio is developing **adjunct biologics** designed to preserve lean muscle and/or prevent fat regain, given alongside or after GLP-1s.

## Pipeline

### Primary (Obesity / Cardiometabolic) — Active Development

**IBIO-600 — Anti-myostatin (GDF8) long-acting monoclonal antibody**

- **Stage (FY25 10-K):** IND-enabling; GLP tox batch manufactured at 200L scale
- **Stage (post-filing, Apr 2026):** **Phase 1 cleared by Australia TGA April 8, 2026**; first patient dosing expected Q2 2026 *(Note: external source, not in 10-K)*
- **Mechanism:** Inhibits myostatin (muscle growth inhibitor) to preserve lean mass during GLP-1-induced weight loss
- **Differentiation:** Engineered half-life of 40-52 days in NHPs, with allometric prediction of 57-147 days in humans → **potential 2-4x/year dosing**
- **Licensed from:** AstralBio (Myostatin License Agreement, December 31, 2024) — up to $28M in milestones + low-to-mid single-digit % sublicense fees

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Pipeline]: "Standard PK calculations indicated the half-life of IBIO-600 in NHPs was approximately 40 to 52 days. By using multiple allometric scaling approaches, we estimated the half-life in humans of IBIO-600 as falling with a range of 57-147 days."

**IBIO-610 — First-in-class anti-Activin E antibody**

- **Stage:** Development candidate selected; IND-enabling studies initiated; first-in-human targeted early calendar 2027 *(external)*
- **Mechanism:** Inhibits Activin E (INHBE), a metabolic regulator. Human genetics show INHBE loss-of-function carriers have reduced visceral fat and better lipid profiles
- **Preclinical data (DIO mouse):** 8.9% body weight reduction as monotherapy; 35.3% weight loss in combo with semaglutide (vs. 27.8% semaglutide alone); **preserved lean mass and prevented rebound weight gain after semaglutide discontinuation**
- **Licensed from:** AstralBio (Activin E License Agreement, April 21, 2025) — up to $28M in milestones

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Pipeline]: "mice receiving IBIO-610 at the time of semaglutide discontinuation regained only 28% of the lost weight and retained significantly lower fat mass at study termination, highlighting the potential of IBIO-610 to prevent rebound weight gain."

**Myostatin × Activin A Bispecific Antibody**

- **Stage:** Late discovery / in vitro proof of concept
- **Mechanism:** Dual inhibition of GDF8 + Activin A for enhanced muscle differentiation

**Amylin Receptor Agonist Antibody (AstralBio collaboration)**

- **Stage:** In vivo proof of concept achieved (2025) — ~60% reduction in acute food intake in obese mice
- **Note:** Program owned by AstralBio; iBio holds an exclusive option

## Technology Platform ("AI Drug Discovery Platform")

Four layers per FY2025 10-K:

1. **AI Epitope Steering (patented)** — designs synthetic "engineered epitopes" to raise antibodies against specific target regions with defined mode of action.
2. **Naive Human Antibody Library** — proprietary library built from public CDR data, filtered for liabilities.
3. **StableHu™** — AI-guided antibody optimization using mammalian display, trained on ~1B human antibody sequences.
4. **Functional Panels:**
   - **EngageTx™** — CD3 T-cell engager panel (NHP cross-reactive, low cytokine release)
   - **ShieldTx™** — antibody masking technology for conditionally activated antibodies

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Platform]: "When combined with our antibody optimization platform, which deeply integrates generative AI tools with mammalian display technology, we can progress from concept to development-ready antibody in as little as seven months."

## Dependencies & Key-Person Risk

- **AstralBio dependency:** Both lead clinical candidates (IBIO-600, IBIO-610) were discovered by and are in-licensed from AstralBio. Termination of these licenses would strip iBio of its primary clinical-stage assets.
- **Key personnel:** Dr. Martin Brenner is both CEO and CSO — dual-role concentration.
- **Capital markets:** With ~$0.4M revenue vs. ~$38M+ annualized gross cash burn, iBio's survival depends entirely on continued equity financing.
- **CDMO counterparties:** No internal manufacturing. Dependent on Lonza and other third-party manufacturers.

> [IBIO_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors Summary]: "Our most advanced product candidates depend on intellectual property licensed from third parties and termination of any of these or future licenses could result in the loss of significant rights."

## The 30-Second Summary

iBio is a 20-person, San Diego-based preclinical antibody company that has **twice-pivoted** its business model in four years. Its sole clinical-stage-adjacent assets — IBIO-600 (anti-myostatin) and IBIO-610 (anti-activin E) — are both **in-licensed from AstralBio** and aimed at preserving muscle mass in GLP-1-treated patients. IBIO-600 received Australian TGA Phase 1 clearance in April 2026 (external), transitioning iBio to clinical-stage. The company has no commercial revenue, a $332M accumulated deficit, and is entirely dependent on capital markets. Its commercial thesis is that AI-designed, ultra-long-half-life antibodies given 2-4x per year can become standard-of-care adjuncts to the $130B+ GLP-1 obesity market.
