### Revenue drivers (FY2025)

- **PAL added $5.4M** (first full year post-October 2024 acquisition)
- **DAVD grew to $3.7M from $1.2M** (+208%)
- **Echoscope equipment sales +30.5%**
- **Rentals -62.2%** (offshore wind pullback)
- Organic (ex-PAL) growth: ~4-5%

The reported +30.7% revenue growth is **mostly acquisition-driven**. True organic growth is modest.

### Gross margin decline explained

FY2025 gross margin fell 330 bps from 69.8% to 66.5% due to PAL (58.6% gross margin) entering the mix. On a segment basis:
- **Marine Products: 74.5% gross margin** (up from 69.8% pure-Products mix)
- **PAL: 58.6%**
- **Services: 58.6%**

Underlying Marine Products margin actually expanded.

## Accounting Flags

### 1. Revenue growth is mostly acquisition-driven
+30.7% headline growth, but ex-PAL organic growth was only ~4-5%. Deceleration vs. FY2022-2024 growth on organic basis. Investors should evaluate PAL acquisition accretion separately.

### 2. Inventory is a significant working capital use
$13.6M inventory on $8.9M COGS = ~560 days of inventory. This is normal for small-batch custom manufacturing but ties up substantial capital. Any demand weakness could force inventory writedowns.

### 3. Unbilled receivables growing rapidly
$3.0M unbilled receivables (+80% YoY) reflects percentage-of-completion accounting on Services contracts. Not a red flag but means revenue recognition leads cash collection.

### 4. No formal backlog disclosure
Unusual for a defense-oriented company. Management references "programs of record" qualitatively but provides no quantified backlog. Makes forward visibility difficult to assess.

### 5. Customer concentration in Services segment (structural)
Services segment derives "20% to 30% of consolidated revenues" from Raytheon and Northrop combined. Loss of either program would be material.

### 6. Currency exposure un-hedged
66.6% foreign revenue + no hedging = reported USD results are volatile on FX swings. Strengthening USD would reduce FY2026 reported results.

### 7. DAVD revenue is "lumpy and back-ended"
Management explicitly flagged Q3-Q4 FY2026 concentration. Quarterly beats/misses will swing materially on Navy procurement timing.
