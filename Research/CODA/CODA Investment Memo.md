# CODA Investment Memo

**Coda Octopus Group (NASDAQ: CODA) | April 12, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

CODA is a $138M market-cap micro-cap underwater technology company operating three segments: Marine Technology Products (real-time 3D volumetric Echoscope sonar — $13M, 74.5% gross margin), Acoustic Sensors & Materials (PAL, acquired Oct 2024 — $5M), and Defense Engineering Services (Colmek + Martech sub-assemblies to Raytheon CIWS and Northrop AQS-24 programs — $8M). Also produces the Diver Augmented Vision Display (DAVD) — a heads-up display for US Navy divers funded by the Office of Naval Research. Debt-free with $28.7M cash, fiscal year ends October 31.

---

## What's Going Right

**Unique technology with no known direct competitor.** Per the company: "In the field of Real Time 3D/4D/5D imaging, we are unaware of other companies offering a similar product." 25+ years of R&D investment, ~21 US patents, multi-generational product (3G → 4G → PIPE® 5D/6D).

**DAVD program with US Navy scaling.** Revenue grew from $1.2M to $3.7M (+208%) in FY2025. Operational at 9 Navy commands. 16 Untethered Systems ordered for SOCOM in March 2025. European Navy trial underway Q2 FY2026. ANU completion expected Q2 FY2026 for untethered variant.

**Defense sole-source positions.** Colmek is the sole-source supplier of parts for Raytheon's CIWS and Northrop Grumman's AQS-24 — programs running 40+ years. Recurring, long-tail revenue.

**Fortress balance sheet.** $28.7M cash (21% of market cap), $0 long-term debt, 8.9x current ratio, $4M undrawn revolver. M&A firepower + resilience to macro shocks.

**Improving customer concentration.** 0 customers >10% of revenue in FY2025, down from 2 customers at 22.9% in FY2023. PAL acquisition diversified the customer base.

**Echoscope NANO Gen positioning for UUV sensors.** DoD FY2026 autonomy budget is $5.3B, $2.2B above FY2025. CODA's ultra-compact 3D sonar targets UUV navigation/obstacle avoidance — a growing defense priority.

**Revenue accelerating.** FY2025 +30.7% (partly PAL); Q1 FY2026 +28.8%. Q2 FY2026 guidance +20% YoY.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Scale disadvantage is extreme.** CODA's $2.45M R&D budget vs. Teledyne's (~$6B revenue parent) hundreds of millions. Kongsberg, Thales also dwarf CODA. If 3D sonar niche proves valuable, larger competitors can outspend or acquire.

**Extreme illiquidity.** Float of only 6.01M shares, insider ownership 46.72%, 1 analyst covering. Any institutional position movement distorts the price.

**Organic growth is modest.** FY2025 +30.7% headline growth was mostly PAL acquisition. Ex-PAL organic growth was only ~4-5%. Products segment grew 3.2% YoY.

**Governance concerns.** Combined CEO + Chairman role (Annmarie Gayle, 14 years). No Lead Independent Director disclosed. No dividend or meaningful buybacks despite $28.7M cash.

**Insider selling.** Niels Sondergaard (largest shareholder, 18%) sold 150,000 shares at ~$8.15 in October 2025. CFO sold $28K in April 2026.

**Revenue is lumpy.** DAVD explicitly "lumpy and back-ended." Customer concentration in Services (Raytheon + Northrop = 20-30%). Quarterly results erratic.

**Premium valuation.** 33x P/E, 17x EV/EBITDA, 4.9x P/S for a $27M revenue company. Any execution miss compresses multiple quickly.

**US offshore wind pullback.** Rental revenue down 62% in FY2025. Structural shift under current administration, not cyclical.

## Decision

**Current stance: Speculative interest only.**

CODA has **genuinely differentiated technology** and a real defense franchise, but it's a tiny company ($27M revenue, $138M market cap, 6M share float) competing in a niche that could be targeted by giants like Teledyne (which has been serially acquiring marine tech companies including BlueView, Reson, Blueprint Subsea, Valeport).

The investment case rests on three things:
1. **DAVD continues scaling** — from $3.7M toward $8-15M over 3 years
2. **Echoscope NANO Gen captures UUV sensor share** — riding the $5.3B DoD autonomy budget
3. **Possible strategic acquisition** — Teledyne or Kongsberg may pay a premium

Against these optimistic scenarios, the risks are substantial: extreme illiquidity, governance concerns (combined CEO/Chairman), premium valuation (33x P/E), lumpy revenue, competitive vulnerability.

**For whom this works:**
- Patient investors (3-5 year horizon) with micro-cap conviction
- Defense/technology specialists comfortable with illiquidity
- Small position sizing (<2-3% of portfolio)
- Willingness to accept binary catalyst risk (DAVD ANU, M&A)

**For whom this doesn't work:**
- Mainstream long-only institutional investors (size constraints)
- Income-focused portfolios (no dividend)
- Investors uncomfortable with governance concerns
- Anyone unwilling to accept -67% downside scenario

**Key triggers to watch:**
- DAVD Untethered ANU completion (Q2 FY2026)
- Next acquisition announcement (size, price, strategic fit)
- Any M&A interest signals from Teledyne/Kongsberg
- DAVD deployment expansion beyond current 9 Navy commands
- Insider buying by CEO or Chairman (would be positive signal)

**Key risks to monitor:**
- Sondergaard continued selling
- Organic growth (ex-PAL) turning negative
- Customer concentration re-emerging
- Competitive product launch by Teledyne or Kongsberg
- Valuation compression toward peer industrial average
