## 2. Valuation Context

These multiples are **premium for a $27M revenue company**. The premium is partly justified by:
- **Fortress balance sheet** — $28.7M cash (EV/Revenue drops to ~3x excluding cash)
- **66%+ gross margins** — software/IP-like economics rather than hardware
- **Unique technology** — no direct competitor in real-time 3D sonar
- **Defense growth narrative** — DAVD deployment + UUV sensors
- **Zero debt** — no refinancing risk

On an EV basis (stripping out cash), CODA trades at roughly **18-19x EV/EBITDA** — still premium but more defensible.

## 7. Acquisition Value

A strategic acquisition analysis may be more relevant than intrinsic valuation for CODA:

**Teledyne's recent marine acquisitions:**
- BlueView Technologies: ~$150M estimated
- Reson: estimated $75-100M
- Blueprint Subsea: undisclosed
- Valeport: undisclosed

A Teledyne acquisition of CODA could easily price at 3-4x EV/Revenue = **$80-108M EV + cash = $110-140M market cap** — roughly in line with current trading price. So the acquisition premium is partially already embedded.

A strategic buyer paying a real premium (5x EV/Revenue = $135M EV + $30M cash = $165M market cap = ~$15/share) would offer ~25% premium.

## 8. Key Valuation Considerations

1. **Cash backing is substantial.** $28.7M cash = $2.54/share = 21% of stock price. EV-based multiples (17x EV/EBITDA) are more meaningful than market cap multiples (33x P/E).

2. **Premium multiple requires execution.** At 33x P/E, CODA needs to continue growing EPS 25%+ annually to justify the price. Recent growth is acquisition-driven; organic growth is modest.

3. **Thin float amplifies volatility.** 6M share float + 46% insider ownership means the stock can move 5-10% on modest volume. Not suitable for large positions.

4. **Acquisition optionality is real.** Teledyne/Kongsberg/Thales are credible acquirers. Strategic value may exceed standalone value.

5. **Dividend isn't a valuation support.** Unlike VICI or STRA, no dividend means investors rely entirely on EPS growth or acquisition for returns.

**The valuation is not cheap.** It requires belief in DAVD scaling, UUV sensor adoption, or an acquisition at some point. For patient, risk-tolerant investors with conviction in defense technology, it can work. For most investors, the 33x P/E + illiquidity + governance concerns make this a difficult holding.
