# Coda Octopus Group (CODA) — Industry Scan

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2024 10-K, external industry research*

---

## 1. Underwater Sonar Market

**Global underwater sonar market:** ~$4.3-5.3B (2025), projected to ~$7.1B by 2033 at ~6.5-7.1% CAGR.

**Global sonar systems (broader):** ~$6.15B in 2025/2026, projected ~$6.5B by 2030 (4.4% CAGR).

**Underwater navigation & positioning sub-segment:** $1.82B, dominated by Thales, Kongsberg, General Dynamics.

*(Note: market sizes from Grand View Research, Fortune Business Insights, MarketsandMarkets — not from uploaded filings)*

### Growth drivers

- **Naval expansion globally** — Asia-Pacific fastest-growing (7.6% CAGR)
- **UUV proliferation** requiring onboard sonar sensors
- **Offshore oil & gas recovery** — subsea infrastructure inspection
- **Subsea cable laying/inspection** — internet cables, offshore wind
- **Autonomous vehicles** across defense and commercial

### CODA's niche positioning

CODA operates in **real-time 3D/4D/5D/6D volumetric imaging sonar** — a differentiated sub-segment distinct from traditional 2D scanning and multibeam sonar. Per CODA's filing, **no direct competitor** in this niche.

## 4. Offshore Wind Market

**Status:** US offshore wind in retreat under current administration. EU/UK/Asia offshore wind continues.

### Impact on CODA

- US rental revenue declined 62% in FY2025
- Offshore wind cable burial/inspection was major Echoscope rental driver 2022-2024
- Management position: "transition to O&G will in the foreseeable future offset any possible reduction of revenue from US offshore renewables"

### Partial offsets

- International offshore wind projects continue (Europe, Asia, Australia)
- Oil & gas infrastructure inspection demand rising as majors resume offshore CAPEX
- Echoscope is product-agnostic — serves whatever subsea application needs imaging

## 5. UUV (Unmanned Underwater Vehicle) Market

**Growth vector:** Proliferation of UUVs creates demand for compact, high-performance sensor packages.

**CODA's position:** Echoscope PIPE NANO Gen Series (launched September 2025) specifically designed as "perception sensor for UUV navigation, obstacle avoidance, and target guidance."

**Competitors in UUV sonar:** Teledyne, Kongsberg, Sonardyne, Blueprint Subsea (acquired by Teledyne in 2023).

The UUV sensor market is where CODA's growth thesis runs alongside the DAVD thesis. Both require multi-year Navy/DoD procurement cycles.

## 6. Regulatory Environment

### Export controls

> "Our real time 3D sonars which are depth rated above 300 meters along with our inertial navigation and attitude measurement sensors (F280® series) are subject to export control for certain countries, including China."

China export restrictions limit CODA's addressable market in the largest naval expansion region.

### Brexit impact

UK operations (Edinburgh, Portland, Higher Bockhampton) face ongoing Brexit-related complications:

[CODA_10K_FY2024.htm]:
> "We do not qualify for work permits in the EU member states and therefore cannot support our EU customers as we did prior to Brexit."

This led to establishing Danish subsidiary (Coda Octopus Products A/S) in 2017 as Brexit mitigation.

### ITAR / defense export

Defense products subject to US International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR). Creates compliance burden but also a barrier to entry.

---

## Key Takeaway

CODA operates in a **specialized niche** (real-time 3D/4D sonar, DAVD) within the broader underwater sonar market, which is growing 6-7% annually. Defense spending tailwinds from UUV proliferation and autonomous systems are favorable. The biggest industry risk is **competitive displacement by larger players** — Teledyne's acquisition spree in marine technology creates both acquisition optionality (CODA could be bought) and competitive risk (Teledyne could invest to replicate CODA's technology). The industry structure supports multiple players at scale (Kongsberg, Teledyne, Thales) but niches like real-time 3D imaging may support only one or two specialized providers.
