# Coda Octopus Group (CODA) — Bear Case

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2025 10-K/A, external data*

---

## 1. Thesis Killer: Small Scale in a World of Giants

CODA generates **$27M in revenue** competing against:
- **Teledyne Technologies** (~$6B revenue, multi-segment conglomerate, serial acquirer of marine technology companies)
- **Kongsberg Maritime** (division of ~$3B+ Kongsberg Gruppen)
- **Thales Group** (€18-20B revenue, naval defense leader)
- **BAE, Raytheon, Northrop** (multi-hundred-billion defense primes — both customers AND potential competitors)

CODA's entire R&D budget is $2.45M. A single Teledyne engineer earns more than CODA's entire acquisition budget for a small company. In the long run, can CODA's technological lead persist against these resources?

[CODA_10K_FY2024.htm, Item 1]:
> "Teledyne has much greater resources, liquidity and market reach than our Company."

The bear argument: CODA's real-time 3D sonar lead is real but temporary. Once Teledyne or Kongsberg decides the market is large enough, they can either acquire CODA or outspend to replicate the technology.

---

## 2. Structural Weaknesses

### 2a. Extreme illiquidity and thin float

- **Shares outstanding: 11.28M**
- **Float: only 6.01M shares**
- **Insider ownership: 46.72%** — three individuals (Sondergaard 18%, Ezralow 9.6%, Emerson 7.7%) control 35%+
- **1 analyst** (Alliance Global Partners)

Any meaningful institutional position movement distorts the price. A single insider block sale (like Sondergaard's 150K shares in Oct 2025) can crater the stock.

### 2b. Revenue is highly lumpy and contract-dependent

DAVD revenue explicitly "lumpy and back-ended." Services segment contracts are long-cycle. Customer concentration in Services (Raytheon + Northrop = 20-30%).

One missed defense contract = 10%+ revenue impact. Quarterly results are erratic.

### 2c. Organic growth is modest

FY2025 headline growth was +30.7%, but ~25% came from PAL acquisition. **Ex-PAL organic growth was only ~4-5%.** Products segment revenue grew 3.2% YoY. This is a modestly growing company, not a high-growth story.

### 2d. Governance concerns

- **Combined CEO + Chairman** role (Annmarie Gayle)
- **No Lead Independent Director** disclosed
- **Low compensation** ($405K for CEO) is flagged as "below median" — may signal difficulty attracting talent
- **Insider selling** — Sondergaard reduced 18% to ~16% in Oct 2025; CFO sold $28K in April 2026

### 2e. Currency exposure un-hedged

66.6% foreign revenue, no hedging. USD strength materially hurts reported results. GBP weakness in 2024-2025 was a tailwind; reversal would hurt.

### 2f. No backlog disclosure

Unusual for defense-oriented company. Lack of forward visibility makes equity valuation difficult.

### 2g. US offshore wind exposure is structural

Not temporary — current administration's energy policy shift is likely to persist through 2028. Rental revenue down 62% in FY2025.

### 2h. Valuation is not cheap

**P/E 33x, EV/EBITDA 17x, P/S 4.9x** — premium multiples for a $27M revenue company. The valuation requires execution on DAVD and NanoGen catalysts that aren't guaranteed.

---

## 3. Assumptions the Bull Case Takes for Granted

1. **DAVD scales to $10-15M.** Bull assumes growing adoption across Navy commands + SOCOM + international. Bear: DAVD revenue is contract-cycle-dependent; ANU delays or budget constraints push revenue out indefinitely.

2. **Echoscope NANO Gen becomes UUV sensor standard.** Bull assumes CODA captures meaningful share of UUV proliferation. Bear: Teledyne, Kongsberg, Sonardyne all target UUV sensors; CODA's relatively small R&D budget limits competitive response capacity.

3. **Next acquisition is accretive.** Management telegraphed intent to do another deal. PAL was acquired for $6.5M at ~$4M revenue (1.6x revenue). Next deal could be overpriced or dilutive.

4. **Defense spending tailwinds persist.** Ukraine war, Taiwan tensions, Navy expansion budgets. But DoD CRs and program cancellations have hurt CODA before.

5. **No acquisition by Teledyne/Kongsberg.** Bull sees CODA as accretive target, but Teledyne has preferred acquiring smaller, more specialized players. CODA may or may not be attractive acquisition target.

---

## 4. Permanent Impairment Scenarios

### Teledyne or Kongsberg develops competing real-time 3D sonar
If a well-capitalized competitor invests $50-100M in R&D over 5 years, CODA's technology lead could evaporate. Echoscope revenue goes to zero over a decade.

### DAVD program cancellation
If US Navy pulls back DAVD funding (budget pressure, program realignment), CODA loses its highest-visibility growth catalyst. Stock likely re-rates to book value of ~$5.

### Key customer loss in Services segment
Loss of Raytheon CIWS or Northrop AQS-24 program = 10-20% revenue decline overnight. Services segment margins collapse.

### Dilutive acquisition
If management overpays for the "next acquisition" (they've signaled intent), could destroy value. Current $28.7M cash could become $10M cash + $20M in goodwill on a questionable business.

### Insider block sales
Sondergaard still holds ~16%. If he continues selling, the thin float means stock pressure is material.

## Summary

CODA operates a genuinely interesting technology (real-time 3D sonar, DAVD) in a protected niche — but at $27M revenue and $138M market cap, it's a tiny company relying on the continued goodwill of US Navy procurement officers and the continued technological lead against well-capitalized competitors. The valuation (33x P/E) prices in significant execution. **The single biggest risk is that this is a good technology inside a too-small company** — vulnerable to larger competitors either acquiring CODA or outspending its modest R&D budget over the next 5-10 years. For patient investors with conviction in DAVD and UUV sensor opportunities, the risk/reward can work. For most investors, the illiquidity, thin float, governance concerns, and competitive vulnerability make this an uncomfortable long-term holding.
