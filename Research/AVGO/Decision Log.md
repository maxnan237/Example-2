# Broadcom Inc. (AVGO) — Decision Log

*Initiated: 2026-04-08 | Analyst coverage based on FY2025 10-K and supporting documents*

---

## Investment Thesis Summary

Broadcom is a $64B-revenue technology conglomerate operating at the intersection of AI-driven semiconductor growth and enterprise infrastructure software. The business generates extraordinary cash flows (42% FCF margin) through a combination of high-margin custom silicon design wins and a deeply embedded software franchise anchored by VMware.

**Bull thesis:** AI custom silicon + VMware subscription conversion = durable double-digit revenue growth at expanding margins. The design-in model creates multi-year revenue visibility, and the capital-light structure converts revenue to FCF at rates unmatched by peers.

**Bear thesis:** 32% customer concentration + 95% TSMC dependency + 57% goodwill-as-assets = a business more fragile than its margins suggest. The AI capex cycle is 3+ years old, the company's own filing warns it "may not be sustainable," and the valuation leaves limited margin of safety.

## Open Questions

1. **Who is the 32% customer?** Not named in filings. If this is a single hyperscaler's AI program, what happens when their next-gen chip design is complete — does Broadcom win the follow-on, or does the customer in-source?

2. **What is the true AI revenue figure?** The 10-K does not break out AI-specific revenue. Earnings calls reportedly disclose this (~$5.2B in Q3 FY2025). Need earnings transcripts to track.

3. **How much VMware churn is actually happening?** The filing shows 90%+ of top 10K customers adopted VCF, but mid-market and SMB behavior is undisclosed. Contract liabilities declining $1.5B YoY could be a signal.

4. **What is the normalized tax rate post-global minimum tax?** Management warns of "material" increase in FY2026. Cash taxes of $2.6B (FY2025) on $22.7B pre-tax income = ~11.4%. If this normalizes to 15-18%, FCF drops $1-3B/year.

5. **What is Broadcom's next acquisition target?** At 1.5x net debt/EBITDA and declining leverage, Broadcom will likely pursue another large deal within 2-3 years. The target and price will matter enormously.

## Decision Framework

**To become a buyer at current prices (~$350), I would need:**
- Evidence that AI capex cycle has 3+ more years of 15%+ growth
- VMware contract liabilities stabilizing or growing (reversing the $1.5B YoY decline)
- Normalized tax rate confirmed at <15%
- AR factoring volume not increasing further (stabilizing at ~$7-8B)
- Succession planning visibility (named internal candidate or COO appointment)

**To become a seller (if already owned), I would need:**
- Top customer revenue declining sequentially for 2+ quarters
- Software segment margin falling below 70%
- New large acquisition at >3x net debt/EBITDA leverage
- Taiwan geopolitical escalation beyond current tensions
- Goodwill impairment charge of any size

**Current stance:** Monitor. The business is excellent but the valuation offers limited margin of safety. Wait for either a price correction (entry below ~$250 for a more attractive risk/reward) or evidence that the bear case triggers are materializing.
