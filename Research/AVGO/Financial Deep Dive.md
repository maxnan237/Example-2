## 1. Revenue Trajectory

### Five-Year Overview

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Consolidated Statements of Operations] and [AVGO_10K_FY2023.htm, Consolidated Statements of Operations]:

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Total Revenue** | **$63,887M** | **$51,574M** | **$35,819M** | **$33,203M** | **$27,450M** |
| YoY Growth | +24% | +44% | +8% | +21% | — |
| Products | $44,847M | $34,960M | $28,949M | $26,277M | $20,886M |
| Subscriptions & Services | $19,040M | $16,614M | $6,870M | $6,926M | $6,564M |

Revenue more than doubled in four years ($27.5B to $63.9B). This growth is a mix of **organic semiconductor expansion** and **inorganic software growth** (VMware acquisition closed Nov 22, 2023).

### Organic vs. Inorganic

**Semiconductor solutions** (largely organic):

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Segment Information]:

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | Growth FY23→25 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Semi Revenue | $36,858M | $30,096M | $28,182M | +31% |

> "Net revenue from our semiconductor solutions segment increased due to strong demand for our networking solutions, primarily custom AI accelerators and AI networking products."

**Infrastructure software** (VMware-driven step-change):

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | Growth FY23→25 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Software Revenue | $27,029M | $21,478M | $7,637M | +254% |

FY2023 software revenue of $7.6B reflected the pre-VMware base (CA, Symantec, Brocade). The FY2024 surge to $21.5B (+181%) was the first full year of VMware. FY2025 growth of +26% reflects the VMware subscription transition and VCF adoption.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "Net revenue from our infrastructure software segment increased primarily due to strong demand for our VCF product, including license revenue recognized on contracts where customers do not have the right to terminate and the transition to a subscription license model."

### Revenue reclassification note

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "In fiscal year 2025, we included upfront license revenue of $7,800 million within products revenue. To conform to the current year presentation, we reclassified $4,601 million of upfront license revenue from subscriptions and services revenue to products revenue for fiscal year 2024."

Upfront licenses: $7,800M (FY25), $4,601M (FY24), $1,058M (FY23). This rapid growth reflects VMware customers signing contracts without termination-for-convenience clauses — a bullish signal for customer commitment.

## 2. Margin Analysis

### Gross Margin

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm and AVGO_10K_FY2023.htm, Consolidated Statements of Operations]:

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gross Profit | $43,294M | $32,509M | $24,690M | $22,095M | $16,844M |
| **GAAP Gross Margin** | **67.8%** | **63.0%** | **68.9%** | **66.5%** | **61.4%** |
| Acq. Amort in COGS | $6,031M | $6,023M | $1,853M | $2,847M | $3,427M |
| **Adjusted Gross Margin** | **77.2%** | **74.7%** | **74.1%** | **75.1%** | **73.9%** |

The FY2024 GAAP gross margin dip to 63% was driven by $6B in VMware-related amortization hitting COGS for the first time. The recovery to 67.8% in FY2025 reflects the amortization base stabilizing while revenue grew. Adjusted gross margin (excluding acquisition amortization) has expanded steadily from 73.9% to 77.2%.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "Gross margin was $43,294 million for fiscal year 2025 [...] As a percentage of net revenue, gross margin was 68% and 63% of net revenue for the fiscal years 2025 and 2024, respectively."

### Segment Operating Margins

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Segment Information]:

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Semi Solutions Op Margin | **57.6%** | **55.7%** | **58.5%** |
| Infra Software Op Margin | **76.8%** | **65.1%** | **73.8%** |

Semi margins are stable at 56-59%. Software margins expanded sharply from 65% to 77% as Broadcom executed its post-VMware cost optimization playbook (headcount reduction, product rationalization).

### Consolidated Operating and Net Margins

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GAAP Operating Income | $25,484M | $13,463M | $16,207M | $14,225M | $8,519M |
| **GAAP Op Margin** | **39.9%** | **26.1%** | **45.3%** | **42.8%** | **31.0%** |
| Net Income | $23,126M | $5,895M | $14,082M | $11,495M | $6,736M |
| **Net Margin** | **36.2%** | **11.4%** | **39.3%** | **34.6%** | **24.5%** |

FY2024 margins were depressed by VMware integration: $9.3B in acquisition amortization, $5.7B in SBC (much from VMware RSU conversions), $1.8B in restructuring, and a $3.7B tax provision driven by an IP transfer. FY2025 margins recovered sharply as these one-time items subsided.

### Margin drivers to watch

The gap between segment-level margins ($42.0B combined operating income) and consolidated operating income ($25.5B) is $16.5B in unallocated corporate costs:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Segment Information]:

| Unallocated Item | FY2025 | FY2024 |
|---|---|---|
| Acquisition-related amortization | $8,062M | $9,267M |
| Stock-based compensation | $7,568M | $5,670M |
| Restructuring & other | $667M | $1,787M |
| Acquisition-related costs | $216M | $549M |

Amortization is declining ($9.3B → $8.1B) as older intangibles fully amortize. Restructuring is also dropping. But SBC is rising ($5.7B → $7.6B), which is a real economic cost that offsets some of the margin improvement.

## 3. Cash Flow Quality

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm and AVGO_10K_FY2023.htm, Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows]:

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Net Income | $23,126M | $5,895M | $14,082M | $11,495M | $6,736M |
| D&A (amort + deprec) | $8,775M | $10,010M | $3,835M | $4,984M | $6,041M |
| SBC | $7,568M | $5,741M | $2,171M | $1,533M | $1,704M |
| **Operating Cash Flow** | **$27,537M** | **$19,962M** | **$18,085M** | **$16,736M** | **$13,764M** |
| Capex | ($623M) | ($548M) | ($452M) | ($424M) | ($443M) |
| **Free Cash Flow** | **$26,914M** | **$19,414M** | **$17,633M** | **$16,312M** | **$13,321M** |
| **FCF Margin** | **42.1%** | **37.6%** | **49.2%** | **49.1%** | **48.5%** |

### FCF vs. Net Income

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FCF / Net Income | 1.16x | 3.29x | 1.25x | 1.42x | 1.98x |

FCF consistently and significantly **exceeds** net income. The primary driver: non-cash amortization of acquisition-related intangibles ($8-10B/year) dwarfs capex ($0.5-0.6B), creating a large positive cash flow adjustment. This is a structural feature of Broadcom's acquisition-heavy model — GAAP earnings are depressed by amortization of intangibles that have already been paid for.

### Capex intensity — exceptionally low

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Capex / Revenue | 1.0% | 1.1% | 1.3% | 1.3% | 1.6% |

The fabless semiconductor model and software-heavy mix drive capex intensity below 2% — far below asset-heavy peers like Intel (capex ~25% of revenue). This enables extraordinary FCF conversion.

### SBC as % of revenue — the real cost to watch

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SBC / Revenue | **11.8%** | **11.1%** | **6.1%** | **4.6%** | **6.2%** |

SBC more than tripled from $2.2B (FY2023) to $7.6B (FY2025), driven by VMware employee retention grants and the expanded workforce. At 11.8% of revenue, this is a meaningful dilution cost that overstates the cash generative quality of the business. **FCF adjusted for SBC:**

| | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 | FY2022 | FY2021 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FCF minus SBC | $19,346M | $13,673M | $15,462M | $14,779M | $11,617M |
| Adjusted FCF Margin | 30.3% | 26.5% | 43.2% | 44.5% | 42.3% |

Even after deducting SBC, Broadcom generates 30%+ FCF margins — still exceptional but notably lower than the headline 42%.

## 6. Working Capital

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Consolidated Balance Sheets]:

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trade Accounts Receivable | $7,145M | $4,416M | +62% |
| Inventory | $2,270M | $1,760M | +29% |
| Accounts Payable | $1,560M | $1,662M | -6% |
| **DSO** (AR / Revenue × 365) | **40.8 days** | **31.3 days** | Deteriorating |
| **DIO** (Inventory / Cash COGS × 365) | **57.2 days** | **50.2 days** | Increasing |
| **DPO** (AP / Cash COGS × 365) | **39.3 days** | **47.4 days** | Declining |

### Red flags

**DSO increase (+9.5 days) — but the real picture is worse.** Reported AR of $7.1B understates true receivables because Broadcom sells receivables off-balance-sheet through factoring arrangements:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Note — Accounts Receivable Factoring]:
> "Total trade accounts receivable sold under the factoring arrangements were $7,401 million, $5,900 million and $3,975 million during fiscal years 2025, 2024 and 2023, respectively."

**Adjusted DSO** (adding back factored AR): ($7,145M + $7,401M) / ($63,887M / 365) = **83 days** — more than double the reported 40.8 days. The factoring has grown 86% in two years ($4.0B → $7.4B), masking the true deterioration in collection timing.

Customer concentration compounds this:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Segment Note]:
> "One customer accounted for 44% and 18% of our net accounts receivable balance as of November 2, 2025 and November 3, 2024, respectively."

One customer represents $3.1B of the $7.1B on-balance-sheet AR (44%) — up from 18%. Combined with the $7.4B in factored receivables, Broadcom's true gross AR is ~$14.5B on $63.9B in revenue.

**Inventory build (+29%):** Inventory grew faster than revenue, from $1.76B to $2.27B. This could indicate planned builds ahead of strong demand (bullish) or slower-than-expected sell-through (bearish). In context of Broadcom's reported strong AI demand, the former interpretation is more likely.

**DPO decline (-8 days):** Broadcom is paying suppliers faster, reducing its natural working capital float. This may reflect renegotiated supplier terms or early payments to secure supply chain priority.

### Cash conversion cycle

| | FY2025 | FY2024 |
|---|---|---|
| DSO + DIO - DPO | 58.7 days | 34.1 days |

The cash conversion cycle nearly doubled, driven primarily by AR and inventory builds. While not alarming in isolation (and partly explained by revenue mix shifts), this represents ~$4.5B in incremental working capital absorption in FY2025 versus the prior year's trends.

## 7. Unit Economics

Broadcom does not disclose traditional SaaS unit economics (CAC, LTV, churn, ARPU) in its filings. However, several proxies are available:

### Software recurring revenue visibility

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Revenue Note]:
> "The remaining performance obligations under these contracts as of November 2, 2025 were approximately $33.3 billion. We expect approximately 35% of this amount to be recognized as revenue over the next 12 months."

RPO of $33.3B represents ~1.2x annual software revenue — providing reasonable forward visibility. The 35% expected within 12 months (~$11.7B) implies a ~3-year average contract duration.

### Employee attrition as retention proxy

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Human Capital]:
> "Our voluntary attrition rate in fiscal year 2025 was approximately 4.1%, which is below the technology industry benchmark."

Low employee attrition (4.1% vs. industry benchmark) suggests the organization is stable post-VMware integration.

### Revenue per employee

~33,000 employees generating $63.9B in revenue = **~$1.94M revenue per employee** — exceptionally high, reflecting the capital-light model and high-value product mix.

## Accounting Flags & Divergences

### 1. SBC inflates FCF appearance

SBC of $7.6B (11.8% of revenue) is added back to operating cash flow but represents real dilution. Headline FCF of $26.9B overstates sustainable cash generation by ~$7.6B. Always evaluate FCF after SBC when assessing capital return capacity.

### 2. Acquisition amortization obscures true earnings power

$8.1B in acquisition-related amortization depresses GAAP operating income by ~13 percentage points. This is a non-cash charge on previously-paid intangibles. Segment-level operating margins (57.6% semi, 76.8% software) better reflect ongoing economic profitability.

### 3. Tax rate is unreliable as a baseline

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "The benefit from income taxes was $397 million for fiscal year 2025 [...] The provision for income taxes was $3,748 million for fiscal year 2024, and was primarily due to the impact of a non-recurring intra-group transfer of certain IP rights to the United States."

Effective tax rates: -1.7% (FY25), 37.8% (FY24), 6.7% (FY23), 7.6% (FY22). These are volatile and not reflective of normalized taxation. Cash taxes paid were $2.6B (FY25) and $3.2B (FY24) — a more reliable indicator, implying a ~12-15% cash tax rate. Management warns this will rise:

> "We are in the process of implementing a global minimum tax, which we expect to materially increase our effective tax rate and cash tax costs for our fiscal year ending November 1, 2026."

### 4. Goodwill dominates the balance sheet

$97.8B in goodwill (57% of assets) means a write-down, while unlikely given current profitability, would be catastrophic to book value. Tangible book value is deeply negative. The balance sheet is only as strong as the earnings power of the acquired businesses.

### 5. AR concentration is extreme

One customer at 44% of AR ($3.1B) creates binary collection risk. While this likely represents a creditworthy hyperscaler, any payment dispute would create a meaningful working capital squeeze.

### 6. AR factoring flatters reported working capital

Broadcom sold $7.4B in receivables off-balance-sheet in FY2025 (up from $4.0B in FY2023). This removes AR from the balance sheet and accelerates cash collection into operating cash flow. Reported DSO of 40.8 days would be ~83 days without factoring. This does not invalidate the FCF figure (the cash is real), but it obscures the true credit extension to customers and makes year-over-year working capital trends misleading. The rapid growth in factoring volume (86% in two years) suggests increasing reliance on this mechanism.

### 7. Contract liabilities declined QoQ

Total contract liabilities fell from $14,495M (FY2024) to $13,016M (FY2025) — a $1.5B decline, driven by the long-term portion dropping from $5,100M to $3,547M. This could reflect revenue recognition outpacing new bookings, or shorter contract durations. Worth monitoring as a leading indicator of software renewal health.
