# Broadcom Inc. (AVGO) — Management Review

*Analysis date: 2026-04-08 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, 2026 Proxy Statement, external research*

## 1. Capital Allocation Track Record

### ROIC trajectory

Broadcom's ROIC has been volatile due to the acquisition-driven model:

| Year | Approx. ROIC | Context |
|---|---|---|
| 2019 | ~5% | Post-CA Technologies acquisition; invested capital expanded |
| 2021 | ~12-15% | Integration gains, semi growth |
| 2023 | ~23% | Peak pre-VMware; lean balance sheet |
| 2024 | ~8% | VMware closed; invested capital nearly tripled to ~$141B |
| 2025 | ~20% | Rapid recovery via margin expansion and revenue growth |

*(Note: ROIC estimates from external financial data sources — mlq.ai, MacroTrends — not from uploaded filings)*

The pattern is consistent: ROIC drops sharply post-acquisition as the capital base expands, then recovers as Broadcom's cost discipline drives margins. This has played out with CA Technologies ($18.9B, 2018), Symantec Enterprise ($10.7B, 2019), and now VMware ($69B, 2023).

### M&A track record — no write-downs

Not found in available filings: any goodwill impairment charges in the last 5 years. Despite $97.8B in goodwill, Broadcom has never taken a material write-down — a strong signal that acquired businesses have maintained or grown their earning power.

| Acquisition | Year | Value | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| LSI Corporation | 2014 | $6.6B | Storage/networking IP retained; drove early semi growth |
| Broadcom Corp | 2015 | $37B | Created the current company; massive networking portfolio |
| Brocade | 2017 | $5.9B | FC SAN management portfolio retained |
| CA Technologies | 2018 | $18.9B | Controversial; established mainframe software franchise |
| Symantec Enterprise | 2019 | $10.7B | Cybersecurity portfolio; integrated into software segment |
| VMware | Nov 2023 | ~$69B | Software margins from 65% → 77%; VCF adoption >90% of top customers |

Post-VMware, Broadcom divested the non-core End User Computing unit to KKR for $3.5B cash — consistent with Tan's pattern of trimming immediately post-close.

### Dividend growth

15 consecutive annual dividend increases. Split-adjusted DPS has compounded at ~13.2% annually (FY2021-FY2025): $1.44 → $1.64 → $1.84 → $2.105 → $2.360.

## 2. Insider Ownership

### CEO holdings

Hock Tan owned approximately **908,474 shares** as of January 2026 (per Form 4 filings), valued at ~$286M at that date. This represents ~0.019% of shares outstanding — low relative to founder-controlled companies but not unusual for a professional CEO at a $900B+ market cap company.

*(Note: insider ownership data from SEC Form 4 filings and external financial data — not from uploaded 10-K filings)*

### Insider transaction pattern

Per SEC Form 4 filings, Tan has executed **19 transactions over 5 years — all sells, zero open-market buys.**

| Date | Transaction | Shares | Approx. Proceeds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dec 2025 | Sell | 130,000 | ~$42M |
| Sep 2025 | Sell | 100,000 | ~$34M |
| Jun 2025 | Gift + Sell | 190,000 gifted; 40,000 sold | ~$10.5M |

Consistent selling is common for CEOs with large equity grants (96% of Tan's compensation is equity), but the absence of any open-market purchases is worth noting. Tan's economic exposure is driven by his PSU grants, not voluntary share accumulation.

## 3. Compensation Structure

### FY2025 CEO compensation

[2026 Proxy Statement]:

| Component | Amount | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| Base Salary | ~$1.2M | <1% |
| Cash Bonus | Minimal | <1% |
| PSU Equity Award | ~$202.4M | ~99% |
| **Total** | **$205.3M** | 100% |

*(Note: compensation data from 2026 Proxy Statement via Bloomberg and Seeking Alpha — not from uploaded 10-K filings)*

### PSU structure — aligned with AI execution

The September 2025 PSU award is tied to specific AI revenue targets through FY2030:
- **Threshold:** $60B cumulative AI product revenue (FY2028-FY2030) — below this, full forfeiture
- **Target:** $90B cumulative AI revenue — standard payout
- **Maximum:** $120B+ — payout triples

This structure ties ~99% of CEO pay to a specific, measurable, long-term operating metric. It is well-aligned with shareholder value creation — provided the AI thesis holds.

### Say-on-pay: 92% approval

The most recent annual meeting produced a 92% favorable vote on executive compensation, indicating broad institutional shareholder support for the incentive structure.

### Assessment

**Positives:** Nearly all pay is at-risk equity; metrics are long-term (through FY2030); PSU forfeiture at AI revenue below $60B creates genuine downside for management.

**Concerns:** The $205M total is one of the highest CEO pay packages in corporate America. While 96% is variable, the sheer magnitude — combined with all-sell insider transaction history — suggests Tan views Broadcom equity primarily as compensation to be monetized, not a personal investment conviction.

*(Note: earnings data from CNBC, Seeking Alpha — not from uploaded filings)*

**Caveat:** This run of beats is concentrated in the AI infrastructure boom (FY2023 onward). In prior semiconductor cycles, results were more mixed. The current sandbagging-and-beat pattern could reverse when AI capex moderates.

## 5. Communication Quality

### What the filing reveals

Broadcom's 10-K is notable for its **directness about risks**:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "The semiconductor industry is undergoing profound change due to the adoption and proliferation of AI and has experienced a significant upturn, which may not be sustainable."

> "Some of these AI customers may have constrained resources or capital and may be unable to pay for their required AI infrastructure."

These are unusually candid risk disclosures for a company riding an AI growth wave. The filing also explicitly discusses the deferred payment/leasing model for AI racks, customer concentration risks, and TSMC dependency — without euphemism.

### What the filing does NOT reveal

- **No named competitors** in the FY2025 10-K (removed from FY2024). This reduces transparency.
- **No segment-level customer churn or retention metrics** for the software business.
- **No AI-specific revenue breakout** in the 10-K (though this is disclosed in earnings calls).

### Earnings call style

*(Note: earnings transcripts not in uploaded filings — based on external reports)*

Hock Tan is known for brief, data-focused earnings calls with minimal promotional language. He typically provides specific financial metrics and avoids broad qualitative claims. Analysts generally characterize his communication style as forthright and disciplined.

## 6. Tenure and Succession

### Current leadership tenure

| Executive | Role | Tenure |
|---|---|---|
| **Hock E. Tan** | President & CEO | Since 2006 (Avago); CEO of Broadcom Inc. since Feb 2016 — **20 years** |
| **Kirsten Spears** | CFO | Since 2018 — **8 years** |

Tan is ~74-75 years old. He and the board have agreed he will remain CEO "at least" through FY2030, incentivized by the AI-linked PSU award.

### Succession risk — significant

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "We are dependent on senior management and if we are unable to attract and retain qualified personnel, we may not be able to execute our business strategy effectively."

The 2026 Proxy discloses that the board maintains both a **longer-term succession plan** (evaluating internal candidates via external advisors) and a **contingency plan** (interim internal succession). However, **no successor has been publicly named or identified.**

Broadcom's business model — deal sourcing, customer relationships (especially with hyperscaler CEOs for custom silicon), financial community credibility, and cost discipline — is heavily concentrated in Tan personally. A sudden departure would create material uncertainty. This is the company's most significant governance risk.

### Board composition

7 of 8 director nominees are independent (87.5%). Key features: annual elections, majority voting, no poison pill, proxy access enabled. The outgoing Lead Independent Director (Eddy Hartenstein) creates a near-term vacancy that has not been publicly filled.

*(Note: board composition data from 2026 Proxy — not from uploaded 10-K filings)*

## Overall Management Quality: **Excellent**

**Rationale:**

Hock Tan has built one of the most successful technology conglomerates of the last two decades through disciplined M&A, relentless cost optimization, and strategic focus on mission-critical infrastructure. The capital allocation track record — no goodwill write-downs, 15 consecutive dividend increases, ROIC recovery after each major acquisition — is among the best in the semiconductor industry.

Compensation is overwhelmingly equity-based and tied to specific long-term AI revenue targets. Guidance credibility is high. Communication is forthright about risks.

The single material governance concern is **succession risk**: Tan is 74-75 years old, no successor is publicly identified, and the business model is heavily key-person-dependent. This is a "when, not if" risk that the board has acknowledged but not yet visibly resolved.
