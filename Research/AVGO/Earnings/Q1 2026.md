## Section 1: Guidance vs. Reality

### What management guided (Q4 FY2025 call, December 11, 2025)

| Metric | Q1 FY2026 Guidance |
|---|---|
| Total Revenue | ~$19.1B |
| Semiconductor Solutions | ~$12.3B |
| AI Semiconductor Revenue | ~$8.2B |
| Infrastructure Software | ~$6.8B |
| Adjusted EBITDA margin | ~67% |

### What actually happened

| Metric | Guided | Actual | Delta | Beat/Miss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Total Revenue** | $19.1B | **$19,311M** | **+$211M (+1.1%)** | **Beat** |
| **Semiconductor** | $12.3B | **$12,515M** | **+$215M (+1.7%)** | **Beat** |
| **AI Semi Revenue** | $8.2B | **$8,400M** | **+$200M (+2.4%)** | **Beat** |
| **Infra Software** | $6.8B | **$6,796M** | **-$4M (in-line)** | **Met** |
| **Adj. EBITDA margin** | ~67% | **68.0%** | **+100 bps** | **Beat** |

### vs. Wall Street consensus

| Metric | Consensus | Actual | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $19.14-19.21B | $19,311M | +$100-171M (+0.5-0.9%) |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $2.02-2.03 | $2.05 | +$0.02-0.03 (+1.0-1.5%) |

### Management's explanation

[Motley Fool Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript — Hock Tan]:
> "AI semiconductor revenue grew 106% year on year to $8.4 billion, way above our outlook."
> "Our customer accelerator business grew 140% year on year in Q1. This momentum continues in Q2."

The upside was driven by faster-than-expected XPU ramp across 6 customers (up from 5), with OpenAI added as the sixth. Hock Tan provided his boldest forward statement to date:

> "We have line of sight to achieve AI revenue from chips, just chips, in excess of $100 billion in 2027."

**Has this explanation been used before?** The "AI demand stronger than expected" narrative has been consistent since Q2 FY2024. However, Q1 FY2026 introduced **three new elements**: (1) the $100B FY2027 AI revenue target, (2) named OpenAI as the sixth XPU customer, and (3) specific gigawatt-scale capacity commitments per customer. These are the most granular forward disclosures Broadcom has ever made.

**Judgment:** Explanation is **strongly data-supported**. The 106% AI revenue growth, 140% XPU growth, 6 named customers, and $73B+ AI backlog with supply chain secured through 2028 are concrete. The $100B FY2027 target is the first time management has committed to a specific dollar figure — this is either high-conviction or reputationally risky sandbagging. Given the PEG-linked PSU ($60-90B threshold), management is now publicly guiding above their own incentive floor. **Not hand-waving; this is management putting their credibility on the line.**

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Semiconductor Solutions | $8,212M | $12,515M | +52% | AI-driven surge |
| Infrastructure Software | $6,704M | $6,796M | +1% | Post-VMware normalization |
| AI Semi Revenue | ~$4,100M | $8,400M | +105% | AI growth rate >100% for first time |
| Non-AI Semi Revenue | ~$4,112M | ~$4,115M | ~0% | Flat — legacy semis stagnant |
| Semi as % of total | 55% | 65% | +10 pts | Mix shifting decisively toward AI/hardware |

**Narrative check:** The revenue story has become **a pure AI story**. Semiconductor growth of 52% is entirely AI-driven (non-AI ~flat). Software grew just 1% on a reported basis, though VMware-specific metrics (ARR +19%, TCV $9.2B) are healthier. Management's "broad-based growth" framing is increasingly misleading — **the business has two speeds: AI (hypergrowth) and everything else (stagnant).**

## Section 3: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved vs. last year

1. **AI revenue more than doubled** ($4.1B → $8.4B, +105%) — crossing the $8B/quarter threshold for the first time. AI is now 44% of total revenue, up from 27%.
2. **XPU customer base doubled** from 3 to 6 — adding Anthropic, a fifth unnamed customer, and OpenAI. This is the most important competitive development of the quarter.
3. **Capital returns surged** — $10.9B returned in Q1 (vs. $17.5B in all of FY2025), signaling management confidence and deleveraging capacity.
4. **Supply chain locked** — Broadcom confirmed capacity secured through 2028 for all XPU customers, de-risking the AI growth trajectory.

### Materially deteriorated vs. last year

1. **Infrastructure software growth collapsed** to +1% YoY (vs. +47% in Q1 FY2025). This is largely a VMware anniversary effect, but the deceleration from +19% in Q4 to +1% in Q1 is sharp. VMware-specific metrics (+13% rev, +19% ARR) are healthier than the segment-level figure suggests.
2. **Revenue mix shifted toward hardware** — semiconductor from 55% to 65% of total. This structurally lowers blended margins as software (93% gross margin) becomes a smaller share.
3. **Non-AI semiconductor revenue flat** at ~$4.1B — broadband, wireless, storage, and industrial are not recovering.
4. **Capex rising** — $250M in Q1 alone (vs. $623M for all of FY2025). Management guided "higher capex in FY2026." The asset-light thesis is under gentle pressure.

### What is new

1. **$100B FY2027 AI revenue target** — first time management committed to a specific annual AI revenue figure. This becomes the key credibility test.
2. **OpenAI named as sixth XPU customer** — first-generation XPU deployment in 2027 at 1+ gigawatt of compute capacity.
3. **Gigawatt-scale capacity commitments** — Anthropic >3 GW by 2027, Meta multiple GW, OpenAI 1+ GW. This unit of measure (gigawatts of AI compute) is new to Broadcom's earnings vocabulary.
4. **$10B share repurchase authorization** — extended through December 2026. Management signaling willingness to return cash aggressively at current prices.

### What did NOT change despite management emphasis

1. **Non-GAAP gross margin** — 77.0% vs. ~77% guided. Despite emphasizing AI momentum, gross margin didn't expand because AI hardware carries lower margins than software. Management honestly guided 77% flat for Q2.
2. **Customer concentration** — still dependent on a small number of hyperscalers. 6 customers is better than 3, but AI revenue concentration within this group remains extreme.
3. **Software as an afterthought** — despite Tan's claims that VCF "cannot be disintermediated," the software segment received minimal airtime on the call. AI dominated the narrative.

## Summary

| Dimension | Rating | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Execution vs. Expectations** | **Improving** | Beat guidance on revenue (+1.1%) and AI (+2.4%); Q2 guide 7% above consensus; Adj. EBITDA margin beat by 100 bps |
| **Management Credibility** | **Strong** | 5th consecutive beat; most granular forward disclosures ever (named customers, GW commitments, $100B target); guidance consistently above Street |
| **Business Momentum** | **Accelerating** | AI growth rate accelerated from 74% to 106% QoQ; XPU customers doubled to 6; Q2 AI guide implies 140% YoY growth; backlog at $162B |

### Key risk to monitor next quarter

The **Q2 FY2026 guide of $22B** is the highest sequential revenue jump in Broadcom's history (+$2.7B or +14% QoQ). If AI demand softens even modestly, this becomes a very high bar. Watch for:
- AI revenue vs. $10.7B guide (any miss would be the first)
- Software revenue vs. $7.2B guide (VMware recovery needed)
- Gross margin vs. 77% guide (AI mix pressure)
- Contract liabilities trend (we flagged $1.5B YoY decline in the 10-K; need Q2 update)
