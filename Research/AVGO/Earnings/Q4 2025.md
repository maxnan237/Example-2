# AVGO — Q4 FY2025 Earnings Analysis

*Quarter ended November 2, 2025 | Reported December 11, 2025*
*Sources: Broadcom Q4 FY2025 press release (investors.broadcom.com), Motley Fool earnings call transcript, Futurum Group analysis*
*Note: Quarterly earnings data sourced from external press releases and transcripts — not from uploaded 10-K filings*

## Section 1: Guidance vs. Reality

### What management guided (Q3 FY2025 call, September 2025)

| Metric | Q4 FY2025 Guidance |
|---|---|
| Total Revenue | ~$17.4B |
| AI Semiconductor Revenue | ~$6.2B |
| Adjusted EBITDA margin | ~67% of revenue |

### What actually happened

| Metric | Guided | Actual | Delta | Beat/Miss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Total Revenue** | $17.4B | **$18,015M** | **+$615M (+3.5%)** | **Beat** |
| **AI Semi Revenue** | $6.2B | **$6,500M** | **+$300M (+4.8%)** | **Beat** |
| **Adj. EBITDA margin** | ~67% | **67.8%** | **+80 bps** | **Beat** |

### vs. Wall Street consensus

| Metric | Consensus | Actual | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $17.46-17.50B | $18,015M | +$515M (+3.0%) |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $1.87 | $1.95 | +$0.08 (+4.3%) |

### Management's explanation

[Motley Fool Q4 2025 Earnings Call Transcript — Hock Tan]:
> "AI semiconductor revenue of $6.5 billion, which was up 74% year on year."
> "We have never seen bookings of the nature that what we have seen over the past three months."

The upside was attributed to stronger-than-expected AI accelerator demand, particularly custom XPU orders. A fifth XPU customer was acquired through a $1B order placed for delivery in late 2026.

**Has this explanation been used before?** Yes — "strong AI demand" has been the consistent explanation for beats since Q2 FY2024. However, the Q4 narrative added a new dimension: specific customer order disclosures ($11B follow-on order, fifth XPU customer). This is more concrete than prior quarters.

**Judgment:** Explanation is **well-supported by data**. AI revenue grew 74% YoY with specific, named customer engagements. The $162B consolidated backlog and $73B AI backlog provide hard evidence. Not hand-waving.

## Section 3: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved vs. last year

1. **AI semiconductor revenue nearly doubled** — from $3.8B to $6.5B (+74%). This is now 59% of semiconductor segment revenue, up from ~46%.
2. **XPU customer count expanded** — from 3 named at start of FY2025 to 5 by Q4 end (plus OpenAI subsequently added in Q1 FY2026).
3. **Software margins expanded sharply** — operating margin from ~72% to ~78%, reflecting VMware integration cost takeout.
4. **Backlog hit record** — $162B consolidated ($73B AI, $73B software), providing unprecedented forward visibility.

### Materially deteriorated vs. last year

1. **Non-AI semiconductor growth is negligible** — just +2% YoY. Broadband, storage, and wireless are not recovering meaningfully.
2. **Infrastructure software growth decelerating** — from 196% YoY in Q4 FY2024 to 19% in Q4 FY2025 as the VMware acquisition anniversary normalizes comparisons.
3. **Capex rising** — $237M vs. $122M, nearly doubling. Management guided capex will be "higher in FY2026." The capital-light model may be subtly shifting as AI demands more infrastructure.

### What is new

1. **Fifth XPU customer acquired** — $1B order for late-2026 delivery. Broadening the custom silicon customer base beyond the initial 3-4 hyperscalers.
2. **$11B follow-on order** from an existing customer — signals multi-year engagement deepening.
3. **$162B backlog disclosure** — first time management provided a consolidated backlog figure of this magnitude.

### What did NOT change despite management emphasis

1. **Non-GAAP gross margin** — 77.9% vs. 77.0% prior year. Despite AI revenue surge, gross margin barely moved. Management emphasizes profitability, but higher AI mix (which carries lower margins than software) is offsetting software margin gains.
2. **Customer concentration** — still heavily dependent on a small number of hyperscalers. The 32% single-customer figure (annual) was not updated quarterly.
