# Broadcom Inc. (AVGO) — Business Overview

*Analysis date: 2026-04-08 | Primary source: FY2025 10-K (period ending Nov 2, 2025)*

---

## 1. What the Company Does

Broadcom is a global technology company operating in two distinct businesses: **semiconductor solutions** and **infrastructure software**. It designs, develops and supplies chips and related hardware for data centers, networking, wireless devices, broadband and industrial applications — alongside a large enterprise software franchise anchored by VMware, Symantec, CA Technologies and mainframe products.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We are a global technology leader that designs, develops and supplies a broad range of semiconductor and semiconductor-based solutions and infrastructure software solutions."

The company traces its lineage through AT&T/Bell Labs, Lucent, Hewlett-Packard, and a string of transformative acquisitions (LSI, Broadcom Corp, Brocade, CA Inc., Symantec Enterprise Security, VMware). As of FY2025, Broadcom had approximately 19,000 patents worldwide.

### Semiconductor Solutions — Key Product Lines

Broadcom sells semiconductor products across **five end markets**:

| End Market | Key Products |
|---|---|
| **Networking Connectivity** | Custom AI accelerators (XPUs), Ethernet switching & routing silicon, Ethernet NICs, PHYs, fiber optic components |
| **Wireless Device Connectivity** | RF front-end modules (FBAR filters), Wi-Fi/Bluetooth combo chips, custom touch controllers, inductive charging ASICs |
| **Servers & Storage** | PCIe switches, SAS/RAID controllers, Fibre Channel HBAs, HDD/SSD controller SoCs and preamplifiers |
| **Broadband** | Set-top box SoCs, DSL/cable/PON/Wi-Fi access platform SoCs |
| **Industrial** | Optocouplers, industrial fiber optics, motion encoders, LEDs, automotive Ethernet products |

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "Our AI semiconductor solutions include custom accelerators or XPUs, Ethernet switching and routing silicon, Ethernet NICs, physical layer devices and optical components, as well as racks and systems based on our XPUs."

### Infrastructure Software — Key Portfolios

| Portfolio | Description |
|---|---|
| **Private Cloud (VMware)** | VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF) — integrated compute, networking, storage, management for private/hybrid cloud. Includes vDefend, Tanzu, Private AI, Live Recovery |
| **Mainframe Software** | AIOps & Automation, databases, DevOps, cybersecurity & compliance for mainframe environments (legacy CA Technologies) |
| **Cybersecurity** | Symantec endpoint, network, information and application security; Carbon Black endpoint protection; identity & access management |
| **Enterprise Software** | AIOps, DevOps, Value Stream Management and network observability for enterprise IT |
| **FC SAN Management** | Fibre Channel SAN switches, modules, subsystems and management software (legacy Brocade) |

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "Many of the largest companies in the world, including most of the Fortune 500, and many government agencies rely on our infrastructure and security software solutions."

## 2. Revenue Model

Broadcom generates revenue through two streams as reported on the income statement:

**Products revenue** ($44,847M, 70% of FY2025 total) — semiconductor hardware sales plus upfront software license fees.

**Subscriptions & services revenue** ($19,040M, 30% of FY2025 total) — ratable software subscriptions, maintenance, support and professional services.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Consolidated Statements of Operations]:
> "Net revenue: Products $44,847 [...] Subscriptions and services 19,040 [...] Total net revenue 63,887"

### Important reclassification note

In FY2025, Broadcom reclassified upfront software license revenue from "Subscriptions and services" into "Products." This changes the optics of the split:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "In fiscal year 2025, we included upfront license revenue of $7,800 million within products revenue. To conform to the current year presentation, we reclassified $4,601 million of upfront license revenue from subscriptions and services revenue to products revenue for fiscal year 2024."

**Adjusted breakdown (removing upfront licenses from Products):**

| Revenue Type | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Semiconductor hardware | ~$37,047M | ~$30,359M | ~$27,891M |
| Upfront software licenses | $7,800M | $4,601M | $1,058M |
| Subscriptions & services (ratable) | $19,040M | $16,614M | $6,870M |
| **Total** | **$63,887M** | **$51,574M** | **$35,819M** |

The software business is increasingly shifting to subscription licensing through VCF. Contracts with termination-for-convenience clauses are recognized ratably; those without are recognized upfront.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Revenue Recognition]:
> "For software arrangements without termination for convenience provisions, we recognize revenue for the license portion of the agreements upfront [...] For software arrangements with termination for convenience provisions, we account for these arrangements as a series of daily contracts, resulting in ratable revenue recognition."

**Forward visibility:** Remaining performance obligations (RPO) stood at ~$33.3 billion as of Nov 2, 2025, with ~35% expected to be recognized within 12 months.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Note on RPO]:
> "The remaining performance obligations under these contracts as of November 2, 2025 were approximately $33.3 billion. We expect approximately 35% of this amount to be recognized as revenue over the next 12 months."

## 3. Customer Base

### Concentration risk — significant and growing

Broadcom has meaningful customer concentration, particularly in semiconductors:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "Direct sales to one semiconductor solutions customer, which is a distributor, accounted for 32% and 28% of our net revenue for fiscal years 2025 and 2024, respectively."

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We believe aggregate sales to our top five end customers, through all channels, accounted for approximately 40% of our net revenue for each of the fiscal years 2025 and 2024."

| Metric | FY2025 | FY2024 | FY2023 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Largest single customer (distributor) | 32% of revenue | 28% | 21% |
| Top 5 end customers (all channels) | ~40% | ~40% | ~35% |
| Distributor sales as % of total | 48% | 48% | 57% |

(Inferred from the rising share of the top customer and the AI accelerator commentary: the single largest customer is likely a distributor serving a major hyperscaler buying custom AI silicon. The filing does not name this customer.)

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Accounts Receivable Note]:
> "One customer accounted for 44% and 18% of our net accounts receivable balance as of November 2, 2025 and November 3, 2024, respectively."

### Enterprise vs. Consumer Mix

The semiconductor business serves primarily **enterprise and hyperscaler** customers (data center networking, AI infrastructure, enterprise storage), with **consumer exposure** through wireless device connectivity (smartphone RF components, Wi-Fi/Bluetooth) and broadband (set-top boxes, modems).

The infrastructure software business is **almost entirely enterprise/government** — Fortune 500 companies, banks, insurers, telcos, government agencies.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "Our software customers generally consist of large enterprises [...] including banks, insurance companies, other financial services providers, government agencies, global IT service providers, telecommunication providers, transportation companies, manufacturers, technology companies, retailers, educational organizations and health care institutions."

### Distribution channels

- **Direct sales force** focused on large OEMs
- **Global distributors** and channel partners (48% of revenue in FY2025)
- **VMware cloud service provider partners**, resellers, hyperscale cloud providers
- **OEMs and their contract manufacturers** (CMs)

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, MD&A]:
> "Gross margin was $43,294 million for fiscal year 2025 [...] As a percentage of net revenue, gross margin was 68% and 63% of net revenue for the fiscal years 2025 and 2024, respectively."

Total operating income was $25,484M (39.9% margin) in FY2025, up from $13,463M (26.1%) in FY2024. ## 6. Key Dependencies

### Manufacturing — heavily outsourced, TSMC-dependent

Broadcom operates a primarily **fabless model**, outsourcing the majority of semiconductor manufacturing:

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1 — Manufacturing]:
> "The majority of our front-end wafer manufacturing operations is outsourced to external foundries, including Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company Limited ('TSMC'). We use third-party CMs for a significant majority of our assembly and test operations, including TSMC, Advanced Semiconductor Engineering, Inc., Foxconn Technology Group, Amkor Technology, Inc. and Siliconware Precision Industries Co., Ltd."

Broadcom retains **internal fabrication** only for proprietary-process products:

> "We use our internal fabrication facilities for products utilizing our innovative and proprietary processes, such as our FBAR filters for wireless communications and our vertical-cavity surface emitting laser and side emitting lasers based on gallium arsenide and indium phosphide lasers for fiber optic communications."

Internal III-V semiconductor wafer fabrication is based in the **U.S. and Singapore**. The company's primary product warehouse is in **Malaysia**.

### Supply chain

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1 — Manufacturing]:
> "We purchase materials from hundreds of suppliers on a global basis. These purchases are generally on a purchase order basis and some parts are not readily available from alternate suppliers due to their unique design or the length of time and cost necessary for re-design or qualification."

### Key customer dependency

As noted above, a single distributor/end-customer represents 32% of revenue (FY2025) and 44% of accounts receivable — creating meaningful concentration risk.

[AVGO_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We expect to continue to experience significant customer concentration in future periods. The loss of, or significant decrease in demand from, any of our top five end customers could have a material adverse effect on our business, results of operations and financial condition."

### Regulatory exposure

Broadcom faces **export control risk** given its advanced semiconductor technology and significant Asia Pacific revenue. The filing notes regulatory risk broadly; U.S. Department of Commerce export restrictions on advanced chips to China represent an ongoing consideration. Not found in available filings: specific quantification of China-exposed revenue.

### Platform dependency — VMware transition risk

The infrastructure software segment depends heavily on successful migration of VMware's perpetual-license customer base to subscription-based VCF. The filing notes that contracts with termination-for-convenience clauses (67% of contract liabilities as of Nov 2, 2025) are recognized ratably, creating both revenue visibility and renewal risk.

## Summary

Broadcom is a $64B-revenue technology conglomerate operating at the intersection of semiconductor hardware and enterprise infrastructure software. The business model is built on:

1. **High-margin semiconductors** (57.6% segment margin) with deep design wins in AI/networking, wireless and storage
2. **Extremely high-margin software** (76.8% segment margin) anchored by VMware/VCF, Symantec and mainframe franchises
3. **Significant customer concentration** — one customer at 32% of revenue, top 5 at ~40%
4. **Heavy Asia Pacific manufacturing dependency** — primarily through TSMC and Asian assembly/test partners
5. **Growing recurring revenue** — $33.3B in remaining performance obligations providing forward visibility
