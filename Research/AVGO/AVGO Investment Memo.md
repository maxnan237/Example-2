# AVGO Investment Memo

**Broadcom Inc. (NASDAQ: AVGO) | April 8, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

Broadcom is a $64B-revenue technology conglomerate that sells the connectivity fabric of AI data centers (custom accelerators, Ethernet switches, NICs, optics) and the software layer that runs enterprise private clouds (VMware). Two segments: semiconductors (58% of revenue, 57.6% operating margin) and infrastructure software (42%, 76.8% margin). The company was built through 20 years of disciplined M&A under CEO Hock Tan.

---

## What's Going Right

**AI is a rocket.** AI semiconductor revenue hit $8.4B in Q1 FY2026, up 106% YoY. Six named XPU customers (Google, Meta, Anthropic, OpenAI, plus two unnamed). Consolidated backlog of $162B. Management guides $100B+ in AI chip revenue for FY2027. Custom silicon design wins create multi-year lock-in — once a hyperscaler commits to a Broadcom XPU architecture, switching requires a full chip redesign.

**VMware is printing money.** Infrastructure software operates at 76.8% segment margins (up from 65% at acquisition close). 90%+ of VMware's top 10,000 customers adopted VCF subscriptions. $73B software backlog. ARR growing 19% YoY. The subscription conversion is working.

**Cash generation is extraordinary.** $26.9B in FCF (42% margin). Capex just $623M (1% of revenue). Net debt/EBITDA improved from 2.6x to 1.5x in one year. 15 consecutive annual dividend increases at ~13% CAGR.

**Management is elite.** Hock Tan has a 20-year track record of acquiring, integrating, and optimizing technology franchises. Zero goodwill write-downs on $97.8B in goodwill. Beats consensus every quarter. Compensation is 96% equity tied to AI revenue targets through FY2030.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Customer concentration is extreme.** One customer = 32% of revenue and 44% of accounts receivable. Top 5 = ~40%. If the largest customer cuts orders 30%, that's ~$6.5B in lost revenue with immediate margin impact.

**TSMC dependency is existential.** 95% of outsourced wafers from a single foundry in Taiwan. No long-term contracts. A Taiwan Strait crisis halts the business.

**The AI cycle will turn.** Broadcom's own filing: the AI upturn "may not be sustainable." The semiconductor industry has had 13+ downturns since 1963, averaging one every 4-4.5 years. This cycle is 3+ years old. Non-AI semiconductor revenue is already flat (+0-2% YoY).

**VMware erosion is real but slow.** Hypervisor market share may decline from 72% to 40% by 2029 (Gartner est.). 67% of contract liabilities have termination-for-convenience clauses (up from 55% a year ago). Contract liabilities declined $1.5B YoY. Nutanix reports 40% of bookings are VMware displacements.

**Hidden accounting items.** $7.4B in receivables sold off-balance-sheet through AR factoring (true DSO ~83 days, not reported 40.8). SBC at $7.6B (11.8% of revenue) overstates FCF quality. Global minimum tax will "materially increase" cash taxes starting FY2026.

**Succession risk.** Hock Tan is 74-75 years old. No named successor. No employment contract. No key-person insurance. The company's deal-sourcing, customer relationships, and market credibility are heavily concentrated in one person.

## Decision

**Current stance: Monitor.**

This is a best-in-class business trading at a price that requires sustained excellence across every growth vector simultaneously. The moat is strong and widening. Management is among the best in technology. But:

- At ~65x trailing earnings and 1.7% FCF yield, there is **limited margin of safety**
- The business is a **two-speed story**: AI hypergrowth (106%) masking stagnant non-AI semis and decelerating software
- **Three concentration risks** (32% customer, 95% TSMC, 57% goodwill) create fragility beneath the surface
- The AI capex cycle is the single variable that matters most — and it is the one least within management's control

**Entry target:** Below ~$250 (implying ~18x forward P/E) for a more favorable risk/reward.

**Key triggers to revisit:**

- AI capex cycle showing signs of peaking (hyperscaler capex cuts)
- VMware contract liabilities declining for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Top customer share exceeding 40% or declining below 25%
- Succession plan announced (named internal candidate)
