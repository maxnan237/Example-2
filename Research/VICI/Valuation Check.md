## 5. Dividend-Based Valuation

Dividend discount model (Gordon Growth):

**P = D₁ / (r − g)**

- D₁ = $1.82 (FY2026 estimated)
- g = 4% (mid-cycle dividend growth)
- r = required return

**At various required returns:**

| Required Return | Fair Value |
|---|---|
| 8% (low rate env) | $45.50 |
| 9% (normalized) | $36.40 |
| 10% (current rate env) | $30.33 |

Current price of $28 implies ~10.4% required return — pricing in elevated rates but below the "stress" level.

## 7. Key Valuation Consideration

The dividend yield of 6.40% on an investment-grade REIT with 100% rent collection since inception is an **empirical anchor**. During COVID (tenants had zero revenue), VICI still collected 100% of rent. This operational track record is exceptional.

**The discount to historical fair value exists because:**
1. Higher rates compressed all REIT valuations
2. Caesars tenant health concerns (2025 regional weakness)
3. Las Vegas visitor decline (-7.5% in 2025)

**If one or more of these reverses**, the stock could re-rate toward historical 15x AFFO. A full re-rating = ~30-40% upside + dividends.

**If they persist or worsen**, the stock could stay range-bound around current levels but the 6.4% yield still compounds.

This is classic "income + optionality" setup — high starting yield with embedded call option on rate cuts and Caesars resolution.
