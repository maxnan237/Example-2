# VICI Properties (VICI) — Business Overview

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Primary source: FY2025 10-K (period ending Dec 31, 2025)*

---

## 1. What the Company Does

VICI is the largest gaming/experiential REIT, owning 93 properties across 26 U.S. states and Canada through long-term triple-net leases to casino and entertainment operators.

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We are a Maryland corporation that is primarily engaged in the business of owning and acquiring gaming, hospitality, wellness, entertainment and leisure destinations, subject to long-term triple net leases."

Formed in October 2017 as a spin-off from Caesars Entertainment Operating Company bankruptcy. HQ: New York. **Only 28 full-time employees** — a pure real estate holding company; all property-level operations are handled by tenants under triple-net structure.

## 7. Growth Strategy

### $39.1B invested since formation

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We have announced approximately $39.1 billion of domestic and international investments across gaming and other experiential assets since our formation in October 2017."

### 2025 activity (~$2.1B at 8.9% initial yields)

- **Golden Entertainment sale-leaseback** (Nov 2025): $1.16B for 7 Nevada properties at 7.5% cap rate, 30-year term
- **One Beverly Hills**: $450M mixed-use luxury development stake
- **North Fork Mono Casino**: $510M financing for tribal casino
- **MGM Northfield Park**: Added Clairvest as 14th tenant
- **PENN Master Lease expansion**: Greektown + Bossier City

### Embedded growth — ROFRs and call rights

ROFRs on multiple Caesars Strip properties (Flamingo, Paris, Planet Hollywood, Bally's, The LINQ), Horseshoe Baltimore, Caesars Virginia. ROFOs with Canyon Ranch, Lucky Strike, IGP. Call rights on Caesars Forum Convention Center, Canyon Ranch Austin, Homefield properties.

### Real estate debt as bridge to ownership

$2.6B in loans/securities at 9.1% weighted avg rate, 3.4 year avg term — designed to convert to real estate ownership.

## 8. Key Dependencies

### Tenant concentration (74% from two operators)

If Caesars or MGM files for bankruptcy or demands lease restructuring, VICI's cash flow would be materially impacted. Caesars faced this in 2017 (VICI's creation). 2025 analyst concern: Caesars regional gaming declined, raising restructuring questions.

### Las Vegas Strip concentration (49%)

A major Las Vegas disruption (terrorism, pandemic, economic shock) would have outsized impact.

### Interest rate sensitivity

$17.1B in debt; 99.2% fixed-rate at 4.464% weighted avg. Low immediate rate risk, but $1.75B matures in 2026, $1.5B in 2027 — refinancing at higher rates would compress spreads.

### Gaming regulatory risk

Tenants face gaming licensing requirements; VICI shareholders can be forced to redeem shares if found unsuitable by gaming regulators.

### Emerging digital gaming competition

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "Certain emerging platforms, such as prediction markets, currently operate under federal rather than state regulation [...] This regulatory disparity may create competitive advantages for such platforms relative to our tenants."
