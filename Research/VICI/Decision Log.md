# VICI Properties (VICI) — Decision Log

*Initiated: 2026-04-12*

---

## Investment Thesis Summary

VICI is the largest gaming REIT, owning 93 irreplaceable properties (including Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, Venetian) on long-term triple-net leases to casino and entertainment operators. Trading at ~$28 with a 6.40% yield — 25% below historical fair value (15-16x P/AFFO vs. 11.5x current) — due to Caesars tenant health concerns and elevated interest rates.

**Bull thesis:** Investment-grade REIT with 100% rent collection since inception (including through COVID), 39.6-year weighted avg lease term, 90% CPI-linked rent, 8 consecutive dividend increases, $39B in acquisitions at accretive spreads, and embedded growth via ROFR pipeline — trading at a 25% discount to historical fair value.

**Bear thesis:** 74% of revenue from two tenants (Caesars 39%, MGM 35%). Caesars regional gaming declining; analysts concerned about lease restructuring. 49% of revenue from Las Vegas Strip. $1.75B in debt refinancing in 2026 at higher rates. Rate environment pressuring all REIT valuations.

## Decision Framework

**To buy at ~$28:**
- 6.40% yield with 100% rent collection track record is rare
- 25% discount to historical fair value provides margin of safety
- Rate cut cycle would re-rate toward 15x P/AFFO (+30-40% upside)
- Even in base case, ~10% total return including dividend
- Caesars concerns are priced in — any positive resolution is upside

**To avoid:**
- Believe Caesars will require significant lease restructuring (>15% rent cut)
- Believe 10-Year Treasury heads to 5-6% (would pressure REIT multiples)
- Believe Las Vegas Strip weakness persists into 2027+
- Believe digital gaming permanently impairs regional casino economics

**Current stance: Constructive income play.** At ~$28 with 6.40% yield, downside is cushioned by dividend. The stock is unlikely to be a home run in the near term given Caesars overhang, but as a long-duration yield vehicle with embedded optionality (rate cuts, Caesars resolution), it offers favorable risk-adjusted returns. The 25% discount to historical valuation plus 6.4% yield creates an attractive total return profile even in muddle-through scenarios.

**Key triggers:**
- Q1 2026 earnings (expected late April 2026) — commentary on Caesars lease
- Fed rate policy shift (if cuts begin, VICI likely re-rates up)
- Any Caesars credit event (bankruptcy, rating downgrade, earnings miss)
- New acquisition announcements — continued growth execution
