## Accounting Flags

### 1. CECL (credit loss reserves) create large non-cash charges

The $178M "change in allowance for credit losses" in FY2025 is a non-cash accounting reserve against potential future tenant defaults. It reduces GAAP net income but is added back in AFFO. Total reserves of $1.75B represent ~3.8% of gross real estate — a prudent cushion but materially depresses reported earnings.

### 2. GAAP vs. contractual rent disparity

GAAP lease revenue of $3.67B vs. contractual (cash) rent of $3.15B creates a $524M non-cash "straight-line" adjustment. The AFFO calculation properly backs this out, so AFFO more accurately reflects economic cash flow.

### 3. Lease classification is complex

VICI's leases are split between "sales-type" (GAAP equivalent to owning the property) and "lease financing receivables" (GAAP treats as financing, like a loan). The split is a function of lease term relative to asset life — economically they are the same (triple-net rental income). This complexity can confuse investors who are used to standard REIT accounting.

### 4. CPI escalators cap at 3% — inflation protection is limited

While 90% of rent is CPI-linked long-term, most leases cap the annual escalation at 3%. In a high-inflation environment, rent would lag. Conversely, rent still escalates at 2% minimum even in deflationary periods.

### 5. Equity issuance funds growth

$375M in equity issued in FY2025 added ~1% dilution but enabled $887M in new investments at 8.9% yields. Spread over cost of equity (~6% dividend yield + growth) is accretive.
