# VICI — Q4 FY2025 Earnings Analysis

*Quarter ended December 31, 2025 | Reported February 25, 2026*
*Sources: VICI IR press release, BusinessWire, Nasdaq/Zacks analysis, Commercial Observer*
*Note: Quarterly data from external press releases — not from uploaded 10-K*

---

## Section 1: Guidance vs. Reality

| Metric | Consensus | Actual | Delta | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total Revenue | $1.02B | **$1.01B** | -$10M | **Miss** |
| AFFO per share | $0.60 | **$0.60** | 0 | **Met** |
| FFO per share | ~$0.70 | **$0.57** | -$0.13 | **Miss** (CECL charge) |
| AFFO (total) | — | **$642.5M** | +6.8% YoY | Beat YoY |

The FFO miss was driven by a **one-time $153M CECL (credit loss reserve) charge** — non-cash, accounting-driven. AFFO (the cash metric that matters for dividends) was in-line with consensus.

Management explanation: CECL reserves increased to reflect tenant credit quality assessments, particularly around Caesars regional portfolio. No actual rent payments missed.

**Judgment:** Revenue miss is small; AFFO met. The FFO noise from CECL is an accounting artifact investors should ignore. The bigger concern is **what the CECL increase implies about VICI's own view of Caesars credit quality.**

## Section 4: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved vs. last year
- **AFFO growth accelerating** (+6.6% vs. +5.1% prior year)
- **Balance sheet strengthened** — investment-grade ratings upgraded (Moody's to Baa3, Nov 2024)
- **$2.1B in new investments** at 8.9% weighted avg yield deployed in 2025
- **Diversification advancing** — Lucky Strike (bowling), Chelsea Piers, One Beverly Hills reduced gaming concentration

### Materially deteriorated vs. last year
- **CECL reserves increased $178M** (vs. $127M prior year) — reflecting concerns about Caesars regional credit quality
- **Revenue growth decelerated** to +4.1% (from +6.6% prior year) — fewer blockbuster acquisitions
- **Dividend growth slowed** to +4.1% (from +5.3%)
- **Stock price declined** — near 52-week low at $28 vs. $34 high

### What is new
- **Golden Entertainment deal** ($1.16B, announced Nov 2025) — first foray into Las Vegas locals market
- **Clairvest as 14th tenant** (MGM Northfield Park transition)
- **PENN lease expansion** to Detroit and Bossier City
- **Refinanced $1.3B in senior notes** in April 2025

### What did NOT change despite emphasis
- **Caesars tenant health** — management did not provide reassurance on Regional Master Lease; analysts remain concerned
- **Las Vegas Strip concentration** — still 49% of revenue
- **Dividend growth rate** — decelerating, not accelerating
