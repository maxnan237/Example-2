# VICI Properties (VICI) — Moat Analysis

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, external data*

---

## 1. Switching Costs — **Very Strong**

This is VICI's primary moat. Casino operators cannot easily relocate — the real estate IS the business. Gaming licenses are tied to specific geographic locations, buildings cost $500M-$4B+ to construct, and customers associate operators with physical properties (Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, Venetian).

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Note 4]:
> "Upon lease inception or modification, we have generally concluded that the lease term includes all of the periods covered by extension options as it was reasonably certain at such time that our tenants would renew the lease agreements. At such time, we believed our tenants were economically compelled to renew the lease agreements due to the importance of our real estate to the operation of their business, the significant capital they have invested and are required to invest in our properties under the terms of the lease agreements and the lack of suitable replacement assets."

The company literally uses the phrase "**economically compelled to renew**" — GAAP auditors agreed this is so structurally true that lease extensions should be included in the initial lease term for accounting purposes.

Evidence: **100% rent collection since 2017 (including through COVID).** When casinos were shut down by government order and had zero revenue, VICI still collected 100% of rent. This is not hyperbole — the triple-net structure and the inability of operators to walk away created this outcome.

**Rating: Very Strong.** Among the strongest switching costs in any REIT sector.

---

## 2. Network Effects — **Absent**

No network effects in single-tenant real estate. Each property stands alone.

**Rating: Absent.**

---

## 3. Cost Advantages — **Strong**

### Investment-grade cost of capital

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Item 1]:
> "We have investment-grade ratings from all three major credit rating agencies, which broadens our access across capital markets and supports a stronger cost of capital."

VICI can issue 10-year senior unsecured debt at ~5% vs. ~8-9% for non-investment-grade gaming operators. This spread — plus the spread between VICI's cost of capital (~6% blended) and acquisition cap rates (8-9%) — is the primary source of accretion on every deal.

### Scale advantages

$46.7B in assets, largest gaming REIT, included in S&P 500. Scale enables:
- Cheapest capital in gaming real estate
- Ability to absorb $1B+ acquisitions (Golden Entertainment, Venetian)
- Diversification that single-property investors can't replicate

### Lean cost structure

28 employees managing a $47B balance sheet. G&A of $65M on $4B revenue = 1.6%. This is among the lowest overhead ratios in any REIT sector.

**Rating: Strong.** Cost of capital is a durable advantage over potential competitors.

---

## 4. Intangible Assets — **Strong**

### Irreplaceable trophy assets

Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, Venetian, Mandalay Bay — these Las Vegas Strip properties cannot be recreated. Limited Strip real estate, gaming license restrictions, $1B+ construction costs, decades of brand development. Similar dynamics for regional market leaders (Borgata in Atlantic City, Beau Rivage on Gulf Coast).

### Relationships and pipeline

ROFRs on 5+ Caesars Strip properties. ROFOs with Canyon Ranch, Lucky Strike, Indigenous Gaming Partners. Call rights on future developments. These contractual arrangements give VICI first look at billions of dollars of potential future acquisitions.

### S&P 500 inclusion (June 2022)

First REIT from IPO to S&P 500 inclusion. Provides index fund demand for the stock, enhancing liquidity and potentially lowering cost of equity.

**Rating: Strong.** Trophy asset ownership and contractual growth pipeline are defensible.

---

## 5. Efficient Scale — **Moderate**

Gaming real estate is a specialized niche. Publicly traded gaming REITs are essentially a duopoly:
- **VICI**: $46.7B assets — Las Vegas Strip + regional + experiential
- **GLPI**: $13.6B market cap — regional focus (Penn, Bally's)

No other publicly traded gaming REIT operates at scale. Private competitors (Blackstone/BREIT, Apollo, sovereign wealth funds) exist but are limited in capacity.

**Rating: Moderate.** The market supports two major players but not a third; VICI has scale advantages over GLPI in the Las Vegas Strip segment specifically.

---

## Moat Trajectory: **Stable to Widening**

1. **Investment-grade rating upgraded** (Moody's to Baa3 in Nov 2024) — lowering cost of capital further
2. **Asset diversification into experiential** (Lucky Strike, Chelsea Piers, Canyon Ranch, golf, One Beverly Hills) — reducing gaming concentration over time
3. **International expansion potential** (Canada, potentially UK/Europe/Asia long-term)
4. **ROFR pipeline keeps growing** with each acquisition

### Potential narrowing risks

- **Interest rate environment** — if rates stay high, cost-of-capital advantage over non-investment-grade operators compresses
- **Tenant concentration** — if Caesars or MGM face financial distress, the triple-net lease structure's value comes into question
- **Gaming disruption** — prediction markets, online gaming, legalized sports betting may reduce long-term demand for physical casinos, pressuring tenant cash flows

---

## Moat Summary

VICI has a **very strong and stable moat** built on extraordinary switching costs (tenants "economically compelled to renew" per GAAP analysis), investment-grade cost of capital, and ownership of irreplaceable trophy assets on the Las Vegas Strip. The 100% rent collection through COVID — when tenants had zero revenue — is empirical proof of the moat's strength. The primary risk is tenant concentration (Caesars + MGM = 74% of revenue), but the triple-net lease structure means VICI gets paid regardless of tenant operating performance as long as they remain solvent. Moat is slowly widening through diversification into non-gaming experiential assets and continued investment-grade balance sheet strengthening.
