# VICI Properties (VICI) — Bear Case

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, external data*

---

## 1. Thesis Killer: Caesars or MGM Lease Restructuring

Caesars (39% of annualized rent) and MGM (35%) together represent **74% of revenue**. If either tenant seeks lease restructuring in a downturn, VICI would face one of three bad outcomes: rent reduction, extended term (lower IRR), or bankruptcy court proceedings.

**Caesars specifically is the concern today.** Per analyst commentary (Scotiabank, Benzinga, Feb 2026):

- Caesars FY2025 Las Vegas net revenue **declined 4.7%** to $4.05B
- Full-year net income **fell ~20%** to $703M
- Las Vegas Strip gaming revenues declined ~10% in Q3 2025
- Multiple analyst downgrades in early 2026 citing Caesars Regional Master Lease restructuring uncertainty

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "The inability or unwillingness of a significant tenant to meet its payment or other obligations under a lease or other payment obligation with us could materially and adversely affect our business."

If Caesars demands a rent reduction of, say, 15% on the Regional Master Lease, that's ~$110M in lost annual rent — directly flowing to AFFO reduction of ~10 cents/share. Stock could re-rate downward sharply.

---

## 2. Structural Weaknesses

### 2a. Interest rate sensitivity on spread and refinancing

VICI's economic model depends on the spread between its cost of capital (~6% blended dividend yield + growth) and acquisition cap rates (8-9%). Every 100 bps rise in rates compresses this spread.

$1.75B in debt matures in 2026 at coupons of 4.25-4.50%. Refinancing at 5.5-6.0% would add ~$20-30M in annual interest. $1.5B matures in 2027. Over several years, refinancing costs compound.

### 2b. Las Vegas Strip concentration (49%)

A severe Las Vegas disruption (pandemic, terrorism, economic shock, F1 cancellation) would impact half of VICI's revenue. 2025 Las Vegas visitor count declined 7.5% — the first post-COVID decline — partly driven by 24% drop in Canadian visitors amid U.S.-Canada tensions.

### 2c. CPI caps limit inflation protection

While 90% of rent is CPI-linked long-term, escalators cap at 3% annually. In a sustained high-inflation environment (5%+ CPI), rent growth would lag real estate value appreciation.

### 2d. Tenant CAPEX requirements can stress operators

Caesars Regional + LV Master Leases require tenants to spend 1% of net revenues on CAPEX with $286M rolling 3-year minimum. If Caesars' cash flow declines, these obligations become more onerous, potentially triggering restructuring requests.

### 2e. Gaming disruption is a long-term headwind

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "Certain emerging platforms, such as prediction markets, currently operate under federal rather than state regulation, potentially allowing them to offer gaming-related products in states where traditional gaming is prohibited."

Prediction markets (Kalshi, Polymarket), DraftKings/FanDuel sports betting, and online casino gaming all pull wallet share away from physical casinos. Regional casino operators are most at risk. If regional gaming revenues decline 20%+, VICI's non-Strip tenants (Century, Foundation, JACK, PENN) could face distress.

---

## 3. Assumptions the Bull Case Takes for Granted

1. **Caesars and MGM stay solvent.** Their leases are triple-net with CPI escalators — but only if they pay rent. Bankruptcies can force renegotiation.

2. **CPI stays 2-3%.** If inflation spikes to 5%+, VICI's capped escalators lag; if it drops to 1%, rent growth slows.

3. **Cap rates stay at 7-9%.** If competition for gaming assets intensifies (private equity re-enters), VICI's accretive acquisition model breaks down.

4. **Interest rates decline.** The bull thesis assumes rate cuts enable valuation re-rating toward 15-16x AFFO historical average. If rates stay elevated, the stock stays range-bound.

5. **Las Vegas recovers.** 2026 UNLV projections assume +2.4% visitor growth. If Canadian tensions persist or recession hits, Las Vegas weakness extends.

---

## 4. Permanent Impairment Scenarios

### Caesars bankruptcy
If Caesars files Chapter 11 (as parent CEOC did in 2017), VICI's leases could be rejected or modified. While VICI's real estate ownership is protected, rent payments could be cut 20-40% during restructuring. Historical precedent: the 2017 Caesars bankruptcy is how VICI was formed — but this time VICI would be on the receiving end.

### Sustained Strip disruption
A Las Vegas specific shock (pandemic, tunnel attack, massive convention cancellations) that reduces Strip revenue by 30%+ for 2+ years would put material pressure on MGM and Caesars Las Vegas operators. Rent restructuring requests would follow.

### Interest rates to 6-7%+
If 10-year Treasury yields rise to 6-7% (vs. 4.3% now), VICI's spread model compresses and the stock likely de-rates to 9-10x AFFO. Fair value could fall to $21-24/share.

## Summary

The VICI bear case is straightforward: **74% of revenue depends on two tenants, 49% of revenue comes from Las Vegas Strip, and the entire business model depends on the spread between cost of capital and acquisition cap rates — all in an environment where Caesars' financial health is deteriorating, Las Vegas visitors are declining, and interest rates remain elevated.** At ~$28 with a 6.4% yield, meaningful pessimism is priced in. But if Caesars demands lease restructuring (as multiple analysts have flagged), the stock could have another 15-25% downside to $21-24 before finding equilibrium.
