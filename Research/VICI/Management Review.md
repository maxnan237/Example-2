# VICI Properties (VICI) — Management Review

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, external data*

---

## 1. CEO: Edward Pitoniak

CEO and Director since VICI's formation in October 2017. Career path:
- **Media:** Cosmopolitan → Times Mirror Magazines (Ski Magazine editor-in-chief)
- **Intrawest Corporation** (ski/golf resort operator), SVP 1996-2004
- **Canadian Hotel Income Properties REIT**, CEO 2004-2009 — led 4 consecutive years of top total return among Canadian hotel REITs
- **Realterm** (logistics private equity real estate), Vice Chair 2015-2017
- **Independent Director, Ritchie Bros. Auctioneers** 13 years

*(Note: CEO background from viciproperties.com, Commercial Observer — not from uploaded filings)*

### Track record at VICI (2017-2025)

Under Pitoniak, VICI has:
- Scaled from post-bankruptcy Caesars spin-off to $47B EV largest gaming REIT
- Completed ~$39B in acquisitions since formation
- Entered S&P 500 in June 2022 — fastest REIT IPO-to-S&P inclusion in history
- Maintained **100% rent collection** through COVID
- Grown AFFO/share every year, dividend every year (8 consecutive raises)
- Expanded from pure gaming into experiential (Lucky Strike, Chelsea Piers, One Beverly Hills, golf, Canyon Ranch)
- Achieved investment-grade ratings from all three agencies (Moody's upgrade to Baa3 in Nov 2024)

This is textbook REIT execution.

## 2. Capital Allocation

### Disciplined acquisition pipeline

VICI has deployed $39B over 8 years at a weighted average **initial cap rate of ~7-8%** while funding with ~6% blended cost of capital. Every deal is spread-accretive.

**2025 investments (~$2.1B at 8.9% initial yields):**
- Golden Entertainment: $1.16B at 7.5% cap rate
- One Beverly Hills: $450M
- North Fork Mono: $510M financing
- Venetian capital investments: $400M

## 4. Guidance Credibility

VICI's guidance has been consistently met or beaten. FY2025 AFFO/share of $2.38 was at/above guidance range. FY2026 guidance of $2.42-$2.45 AFFO/share implies 2-3% growth — conservative given $2.1B in 2025 acquisitions at 8.9% yields that should ramp into 2026.

**Recent miss:** Q4 FY2025 revenue came in ~$10M below consensus (FFO miss driven by $153M CECL charge, AFFO in-line). Management explained this as accounting noise, not operational weakness. Analysts accepted the explanation.

## 5. Communication Quality

VICI's disclosures are among the clearest in REIT-land:
- Detailed lease-by-lease rent disclosures in Note 4
- Explicit AFFO reconciliation (industry standard but thorough)
- Transparent tenant concentration (Caesars 39%, MGM 35%)
- Explicit acknowledgment of risks (Las Vegas Strip 49%, prediction market threats)

No obvious opacity or governance red flags.

---

## 6. Overall Assessment: **Excellent**

Pitoniak has executed a best-in-class REIT strategy: disciplined acquisitions at accretive spreads, diversification into experiential while maintaining gaming focus, investment-grade balance sheet, consistent dividend growth. The 100% rent collection through COVID is empirical proof of the business model's durability under stress.

**One area to watch:** Pitoniak is 69 years old (born ~1956). He has been at VICI since inception (8+ years). No explicit succession plan disclosed. Given the concentrated leadership team (28 employees) and the importance of CEO relationships with tenant CEOs (Caesars, MGM, Hard Rock), succession planning will become a watch item.
