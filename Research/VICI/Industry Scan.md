# VICI Properties (VICI) — Industry Scan

*Analysis date: 2026-04-12 | Sources: FY2025 10-K, external industry data*

---

## 1. Gaming Real Estate Market

**Scale:** Gaming REITs own 116 properties across 21 states with gross asset value of ~$26.9 billion (Nareit, YE 2024). U.S. casino/gaming market was ~$160-161B in 2025 (Market Research Future).

**Structure:** The sale-leaseback model has become dominant for casino real estate — operators (Caesars, MGM, Penn, Bally's) unlock real estate value while retaining operational control. This trend since ~2013 has created two dominant publicly traded landlords:

- **VICI Properties** — ~$47B EV, largest gaming REIT, Las Vegas Strip heavy
- **GLPI (Gaming & Leisure Properties)** — ~$13.6B market cap, regional gaming focus

Together, VICI and GLPI own the vast majority of publicly traded gaming real estate.

## 4. Gaming Industry Trends

### Las Vegas 2025-2026

- **2025 visitor count: 38.5M** (-7.5% YoY) — first post-COVID decline
- **2025 Strip gaming revenue: ~$8.8B** (record, despite visitor decline) — higher spend per visitor
- **Canadian visitor decline: -24%** — U.S.-Canada tensions
- **2026 UNLV projection: 40.1M visitors** (+2.4%) — supported by F1, sports, conventions

Las Vegas is shifting from mass-tourism to high-end spending. Record gaming revenue on fewer visitors suggests premium segment strength (VIP gaming, luxury hotels). VICI's trophy properties (Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, Venetian) are better positioned than budget properties.

### Regional gaming softness

Caesars' regional gaming revenues declined in 2025. Consumer discretionary spending pressure (inflation, rate stress) has hit lower-income gamblers hardest. This drives analyst concern about Caesars Regional Master Lease restructuring.

### Emerging threats

**Prediction markets** (Kalshi, Polymarket) operating under federal oversight may offer gaming-style products in states where gambling is restricted. **Online sports betting** (DraftKings, FanDuel) and **online casinos** (iGaming legal in 7+ states) pull wallet share from physical casinos.

*(Note: industry trends from Las Vegas Review-Journal, Nareit, Market Research Future — not from uploaded filings)*

## 5. Interest Rate Environment

**10-Year Treasury (April 2026): 4.31%**

VICI's 6.4% dividend yield offers a ~210 bps spread over Treasuries — narrower than historical norms (300-400 bps). REITs generally trade inversely to interest rates:

- When 10Y rises → REIT yields must rise → stock prices fall
- When 10Y falls → REIT yields fall → stock prices rise

VICI's 2-year underperformance (price -0.3% in 2024, +1.9% in 2025) correlates with elevated Treasury yields. Any Fed rate cut cycle or 10Y decline would likely drive meaningful re-rating.

**Refinancing exposure:** $1.75B due 2026 at 4.25-4.50% coupons. Refinancing at 5.5-6.0% adds ~$20-30M in annual interest.

## 6. Regulatory Environment

**Gaming regulation:** Each U.S. state and tribal nation has its own gaming regulatory framework. Tenants require licenses in each jurisdiction. VICI itself is subject to shareholder redemption if any investor is deemed "unsuitable" by state gaming regulators.

[VICI_10K_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "Our charter provides that any of our shares held by investors who are found to be unsuitable by state gaming regulatory authorities are subject to redemption."

**REIT requirements:** Must distribute 90%+ of taxable income. VICI is structured to comply and has investment-grade rating from all three major agencies.

---

## Key Takeaway

Gaming real estate is a duopoly (VICI + GLPI) in a maturing sale-leaseback market. VICI's differentiation is its Las Vegas Strip concentration — trophy assets with high-end customer bases that outperform regional peers in softening consumer environments. The primary sector headwinds are: (1) tenant financial stress, particularly Caesars regional; (2) interest rate pressure on REIT valuations; (3) emerging digital gaming competition to tenant operators. The primary tailwinds are: (1) continued sale-leaseback deal flow, (2) tourism recovery in Las Vegas expected in 2026, (3) eventual rate cut cycle that would re-rate REITs higher.
