# VICI Investment Memo

**VICI Properties (NYSE: VICI) | April 12, 2026**

---

## The Business in 30 Seconds

VICI is the largest gaming/experiential REIT, owning 93 irreplaceable properties including Caesars Palace Las Vegas, MGM Grand, Venetian, and Mandalay Bay. Long-term triple-net leases (39.6-year weighted avg term, 90% CPI-linked long-term) to casino and entertainment operators. 100% occupancy, 100% rent collection since 2017 formation — including through COVID when tenants had zero revenue. Investment grade from all three agencies. $39B invested since inception. Trades at $28 with a 6.40% dividend yield.

---

## What's Going Right

**100% rent collection since inception.** This is not marketing — during COVID, casinos were shut down by government order with zero operating revenue, and VICI still collected 100% of rent. The triple-net structure and "economically compelled to renew" GAAP analysis create exceptional tenant lock-in.

**Investment-grade balance sheet.** All three agencies rate VICI investment grade. 99.2% fixed-rate debt at 4.46% weighted avg. Well-laddered maturities through 2054. $3.2B in liquidity.

**Accretive capital allocation.** $2.1B deployed in 2025 at 8.9% weighted avg initial yields against ~6% blended cost of capital. Every deal is spread-positive. Embedded growth via ROFR pipeline (5+ Caesars Strip properties, Canyon Ranch, Lucky Strike, IGP).

**8 consecutive dividend increases.** Current $1.80 annualized dividend has grown ~6.6% CAGR since IPO — described as "triple the rate of similar REITs" (Motley Fool).

**Diversification underway.** Expanding beyond pure gaming into Lucky Strike bowling, Chelsea Piers, One Beverly Hills, Canyon Ranch — reducing gaming concentration while adding experiential assets.

**S&P 500 member** (June 2022) — fastest REIT IPO-to-index inclusion in history. Supports institutional demand and cost of capital.

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Tenant concentration — 74% from two operators.** Caesars (39% of rent) and MGM (35%) dominate. Caesars' regional gaming revenues declined in 2025; analysts flagged lease restructuring risk (Scotiabank, Baird cut targets citing this in Feb 2026). If Caesars demands even a 10% rent cut on the Regional Master Lease, that's ~$75M in lost AFFO.

**Las Vegas Strip concentration (49%).** 2025 Las Vegas visitors declined 7.5% (first post-COVID decline). Canadian visitors fell 24%. A sustained Las Vegas disruption would hit half of VICI's revenue.

**Interest rate sensitivity.** REITs trade inversely to rates. 10-Year Treasury at 4.31% is elevated vs. historical norms. $1.75B matures in 2026 and will refinance at higher rates.

**CECL reserves increasing.** $178M non-cash credit loss reserve in FY2025 vs. $127M prior year — signals management concern about tenant credit quality.

**CPI caps limit inflation protection.** Most escalators capped at 3%, so in high-inflation environments rent growth lags.

**Digital gaming threat to tenants.** Prediction markets, online sports betting, iGaming pull wallet share from physical casinos — particularly regional properties.

## Decision

**Current stance: Constructive income play.**

At ~$28 with a 6.40% dividend yield, VICI offers an attractive setup for income-focused investors:
- **Dividend is well-covered** (74% AFFO payout) and likely to grow 3-5% annually
- **25% discount to historical fair value** provides margin of safety
- **Rate cut optionality** could drive 30-40% upside via multiple expansion
- **Caesars concerns are already priced in** — any positive resolution is asymmetric upside
- **100% rent collection through COVID** is the strongest empirical evidence of the business model's durability

This is not a high-growth stock — AFFO grows ~5% annually. But adding the 6.4% yield creates total returns of ~11% even in muddle-through scenarios. The bull case offers ~40% upside if rates fall and Caesars health improves.

**Key triggers:**
- Q1 2026 earnings (late April 2026) — Caesars commentary
- Fed rate cut cycle — would drive meaningful re-rating
- Caesars Regional Master Lease resolution — any clarity (positive or negative)
- New acquisition announcements — continued pipeline execution

**Key risks to monitor:**
- Any missed rent payment from any tenant (100% collection would be broken)
- CECL reserves continuing to expand quarter-over-quarter
- Las Vegas visitor declines persisting into 2026
- 10-Year Treasury exceeding 5.5%
