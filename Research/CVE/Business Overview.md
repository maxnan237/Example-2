# Cenovus Energy (CVE) — Business Overview

*Analysis date: 2026-04-13 | Sources: FY2025 40-F, AIF, MD&A (filed Feb 18-19, 2026)*
*Note: Canadian filer; reports in CAD under IFRS. Fiscal year ends December 31.*

---

## 1. What the Company Does

Cenovus Energy is one of Canada's largest integrated oil & gas companies. The business spans the full value chain — from oil sands production in Alberta to refining in the U.S. Midwest.

[CVE_AIF_FY2025.htm, Description of the Business]:
> "We are one of the largest Canadian-based crude oil and natural gas producers, with upstream operations in Canada and the Asia Pacific region, and one of the largest Canadian-based refiners and upgraders, with downstream operations in Canada and the U.S."

> "Our integrated upstream and downstream operations help to mitigate the impact of volatility in light-heavy crude oil differentials and contribute to the Company's net earnings by capturing value from crude oil, natural gas and NGLs production through to the sale of finished products."

- **HQ:** Calgary, Alberta
- **Listings:** TSX (CVE) and NYSE (CVE)
- **Employees (FY2025):** 7,211 FTE
- **Founded:** 2009 (spinout from Encana); transformed by 2021 Husky merger and 2025 MEG acquisition

## 2. Operating Segments

### Upstream

**Oil Sands** — SAGD bitumen production from Foster Creek, Christina Lake (incl. acquired MEG), Sunrise, Lloydminster thermal & conventional. Total 644 MBOE/d in FY2025.

**Conventional** — NGLs and natural gas from Edson, Clearwater, Rainbow Lake, and Northern Corridor (Elmworth, Wapiti) in Alberta and BC.

**Offshore** — Atlantic Canada (Terra Nova, White Rose), China (Liwan), Indonesia (40% Husky-CNOOC Madura JV).

### Downstream

**Canadian Refining** — Lloydminster Upgrader (heavy oil → synthetic crude), Lloydminster Refinery (asphalt/diesel), Bruderheim crude-by-rail terminal, two ethanol plants. ~108 Mbbls/d.

**U.S. Refining** — Three wholly-owned refineries: Lima (OH), Toledo (OH), Superior (WI). Combined ~472 Mbbls/d capacity. **Note: 50% WRB JV (Wood River + Borger) divested to Phillips 66 in Sept 2025 for US$1.4B.**

## 5. Key Acquisitions

### Husky Merger (January 2021)

Combined two Canadian energy companies. Added:
- Lloydminster Upgrader and Refinery
- Sunrise oil sands SAGD
- Atlantic Canada offshore (Terra Nova, White Rose)
- Asia Pacific offshore (China, Indonesia)
- WRB Refining JV (50% Wood River + Borger refineries — later divested 2025)
- Brought in CK Hutchison (16.8%) and L.F. Investments (12.3%) as large block shareholders

### MEG Energy Acquisition (November 13, 2025)

[CVE_AIF_FY2025.htm, 2025 Three Year History]:
> "Purchase consideration for the MEG Acquisition included $3.4 billion in cash partially funded through the receipt of a $2.7 billion term loan facility, and the issuance of 143.9 million Cenovus common shares with a fair value of $3.7 billion."

- Total deal value: **C$8.5B+ (C$3.4B cash + C$3.7B stock + assumed debt)**
- Added Christina Lake North asset (directly adjacent to existing Christina Lake)
- Added 1,483 MMBOE 2P reserves
- ~110,000 BOE/d incremental production
- Synergy target: C$150M in 2026/2027, growing to C$400M+ by 2028

Cenovus's own refineries are the largest "customer" — vertical integration reduces external customer concentration.

## 9. Pipeline Access — TMX Significance

[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm, Oil Sands]:
> "At Foster Creek, per-unit transportation expenses increased primarily due to higher sales volumes sold at West Coast destinations through the use of TMX, increasing to 32 percent (2024 – 20 percent)."

Trans Mountain Expansion has materially shifted Cenovus's market access toward Pacific Basin/tidewater pricing, narrowing the WCS-WTI differential from ~$18/bbl pre-TMX to ~$11-12/bbl post-TMX. **Direct positive for Cenovus's realized prices.**

## 10. Capital Allocation Framework

[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm, Returns to Shareholders Target]:

**Net Debt target: C$4.0 billion** (~1.0x Net Debt / Adjusted Funds Flow at WTI US$45/bbl)

**Shareholder Return Framework (post-MEG):**
- Net Debt > C$6B → 50% of Excess Free Funds Flow returned to shareholders

- Net Debt at C$4B → 100% returned

**Buybacks:** Active NCIB renewed Nov 2025 — up to 120.3M shares through Nov 2026. FY2025: 89.4M shares for C$1,995M at C$21.87 average.

## 11. Sustainability / ESG

**Pathways Alliance** (rebranded "Oil Sands Alliance" Feb 2026): Six-company CCUS consortium proposing C$16.5B carbon capture and storage network in northern Alberta. Target: 4.2 Mt CO2/year capture by 2030. **Status: Pre-FID, contingent on regulatory and fiscal support.**

[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm, Risk Factors]:
> "If this project is delayed or does not proceed, Cenovus's ability to achieve its GHG reduction goals and ambitions will be delayed and may not be achieved."

Note: FY2025 filings do not commit to a specific net-zero target year or absolute GHG reduction percentage.

## 12. Key Dependencies

1. **Crude oil prices** — primary revenue driver; WTI/WCS sensitivity dominates everything
2. **Pipeline egress** — TMX critical for tidewater access; future expansion needed
3. **Royalty regime** — Alberta Crown royalties scale with WTI (1-9% pre-payout, 25-40% post-payout net revenue)
4. **Federal carbon policies** — carbon pricing, GHG regulations, Pathways Alliance support
5. **Refining margins** — crack spreads drive U.S. Refining segment
6. **MEG integration execution** — C$400M+ synergy target depends on this
7. **Indigenous consent** — for any new oil sands development
8. **Foreign exchange** — USD revenues vs. CAD costs creates structural exposure
