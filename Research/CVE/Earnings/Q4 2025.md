## Section 6: Strategic Updates

### MEG integration milestones
- Nov 13, 2025: Acquisition closed
- Jan 1, 2026: MEG amalgamated into Cenovus
- 2026 capex of ~$850M for Christina Lake North expansion
- $150M synergies targeted in 2026

### WRB divestiture aftermath
- Sold Sept 30, 2025 for US$1.4B
- U.S. Refining throughput drops to 430-450 Mbbls/d (from 515.9 Mbbls/d in FY2025)
- Proceeds applied to MEG financing + buybacks

### Capital returns framework adjustment
[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm]:
> "As at December 31, 2025, our Net Debt position was $8.3 billion and, as a result, our returns to shareholders target for the three months ended March 31, 2026, will be 50 percent of the first quarter's Excess Free Funds Flow."

Capital returns paused at 50% level (down from 100% pre-MEG) until net debt drops to C$6B.

### Net debt deleveraging path
- Dec 31, 2025: C$8.3B
- Target: C$4B (long-term)
- At current oil prices: ~12-18 months to reach C$6B threshold

## Section 7: What Actually Changed?

### Materially improved vs. last year
- **Net earnings +25%** despite lower revenue
- **Production record** (834.2 MBOE/d for full year, 917.9 in Q4)
- **Refining margin recovery** ($205M vs -$312M)
- **NCIB execution** ($1,995M at $21.87 avg — opportunistic)
- **MEG closure** transformed scale

### Materially deteriorated vs. last year
- **Net debt jumped** C$3.7B (post-MEG)
- **Capital returns constrained** to 50% of Excess FFF (vs. 100% prior)
- **Variable dividend dropped to zero** (was $0.135/share in FY2024)
- **Revenue down 8.4%** (lower oil prices)

### What is new
- **Three U.S. refineries instead of five** (post-WRB sale)
- **Larger Christina Lake position** (post-MEG)
- **Higher base dividend** (raised May 2025: C$0.18 → C$0.20)

| Dimension | Rating |

| Execution vs. Expectations | **Strong** — 28.6% EPS beat, record production |
| Management Credibility | **Strong** — disciplined capital framework working |

The **MEG acquisition + WRB divestiture** simplified and extended the asset base. **2026 will be the integration year** — expect strong production growth (945-985 MBOE/d guided) but capital returns constrained until deleveraging progresses.

**Next earnings catalyst:** Q1 FY2026 (expected May 2026) — will show first full quarter of MEG production + refined 2026 guidance.
