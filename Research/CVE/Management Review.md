## 2. Capital Allocation Track Record

### M&A discipline

**Husky Merger (January 2021):**
- Created the integrated upstream + downstream business
- Brought in CK Hutchison and L.F. Investments as block shareholders
- Provided diversification and refining hedge
- **Outcome:** Generally well-executed integration; significant synergies achieved

**Bri-Chem and other smaller divestitures (2021-2023):**
- Continued portfolio cleanup
- Modest impact

**WRB Refining Divestiture (September 2025):**
- Sold 50% interest in Wood River + Borger refineries to JV partner Phillips 66 for **US$1.4B (C$1.9B)**
- **Strategic rationale:** Simplifies portfolio; lower-margin assets; capital recycled to debt paydown + buybacks
- **Outcome:** Reasonable price; eliminates JV complexity; refining capacity reduced ~140 Mbbls/d

**MEG Energy Acquisition (November 2025):**
- C$8.5B+ deal (C$3.4B cash + C$3.7B stock + assumed debt)
- Adjacent to existing Christina Lake — strong strategic fit
- Synergy target: C$150M (2026/2027), growing to C$400M+ by 2028
- **Outcome (early):** Premium paid (~50% on MEG); ~50/50 cash/stock structure preserves balance sheet flexibility but adds debt + dilution
- **Risk:** First major M&A under McKenzie's CEO tenure — execution still being tested

### Net debt management

[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm]:
- 2023 Net Debt: C$5.06B
- 2024 Net Debt: C$4.61B (dropped to near target)
- **2025 Net Debt: C$8.29B (jumped due to MEG)**
- Target: C$4.0B

Pre-MEG, CVE had nearly hit the C$4B Net Debt target, then took on C$3.7B in incremental debt for MEG. Plan to deleverage back over 2-3 years.

### Capital returns

| Year | Buybacks ($M) | Dividends ($M) | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY2023 | $1,061 | $990 | $2,051 |
| FY2024 | $1,445 | $1,506 (incl. variable) | $2,951 |
| FY2025 | $1,995 | $1,437 | $3,432 |

**$8.4B returned to shareholders over 3 years.** Strong execution.

NCIB renewed Nov 2025 (120.3M shares authorized through Nov 2026). Active deployment at low prices (C$21.87 average in FY2025) — opportunistic.

### Capital returns framework discipline

[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm]:
> "While Net Debt is above $6.0 billion, the Company will target to return approximately 50 percent of Excess Free Funds Flow to shareholders, with the remainder allocated to deleveraging."

**Disciplined framework that automatically prioritizes debt paydown post-MEG.** This shows financial discipline rather than chasing capital returns.

## 8. ESG / Sustainability Communication

[CVE_AIF_FY2025.htm]:
> "The CSR report differs from previous Environmental, Social and Governance reports published by the Company in that it does not include information regarding Cenovus's environmental performance and plans due to changes to Canada's Competition Act in June 2024."

This is a notable disclosure issue — Bill C-59 in Canada created stricter requirements for environmental claims, causing many resource companies to **reduce environmental disclosures**. CVE is no exception. **Less environmental information in current disclosures than in prior years.**

No explicit net-zero target year disclosed in FY2025 filings. Net-zero ambitions tied to Pathways Alliance progress (which is pre-FID).

## 9. Overall Assessment: **Good (with caveats)**

### Strengths

1. **Disciplined capital allocation framework** with explicit net debt targets
2. **Strong M&A track record** — Husky integrated successfully; MEG looks strategically sound
3. **Production execution** — record output in FY2025
4. **Transparent disclosures** within Canadian Competition Act constraints
5. **Stable management team** — McKenzie (3 years CEO) and Sandhar (3 years CFO) settled in
6. **Mid-BBB credit ratings** achieved across all four agencies

### Concerns

1. **Insider ownership <0.2%** — limited personal alignment
2. **Block shareholders at 29% combined** — significant overhang risk
3. **Reduced ESG disclosures** post-Bill C-59 — less transparency on environmental KPIs
4. **MEG integration ahead** — first major M&A under McKenzie; execution unproven
5. **Carbon policy uncertainty** — Pathways Alliance CCUS project pre-FID

### Summary

McKenzie has executed a coherent strategy through a complex period: hit net debt target, divested non-core (WRB), acquired strategic adjacent asset (MEG), maintained capital returns. The **next 2-3 years are about MEG integration and deleveraging** — execution will determine whether the recent multiple expansion is justified.

Management is **competent and disciplined**, but limited insider ownership and concentrated block shareholders create governance friction worth monitoring.
