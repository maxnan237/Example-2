## 5. Net Debt / Capital Allocation Framework

[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm, Returns to Shareholders Target]:

| Net Debt Range | Capital Returns Policy |
|---|---|
| > C$6B (current) | 50% of Excess Free Funds Flow |
| C$4-6B | 75% of Excess Free Funds Flow |
| At C$4B (target) | 100% of Excess Free Funds Flow |

**Current Net Debt / FFF: C$8.3B / C$3.96B = 2.1x.** Need to reduce by C$2.3B to reach C$6B threshold (75% returns); C$4.3B to reach C$4B target (100% returns).

At current oil prices (WTI US$100+), this could happen within 12-18 months. At lower oil prices (WTI US$70), could take 3+ years.

## Accounting Flags

### 1. MEG accounting — only 6 weeks contribution in FY2025
MEG closed Nov 13, 2025 — only ~6 weeks of operating contribution in FY2025 results. FY2026 will show full-year MEG impact (production, revenue, operating costs, debt service).

### 2. WRB divestiture distorts comparisons
WRB (50% Wood River + Borger refineries) sold Sept 30, 2025 for US$1.4B. U.S. Refining throughput dropped ~140 Mbbls/d in Q4 2025 vs. earlier periods. FY2026 numbers will reflect smaller refining footprint.

### 3. Foreign exchange exposure
[CVE_MDA_FY2025.htm]:
> "Cenovus's revenues are predominantly based on U.S. dollar benchmark prices, and a significant portion of our long-term debt and interest expense is denominated in U.S. dollars, while many of our operating and capital costs are in Canadian dollars."

USD/CAD movements create translation noise. FY2025 FX gain of $361M vs. FY2024 loss of $462M = $823M swing — purely currency-driven, not operational.

### 4. Decommissioning provisions
$92M+ in mine closure obligations on balance sheet (component of long-term liabilities). Mostly non-cash today but represents real future cash outflow over decades.

### 5. Capital intensity is structural
Sustaining capex C$3.5-3.6B/year; growth capex C$1.2-1.4B/year. Total capex of ~$5B is structural — limits FCF distribution capacity even at high oil prices.

### 6. Stock-based compensation
Not separately broken out in MD&A summary but typically C$80-120M annually for an integrated of CVE's size. Modest dilution impact.
