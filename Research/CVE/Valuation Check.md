## 5. Sensitivity to Oil Prices

CVE is highly leveraged to crude oil prices. Rough sensitivities (CAD millions, AFF impact per US$10/bbl):

| WTI Move | Impact on AFF |
|---|---|
| +US$10/bbl | +~C$1.5B/year |
| -US$10/bbl | -~C$1.5B/year |

At current WTI US$100+/bbl vs. 5-year average US$75/bbl:
- AFF generation 30-40% above mid-cycle
- Free funds flow ~C$4-5B above mid-cycle

If oil normalized to US$70/bbl mid-cycle:
- AFF would drop from C$8.9B to ~C$4.5B
- Free funds flow would drop from C$3.96B to <C$0
- Dividend ($1.4B) and modest buybacks could continue but no Excess Free Funds Flow available

## 7. Key Valuation Considerations

### Why CVE has run so hard (+131% in 1 year)

1. **Oil price spike** (WTI from ~US$70 to US$105+)
2. **MEG accretion narrative** — synergies + production growth
3. **TMX market access** narrowed WCS differential
4. **WRB divestiture** simplified the story
5. **Active NCIB** providing buyback support
6. **Deleveraging trajectory** to enable higher capital returns

### What's already priced in

At forward P/E ~12x, market is pricing CVE for:
- Sustained WTI above US$80/bbl
- MEG synergies fully realized
- Net debt to C$4B target within 2 years
- 100% of FFF returned to shareholders by 2027-2028

### What's NOT priced in (potential upside)

- Sustained oil prices US$100+/bbl
- Sub-target net debt by mid-2027 (faster deleveraging)
- Pathways Alliance CCUS proceeds at scale
- Additional accretive M&A (e.g., Deep Basin asset sale + redeployment)

### What's NOT priced in (potential downside)

- Recession-driven oil correction to US$60/bbl
- Refining margin collapse
- Major operational incident (pipeline, refinery, oil sands)
- Federal carbon policy tightening
- CK Hutchison stock sale (16.8% block overhang)
